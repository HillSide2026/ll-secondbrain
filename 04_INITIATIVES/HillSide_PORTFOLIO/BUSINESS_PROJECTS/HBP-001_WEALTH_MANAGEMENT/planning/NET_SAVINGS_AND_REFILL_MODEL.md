# Net Savings And Refill Model

Project ID: `HBP-001`
Stage: `Planning`

## Planning Use

Use this file to connect the wealth plan to `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW`. The goal is to
show how quickly liquid capital can be rebuilt after a major deployment.

## Current Position

- current explicit 2026 Levine Law -> Matthew business-income target:
  `CAD 72,000` salary plus `CAD 8,000` bonus = `CAD 80,000`
- current planning assumption: this level of business income approximately
  covers living costs, so net savings from business income are near zero
- current implication: investment income and portfolio compounding may still
  grow the base, but they do not yet create a durable refill assumption

## Conceptual Distinction

- Business income means cash actually paid from `Levine Professional
  Corporation` or `17513721 Canada Inc` to Matthew, including salary, bonus,
  dividends, and approved distributions.
- Investment income means interest, dividends, and realized gains generated by
  Matthew's invested capital.
- The refill model should be anchored first in business income, because that is
  the more controllable owner-cash lane.
- Investment income should be tracked separately so market performance is not
  mistaken for durable post-deployment refill capacity.

## Model Lines

| Line | Definition | Decision Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Gross business-income inflow | compensation, bonus, dividends, and approved distributions from Levine Law or `17513721 Canada Inc` to Matthew | starting point for refill capacity |
| Taxes and mandatory remittances | taxes and unavoidable statutory outflows | reduce true refill capacity |
| Personal living costs | housing, lifestyle, insurance, and recurring personal spend | determine whether income merely sustains life or actually rebuilds capital |
| Business-support outflows | cash support required by businesses or entities | prevent false optimism about what is really available |
| Net savings from business income | business-income inflows less taxes, living costs, and business-support outflows | the core durable refill line for the wealth plan |
| Investment income and realized gains | high-interest savings interest, portfolio dividends, and realized gains on personal capital | tracked separately so return on capital is not mistaken for operating refill |
| Total liquid-base change | change in liquid position after business-income savings plus investment results | explains what actually happened to the base in a period |
| Refill capacity | net savings from business income available to rebuild reserves after a deployment | determines how aggressive a housing budget can safely be |

## Refill Tiers

| Tier | Condition | Wealth-Plan Implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tier 0 | net savings less than or equal to zero | major housing budgets must be treated as a draw on the base with little or no natural refill |
| Tier 1 | net savings are positive but modest | conservative housing budgets may be viable, but reserve rebuild time must be modeled explicitly |
| Tier 2 | net savings are strong and durable | wider housing flexibility may be justified if reserves and targets remain protected |

## Current Planning Rule

Until the rolling net-savings line from business income is visibly positive and
stable in `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW`, no housing budget should assume fast rebuild of
liquid capital.

## Revenue-Growth Linkage

`HBP-008` and `HBP-009` are the projects intended to improve business-income
refill capacity. Their upside does not count in housing planning until it
becomes visible in `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW` reporting rather than existing only as
aspiration.

## Current Working Staircase

For low-return planning, the current working staircase is recorded in
`SG_01_2030_SCENARIO_MODEL.md`:

- `2026`: `CAD 0`
- `2027`: `CAD 7,000`
- `2028`: `CAD 14,000`
- `2029`: `CAD 21,000`
- `2030`: `CAD 28,000`

This is the current reference path for the `4%` case. It shows a controlled,
incremental increase in net savings from business income rather than an abrupt
jump to a fully mature refill level.

The corresponding planning gross-up to pre-tax business income is recorded in
`SG_01_2030_SCENARIO_MODEL.md`. Under the current simplifying tax assumption,
the staircase implies approximate pre-tax business-income targets of `CAD
89,950` in `2027`, `CAD 100,034` in `2028`, `CAD 110,340` in `2029`, and `CAD
121,569` in `2030`, with `CAD 80,000` treated as the lifestyle base.

## Decision Standard

The wealth plan is not ready if it can state a net-worth goal but cannot answer
how a major housing deployment would be refilled afterward.
