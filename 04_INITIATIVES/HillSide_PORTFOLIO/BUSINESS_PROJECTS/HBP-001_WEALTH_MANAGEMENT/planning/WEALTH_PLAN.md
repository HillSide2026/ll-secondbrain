## Core Planning Thesis

1. Preserve the liquid base first.
2. Improve recurring net savings and refill capacity.
3. Deploy capital selectively only when the path to the long-term targets
   improves without damaging liquidity or optionality.

## Wealth Engines

| Engine | Role in the Plan | Current Status | Planning Implication |

| Liquid portfolio compounding | Preserves and grows the base with the clearest measurement feedback | active | Must not be weakened by undisciplined major deployments |
| Operating-business earnings and distributions | Creates refill capacity and can accelerate the path to target | weak today | Depends on `HBP-002`, `HBP-008`, and `HBP-009` producing visible net savings |

## Capital Governance Model

The execution-ready wealth plan requires six linked artifacts:

1. `OPENING_BALANCE_SHEET.md`
2. `VALUATION_AND_CLASSIFICATION_POLICY.md`
3. `LIQUIDITY_AND_CAPITAL_GUARDRAILS.md`
4. `NET_SAVINGS_AND_REFILL_MODEL.md`
5. `HOUSING_BUDGET_POLICY.md`
6. `METRICS.md`

Together these artifacts define what the current base is, what portion is truly
deployable, how quickly capital can be rebuilt, and how major housing decisions
must be tested.

## Major Capital Decision Rules

- Major budgets are set against unrestricted liquid capital, not total
  consolidated net worth.
- Corporate cash sitting inside `17513721 Canada Inc` is not treated as personal
  unrestricted cash unless a deliberate distribution, transfer, or other release
  event is documented.
- Private-business equity and unrealized sale theses may support the long-term
  wealth path, but they do not count as deployable housing capital unless
  monetization timing is documented credibly.
- Housing budgets may not assume future revenue growth unless the resulting net
  savings are already visible in `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW`.
- Toronto housing and Japan home acquisition decisions share one capital pool
  and may not reserve the same dollars twice.
- Until the Toronto primary-housing direction is resolved, any Japan-home
  budget remains provisional.

## Housing Linkage

`TORONTO_HOUSING` and `JAPAN_HOME_DECISION` are downstream decision projects.
Their budgets are valid only when:

- the budget is expressed through `HOUSING_BUDGET_POLICY.md`
- the liquid-capital and reserve rules in
  `LIQUIDITY_AND_CAPITAL_GUARDRAILS.md` are applied
- the refill assumptions in `NET_SAVINGS_AND_REFILL_MODEL.md` are explicit

## Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |

## Planning Exit Standard

Planning is not complete when the wealth objective merely sounds more
sophisticated. Planning is complete only when the baseline, classification
policy, liquidity rules, refill model, and housing-budget policy are specific
enough for ML1 to approve or reject real capital decisions.
