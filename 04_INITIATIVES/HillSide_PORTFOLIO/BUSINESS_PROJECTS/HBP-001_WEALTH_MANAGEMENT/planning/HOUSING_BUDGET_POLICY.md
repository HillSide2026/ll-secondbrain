# Housing Budget Policy

Project ID: `HBP-001`
Stage: `Planning`

`TORONTO_HOUSING` and `JAPAN_HOME_DECISION`.

## Policy Rules

1. Housing budgets are determined from liquid capital, not total long-term net
   worth.
2. Personal unrestricted cash and entity-bound corporate cash are not treated
   as the same pool.
3. Business equity, projected venture upside, and unrealized sale theses do not
   fund housing budgets.
4. The Toronto and Japan projects draw from one common housing capital pool.
5. Until the Toronto primary-housing direction is resolved, any Japan-home
   budget is provisional only.
6. A housing option is not budget-valid unless it states:
   - maximum purchase price
   - maximum upfront cash deployment
   - maximum monthly carrying cost
   - minimum post-close unrestricted liquidity
   - financing assumption
   - transaction buffer
   - explicit no-go triggers
7. If current net savings from business income are near zero, a housing budget
   must prove that the post-close liquidity position remains resilient without
   assuming quick refill.
8. Any option that breaches reserve rules, requires forced sale of illiquid
   assets, or depends on business-income optimism not yet visible in
   `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW` is a no-go.

## Budget Status Levels

| Status | Meaning | Allowed Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Provisional | rough estimates exist but not all fields are locked | comparison only |
| Anchor-ready | the option is fully specified and consistent with wealth-plan rules | supports a planning-exit decision |
| Execution-ready | external facts are confirmed and ML1 approves advancement | can support a real execution recommendation |

## Required Output By Housing Project

| Project | Required Budget Output | Special Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `TORONTO_HOUSING` | one budget card for owned Toronto housing and one budget card for leaving Toronto | the preferred path must prove primary-housing stability, not just theoretical affordability |
| `JAPAN_HOME_DECISION` | one budget card for pursue and one discipline note for defer / no | any pursue case must show it does not crowd out primary-housing security or reserve integrity |

## Shared No-Go Triggers

- post-close unrestricted liquidity below the reserve floor
- negative or fragile refill capacity
- unresolved tax, legal, or financing issues large enough to distort the real cost
- simultaneous reservation of the same capital for both Toronto and Japan paths

## Decision Standard

The housing projects do not have valid budgets merely because they can name a
purchase price. They have valid budgets only when the option fits the wealth
plan's liquidity, refill, and no-go rules.
