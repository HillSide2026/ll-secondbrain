# Valuation And Classification Policy

Project ID: `HBP-001`
Stage: `Planning`

## Planning Use

Use this file to classify assets and liabilities consistently and to decide what
actually counts as deployable capital.

## Classification Codes

| Code | Meaning | Budget Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `U1` | unrestricted liquid capital | fully eligible for budget calculations, subject to reserve rules |
| `U2` | market-valued near-liquid holdings | eligible for budget calculations only after valuation date and exit assumptions are made explicit |
| `R1` | restricted, encumbered, or entity-bound assets | excluded from deployable-capital calculations unless the restriction is released or the entity boundary is deliberately crossed |
| `I1` | illiquid strategic equity | counted for long-term wealth framing, excluded from housing deployment |
| `C1` | conditional, contingent, or thesis-linked value | carried at zero for deployment until legally committed or realized |
| `L1` | liabilities, taxes, and obligations | deducted fully from deployable-capital calculations |

## Valuation Rules

- Cash and cash equivalents are carried at statement value.
- Public securities and market-priced funds are carried at the latest available
  statement value, with valuation date noted explicitly.
- Private-business equity must be valued conservatively and documented with the
  logic used. If no credible decision-use estimate exists, the value can remain
  in long-term wealth framing but is treated as non-deployable.
- Sale theses, expected exits, contingent recoveries, and other unrealized cases
  are carried at zero for deployment unless there is a signed instrument or
  realized cash receipt.
- Liabilities are carried at the best current estimate of principal, accrued
  obligations, and taxes.

## Deployment Rule

Only `U1` and the usable portion of `U2`, less `L1` items and required reserves,
count toward housing budgets or other major capital commitments.

## Revaluation Rule

- update liquid values at least quarterly
- re-check values before any material housing or acquisition decision
- update business-equity judgment when a material operating or sale event occurs

## Conservatism Rule

When there is a choice between a flattering estimate and a decision-useful one,
the policy favors the decision-useful estimate. The wealth plan should not
create fake capacity by overstating what can actually be spent.
