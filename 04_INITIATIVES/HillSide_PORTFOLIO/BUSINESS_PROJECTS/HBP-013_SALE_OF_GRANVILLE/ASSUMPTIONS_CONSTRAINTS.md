# Assumptions and Constraints

**Project:** Sale of Granville
**Project ID:** HBP-013
**Stage:** Planning

## Assumptions

- FINTRAC registration remains the core credibility threshold for the eventual entity sale
- RPAA status and supporting-stack clarity improve the licensed-entity story even if they are not the sole hard blocker
- the eventual buyer will be buying the licensed entity, not BaaS services or a standalone software product
- a light supporting stack can be more valuable than a heavy stack if it is cheaper, transferable, and diligence-useful
- EMI written indication may be sufficient for planning and readiness purposes before any full operating launch

## Constraints

- no active sale launch is authorized during this planning phase
- no meaningful operating volume should be run through `174` without separate ML1 approval
- vendor work must remain subordinate to the licensed-entity sale thesis
- no project artifact may imply that software itself is the product being sold
- planning should prefer transferability, reversibility, and cost discipline over platform breadth

## Locked Planning Direction

Currently locked:

- the project is about licensing and preparing the entity for eventual sale
- the project is not about building and marketing BaaS services
- FINTRAC, AML, RPAA, EMI, and minimum supporting-stack clarity are the core readiness lanes

Still not locked:

- the exact minimum acceptable support stack
- whether Rhizome founder arrangement or another path is the best supporting software posture
- whether Kwiikpay becomes relevant to 174 or remains outside the entity thesis
- the exact future sale-launch timing after readiness is assembled

## Planning Boundary

This planning stage is limited to scope control, readiness definition, packet
assembly, and later-stage sale-authorization criteria.
