# Success Criteria

Project: `HBP-013` - Sale of Granville

## Primary Success Threshold

**`17409052 Canada Inc` becomes a licensed, market-ready entity with a coherent
diligence package, so it can be sold later as the licensed entity on ML1
direction.**

## Success Criteria Table

| Criterion | Target | Window | Signal |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Licensing gate complete | FINTRAC registration issued and AML package ready for review | Core planning window | Entity is licensed, not just incorporated |
| Preferred readiness complete | RPAA status clarified, EMI written indication obtained, software / Kwiikpay posture documented | Before sale launch decision | Story is differentiated, not just licensed |
| Market-ready package ready | Diligence index, management narrative, and supporting readiness materials assembled | Before any sale launch | Buyer can understand the licensed entity |
| Scope discipline maintained | Software and connector work remains support for the entity, not a separate BaaS product thesis | Throughout planning | Project does not drift |
| Sale option preserved | Licensed entity can be marketed later without major rework | After readiness package is assembled | ML1 has a real future disposition option |

## Minimum Viable Outcome

Minimum success is FINTRAC issuance plus a coherent market-ready package for
the licensed entity.

## Failure Conditions

- FINTRAC registration remains unresolved long enough that the licensed-entity
  thesis cannot be stabilized
- EMI, safeguarding, or diligence gaps make the licensed-entity story too thin
- the project drifts into overbuilding an operating or BaaS business
- software spend outpaces what is needed to support the eventual entity sale

## Out of Scope for This Phase

- running substantial funds-flow volume through `174`
- migrating Levine Law or FinSure activity into `174`
- building a heavyweight permanent BaaS or banking platform
