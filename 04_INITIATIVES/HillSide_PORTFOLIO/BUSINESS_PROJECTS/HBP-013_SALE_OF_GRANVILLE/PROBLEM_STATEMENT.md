# Problem Statement

Project: `HBP-013` - Sale of Granville

## Problem

`17409052 Canada Inc` may have future sale value once licensed, but there is
not yet a controlled plan for getting the entity licensed and market-ready for
an eventual sale.

Current gaps:

- no dedicated HillSide project packet existed for `174`
- licensing, readiness, and vendor materials were spread across the entity
  branch rather than organized into one controlled project
- FINTRAC, AML, RPAA, EMI, and software items are not yet assembled into one
  market-ready licensed-entity narrative
- vendor conversations risk drifting into "selling BaaS services" instead of
  supporting the eventual sale of the licensed entity

## Proposed Solution

Stand up a controlled HillSide project that converts the existing 174
value-stack work into a gated licensing and market-readiness plan, with sale
execution deferred until ML1 decides the licensed entity is ready to market.

## Impact of Not Solving

- `174` may remain a stranded regulatory asset with unclear licensing and
  disposition timing
- eventual sale value may be impaired because the licensed-entity story stays
  fragmented
- ML1 may spend time advancing counterparties or software that does not
  actually support licensing or eventual entity sale
- capital allocation decisions may be distorted by overbuilding the support
  stack
