# Business Case — HBP-013 — Sale of Granville

## Strategic Rationale

`17409052 Canada Inc` is a non-core asset that may command more value if it is
first made into a licensed, market-ready entity and only then sold, rather
than remaining a static shell inside `17513721 Canada Inc`.

The strategic case is:

1. **Recycle capital into the parent.** A successful sale moves value out of a
   licensed asset and into `17513721 Canada Inc`.
2. **Monetize licensing and readiness, not BaaS services.** The objective is
   to sell the licensed entity, supported by FINTRAC path, AML story, RPAA
   path, EMI thesis, and only the minimum supporting software posture.
3. **Avoid stranded build time.** Without a controlled scope, ML1 risks
   spending time on software or connectors that turn into a pseudo-product
   thesis instead of supporting the eventual sale of the licensed entity.

## Value-Creation Logic

| Value Driver | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| FINTRAC registration | Establishes the minimum hard-credibility layer for the licensed entity |
| AML package | Lets a buyer assess whether compliance work is real, not theoretical |
| RPAA status clarity | Improves the payments-regulatory story and diligence defensibility |
| EMI written indication | Shows a plausible operating and safeguarding path |
| Reversible software posture | Signals discipline and transferability instead of drifting into a BaaS build |
| Buyer-readable diligence folder | Makes the asset understandable and speeds buyer review |

## Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Why Not Chosen |
| --- | --- |
| Hold `174` dormant pending only FINTRAC | Preserves optionality but creates no defined monetization path |
| Sell immediately "as is" | Likely leaves value on the table because the licensed-entity story is too thin |
| Build and market BaaS services | Changes the project into a different business and adds cost, complexity, and execution risk |
| Build a heavier operating platform before sale | Raises cost, timeline, and lock-in risk before entity-sale readiness is proven |
| Combine `174` sale with Ontario MSB Corp by default | Adds complexity and blurs what the buyer is buying |

## Pricing / Outcome Framing

No fixed valuation target is stated at this stage. Eventual price will depend on:

- timing of FINTRAC issuance
- clarity of the RPAA and EMI story
- quality and transferability of the diligence package
- whether the buyer is acquiring a licensed shell, a readiness package, or a
  lightly supported operating posture

## Recommendation

Proceed with the controlled licensing-and-sale-readiness path. The
risk-adjusted case is stronger than either a dormant hold or a premature BaaS
build: limited additional build, clearer licensed-entity narrative, better
buyer defensibility, and cleaner capital recycling into `17513721 Canada Inc`.
