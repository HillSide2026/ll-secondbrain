canonical Levine Law strategic horizon stops at `2028`.

single-path annual plan until ML1 resolves the `2028` closeout and confirms
whether Levine Law is operating on:

- **Path A — Controlled Boutique**
- **Path B — Early Platform**

## Common Non-Negotiables For 2029

Regardless of which path is chosen, the following must remain true:

- Levine Law stays inside a narrow, specialist, high-fit market position
- F02 and F03 remain distinct and governed
- F02 continues to serve as the core operator channel
- F03 continues to serve as the specialist authority channel
- delivery systems, supervision logic, and scope caps remain documented
- ML1 does not authorize complexity that destroys economics or sustainability
- the `2029` plan must be built around real ML1 bandwidth and supervision
  limits rather than abstract scaling assumptions

## Working LL Distribution To ML1 For 2029

The LL planning packet should also carry both a fixed `2029` owner line and a
total `2029` owner-revenue line. `2029` sits after the `2028` platform
decision, so the payout plan should flow from LL operating logic, not from a
separate wealth-model gross-up.

Inherited LL payout sequence:

- `2026`: `CAD 80,000` fixed and total revenue to ML
- `2027`: `CAD 85,000` fixed revenue to ML, with `CAD 90,000-95,000` total
  revenue to ML after any approved profit distribution
- `2028`: `CAD 90,000` fixed revenue to ML, with `CAD 100,000` total revenue
  to ML after profit distribution

The `2029` payout logic should be:

- step up again from the `2028` fixed and total lines
- require any distribution above the fixed line to come from real profit after
  retained earnings
- refuse any platform structure that adds complexity without improving owner
  economics

| Path | Fixed revenue to ML1 | Additional distribution after retained earnings | Total revenue to ML1 | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `Path A — Controlled Boutique` | `CAD 95,000` | about `CAD 15,000` | `CAD 110,000` | Clean step-up from `2028` without forcing platform complexity |
| `Path B — Early Platform` | `CAD 95,000` | at least about `CAD 15,000` | at least `CAD 110,000` | Not approvable unless the added layer beats Path A on owner economics and retained profit |

These are Levine Law planning lines for revenue or payout to ML1, whether later

## The 2029 Planning Question

The key question for `2029` is no longer whether Levine Law can launch the
channels. By this point the question is:

> What firm form should exist after the `2028` platform decision?

The practical follow-on question is:

> How much profit is actually achievable given Levine Law's real constraints?

The `2029` answer must be judged against the following constraints:

- non-elite market position
- limited cognitive bandwidth, including a working ceiling of roughly `20`
  billable hours per week for ML1
- limited charisma and extroversion, which likely makes it difficult for ML1
  to supervise more than one lawyer effectively

This means the `2029` choice is not simply "boutique versus platform." It is a
profit-under-constraints decision. If a more complex structure produces more
revenue but lower actual owner profit, lower control, or a supervision burden
that ML1 is unlikely to carry well, that structure should be treated as
strategically inferior.

The answer depends on the selected path.

## Path A - Controlled Boutique Annual Plan

### Strategic Posture

- preserve a tightly controlled, high-realization specialty boutique
- keep leverage narrow or minimal
- optimize for income stability, niche authority, and operating simplicity
- treat bounded human limits as a strategic advantage rather than a defect to
  be "solved" through premature scale

### 2029 Objectives

- keep F02 efficient, high-fit, and disciplined
- keep F03 authority-led and specialist-focused
- maintain documented delivery systems without scaling headcount beyond what is
  needed for bounded relief
- deepen the firm's reputation and client quality rather than broadening the
  platform
- preserve optionality for future equity value without forcing an early
  platform model

### Path A Success Condition

At the end of `2029`, Levine Law must be a stronger, more profitable, more
durable firm.

Under Path A, that means profit, control, and sustainability all improve
without taking on staffing or operational complexity that outruns the firm's
actual control systems.

## Path B - Early Platform Annual Plan

### Strategic Posture

- convert the specialty boutique into an early platform with real delegation,
  client independence from ML1, and higher asset value
- expand only where the service lines are repeatable and supervision remains
  economically sound
- assume, unless live evidence proves otherwise, that ML1 is unlikely to
  supervise more than one lawyer effectively
- treat `2029` as the earliest plausible breakout year for a single
  practice-group-leader-style lawyer who can run files, hold client
  relationships, and generate profit for ML1

### 2029 Objectives

- operationalize a second-fee-earner or equivalent delivery layer only where
  LLP-038 and LLP-033 evidence support it
- reduce ML1-person dependence in client handling, delivery workflow, and
  repeat service execution
- make the firm visibly partner-ready or investment-ready inside its Tier 5
  niche
- keep authority depth in payments / MSB / RPAA / AML work while increasing
  delivery capacity
- prove that added structure increases real owner profit after supervision
  burden, complexity cost, and market-position limits are accounted for
- confirm that any practice-group-leader-style role improves actual owner
  economics rather than simply adding senior payroll and governance complexity

### Financial Planning Input For Path B

The repo's current long-range financial input is the `2030` second-fee-earner
sensitivity note. It is not a `2029` target, but it gives a planning band for
what a leveraged structure might need to support later owner-income goals:

- at `30%` contribution margin: about `CAD 379,000` total firm revenue
- at `35%` contribution margin: about `CAD 359,000` total firm revenue
- at `40%` contribution margin: about `CAD 344,000` total firm revenue

Use this only as a planning reference. It is not an approved annual revenue
commitment for `2029`.

For HBP-008 planning, any Path B structure must still support at least the
`CAD 95,000` fixed line and about `CAD 110,000` of total revenue to ML1 above,
and should be judged against whether it outperforms the controlled-boutique
case after supervision and complexity cost.

### Path B Success Condition

At the end of `2029`, Levine Law functions as a genuine early platform:
repeatable service lines, evidence-based leverage, stronger firm-owned client
relationships, and real enterprise value beyond ML1's direct personal output.

Path B should be treated as successful only if it outperforms the
controlled-boutique path on actual profit, sustainability, and control under
ML1's real supervision limits.

## Gating Decision Before 2029 Is Finalized

Before ML1 can approve a single-path `2029` annual plan, the repo should have:

- a closed `2028` review against `ANNUAL_PLAN_2028.md`
- an explicit Path A vs. Path B decision record
- an updated staffing and economics read from LLP-033 and LLP-038
- a `2029` budget or operating target aligned to the chosen path
- an explicit judgment about what profit is achievable given ML1's bandwidth,
  supervision ceiling, and actual market position
- evidence that one senior self-managing lawyer is genuinely supportable before
  assuming anything beyond that

Until those exist, this document should be read as a scenario frame rather
than a finalized annual plan.
