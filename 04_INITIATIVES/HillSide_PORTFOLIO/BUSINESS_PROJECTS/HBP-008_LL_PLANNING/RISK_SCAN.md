# Risk Scan

**Project:** LL Planning
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## Primary Risks

### R-01 — Associate sourcing fails or takes too long

**Description:** The required profile — 4–5 years call, corporate/commercial and financial
services experience, willing to work part-time on a contractor split — is rare and does
not respond to job postings. Sourcing through referral takes time. If sourcing starts
late or the network does not produce a match, the associate layer does not activate and
2027 revenue stays at the ML1 solo ceiling.

**Likelihood:** Medium. The profile exists in the Ontario market but the combination
of experience requirements and part-time contractor structure narrows the field
significantly.

**Impact:** High. Without associate activation, total firm revenue cannot reach
CAD 400,000 and ML1 is forced to either bill past the $25k cap or accept lower
collections.

**Mitigation:** Begin sourcing in H2 2026, before the LLP-033 activation gate is
formally met. Target former colleagues, alumni networks, and professional referrals.
Do not wait for a job posting to produce a candidate.

---

### R-02 — Associate engagement but low billings

**Description:** The associate is engaged but does not produce CAD 160,000 in billings.
Year-one contractor arrangements frequently underperform billing targets because the
associate is building familiarity with templates and workflows, juggling other work, or
receiving fewer delegated files than projected.

**Likelihood:** High for the full CAD 160,000 target; medium for the CAD 100,000 floor.

**Impact:** Medium. At CAD 100,000 associate billings, total firm revenue is CAD 340,000
— below target but the model is still validated. Below CAD 80,000, the model is not
proven and 2027 reads as a partial success.

**Mitigation:** Set CAD 100,000 as the floor target and CAD 160,000 as upside. Ensure
a sufficient backlog of delegable files is ready at engagement. Review monthly.

---

### R-03 — Associate retention risk

**Description:** A part-time contractor with other income sources has low switching
costs. If Levine Law file flow becomes intermittent, the files are poor quality, or a
better opportunity emerges, the associate disengages mid-year.

**Likelihood:** Medium. Part-time contractors are inherently transactional unless the
relationship is actively maintained.

**Impact:** High. Mid-year departure disrupts active files, requires re-sourcing,
and damages the 2027 revenue model.

**Mitigation:** Maintain steady, predictable file flow of 8–10 delegable hours per
week. Provide genuinely substantive files. Preserve associate autonomy within
well-defined guardrails. Communicate a visible growth pathway if the model scales.

---

### R-04 — Supervision burden exceeds model

**Description:** The associate model assumes limited ML1 supervision per file. If the
associate requires more direction than expected — due to unfamiliarity with templates,
file complexity, or quality issues — ML1 supervision time rises and the net contribution
margin on associate billings deteriorates.

**Likelihood:** Medium for the first 2–3 months; lower after ramp-up.

**Impact:** Medium. Excessive supervision does not destroy the model but it reduces
ML1 capacity and compressed contribution margin.

**Mitigation:** Define matter eligibility tightly. Invest in onboarding — templates,
scope definitions, supervision cadence — before the first files are delegated.
Treat the first 60 days as a paid ramp, not full-speed delivery.

---

### R-05 — F02 and F03 channels underperform in 2027

**Description:** HBP-008 assumes F02 is the primary acquisition channel and F03
produces named specialist revenue. If F02 conversion from Health Check to fractional
counsel is slower than projected, or F03 remains a content experiment with no paying
specialist work, total collections fall short even with the associate active.

**Likelihood:** Medium. Channel performance depends on 2026 validation work that has
not yet occurred.

**Impact:** High. Channel underperformance affects both ML1 billings and the associate
delegable file volume.

**Mitigation:** Treat 2026 F02 and F03 traction data as the primary input into 2027
revenue planning. Do not lock 2027 channel targets until Q4 2026 actuals are available.

---

### R-06 — HBP-007 does not achieve 2026 target

**Description:** If Levine Law does not reach CAD 240,000 in 2026 collections, the
LLP-033 activation gate may not be met, and HBP-008 planning assumptions are invalid.

**Likelihood:** Low to medium.

**Impact:** High. HBP-008 becomes premature and should be deferred pending revised
2026 actuals.

**Mitigation:** Monitor HBP-007 monthly. Revise HBP-008 targets in Q4 2026 based on
actual 2026 performance before planning is authorized.
