# Retained Profit Model 2026-2029

**Project:** LL Planning
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## Purpose

Restate the `2026-2029` Levine Law model on one simple basis:

- fees
- fixed owner distribution
- extra owner distribution
- retained profit at `30%`, `35%`, and `40%`

This note uses the following rule:

```text
retained profit
= (fees x retained-profit margin) - extra owner distribution
```

The fixed owner distribution is treated as already embedded inside the firm's
cost base for the year. In other words, if Levine Law collects `CAD 240,000`
and can still retain `30%` after paying the lawyer the fixed `CAD 80,000`
owner line, retained profit is `CAD 72,000`.

## Interpretation

- `2026` is already a meaningful retained-profit year if the firm can truly
  hold `30-40%` retained profit after the fixed `CAD 80,000` owner line.
- `2027` becomes economically attractive quickly if the firm reaches the
  `CAD 340,000-400,000` fee band and keeps the extra owner distribution modest.
- `2028` and `2029` still do not have approved fee targets in HBP-008. The
  figures above are therefore owner-distribution and retained-profit planning
  references, not locked operating budgets.
- The main planning discipline is: raise the fixed owner line gradually, keep
  extra distributions bounded, and preserve enough retained profit to fund
  durability and leverage.

## Backlog

The fully tax-adjusted corporate model remains backlog work for HBP-008.

That future model should explicitly address:

- corporate tax on retained earnings inside Levine Law
- tax treatment differences between salary, bonus, dividend, and other owner
  payment forms
- whether retained profit is actually left inside Levine Law or distributed
- how much of the nominal retained-profit line is truly deployable after tax

Until that work is completed, this note should be read as a pre-tax corporate
planning shorthand rather than a finalized after-tax corporate model.
