# Problem Statement

**Project:** LL Planning
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## The Problem

ML1's personal billing capacity has a hard ceiling. At $480/hour with disciplined intake,
the practical monthly cap is approximately CAD 25,000. That puts ML1-solo annual
collections at approximately CAD 300,000 under optimal conditions, and the 2026 program
is targeting CAD 240,000 as the validated baseline.

The HBP-001 wealth plan requires owner compensation to grow from CAD 80,000 to
approximately CAD 122,000 by 2030. The second fee earner sensitivity model shows this
requires total firm revenue in the CAD 344,000–379,000 range. ML1 billing alone cannot
close that gap.

2027 is the first year the gap can be addressed through leverage. But leverage introduces
a new problem: finding a part-time contractor with the right profile, keeping them engaged,
and proving that the Levine Law delivery model transfers. None of those problems solve
themselves without a governed program.

## Why 2027 Is Distinct From 2026

2026 was a production validation year. The question was: can the firm generate CAD 240,000
in collections as a solo platform? 2027 is a leverage validation year. The question is:
can a part-time contractor associate add CAD 160,000 in billings without degrading
quality, absorbing too much ML1 supervision time, or departing before the model is proven?

These are operationally distinct problems requiring distinct planning.

## The Associate Profile Problem

The right associate for Levine Law is a specific profile: 4–5 years call, capable of
running files from end to end with limited supervision, and experienced in both
corporate/commercial and financial services law. The financial services requirement
is critical — it means the associate can handle F03 payments/MSB work, not just F01
and F02 corporate files.

This profile does not respond to a job posting. It is sourced through referral networks
and professional relationships. Sourcing must begin in H2 2026, before the activation
gate is formally triggered.

The part-time contractor structure ($64,000 in compensation on $160,000 in billings at
a 40% split) works only for someone who has other income or is deliberately working
part-time. That person has low switching costs and no obligation of exclusivity. Keeping
them engaged requires steady file flow, good files, autonomy, and a visible growth pathway.

## What Happens If This Is Not Governed

- the associate activation decision is made informally, with no explicit economics
  or profile requirements
- ML1 bills past the $25k ceiling because there is no structured cap and delegation model
- sourcing starts too late and the right person is not available in H1 2027
- the associate takes the arrangement but underdelivers because the delegation model,
  matter eligibility, and supervision structure were never defined
- 2027 ends without proving the leverage model, and the 2030 income target becomes
  dependent on ML1 billing alone
