# Business Case

## Why This Project Exists

HBP-007 established whether Levine Law can generate CAD 240,000 in annual collections
as a solo ML1 platform. HBP-008 asks the next question: can the firm add a part-time
delivery associate and grow total collections to CAD 400,000 without ML1 billing more
than $25,000 per month?

This project exists because the 2027 operating year has a fundamentally different
design than 2026. The new element — the associate layer — changes the economics,
the talent model, the supervision obligation, and the revenue structure. Without a
governed HillSide project, those decisions are made informally and the risks are not
tracked.

## Strategic Need

The HBP-001 wealth plan requires owner compensation to grow from CAD 80,000 (2026) to
approximately CAD 122,000 by 2030. The second fee earner sensitivity model confirms
this requires total firm revenue in the CAD 344,000–379,000 range, depending on
contribution margin. CAD 400,000 in 2027, if achieved, puts the 2030 target in reach
two to three years early.

ML1's personal billing ceiling ($25,000/month) means all growth above the 2026 baseline
must come from associate leverage. 2027 is the first year that leverage is tested.

The choice of associate profile — corporate/commercial with financial services experience
— is also strategic. It means the associate can handle F01 and F02 delegated files AND
F03 payments/MSB specialist work, covering the full Levine Law matter mix rather than
only the corporate lane.

## Expected Value

- total firm collections of CAD 400,000 versus the 2026 CAD 240,000 baseline
- fixed revenue to ML1 step from CAD 80,000 to CAD 85,000
- total revenue to ML1 of CAD 90,000–95,000 after any profit distribution made
  after retained earnings
- first proof point that the Levine Law leverage model works
- pre-tax firm retained earnings of approximately CAD 155,000-160,000 in the
  2027 target case, before the HBP-008 corporate-tax adjustment
- F03 payments practice producing named specialist revenue rather than remaining
  a content experiment
- associate model proven as a repeatable delivery layer

## Cost of Not Formalizing

- associate activation decisions are made without explicit economics, profile
  requirements, or activation gates
- the associate relationship is managed informally with no retention or performance
  framework
- ML1 risks billing above the $25k ceiling without a structured cap and delegation model
- the 2027 year operates without an explicit fixed owner line or distribution
  rule, leaving the owner-revenue path unmonitored

## Core 2027 Business Thesis

2026 validated the solo platform. 2027 validates the leverage model. The business case
is: activate a part-time contractor associate with the right profile, delegate a
bounded and growing set of Levine Law matters, and let ML1 hold the $25k/month
ceiling while associate billings drive total firm revenue to CAD 400,000.

If the leverage model proves out in 2027, 2028 can deepen it. If it does not,
the firm remains a disciplined solo platform at the $300,000 ceiling.
