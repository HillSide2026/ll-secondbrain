# LL Time Investment Benchmarks

**Project:** LL Planning
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## Decision Question

How much ML1 time should continue to be invested in Levine Law as a business,
distinct from time required to serve existing paying clients?

This is an owner-level allocation question. It governs whether LL deserves
continued founder attention for strategy, marketing, systems, service design,
associate leverage, and specialty-platform development.

It does not govern whether existing client obligations are performed. Client
delivery remains mandatory once accepted.

## Core Thesis

LL justifies continued investment of ML1 time only if it is proving all four of
the following:

1. cash reliability
2. controlled capacity
3. improving client quality and per-hour economics
4. evidence that the business is becoming less dependent on pure ML1 effort

Revenue alone is not enough. A year of high collections that consumes all ML1
capacity, worsens health risk, or produces no repeatable channel or delivery
asset does not justify expanding time investment.

## Investment Modes

| Mode | Trigger | ML1 non-client LL time budget | Authorized activity | Prohibited activity |
|---|---|---:|---|---|
| `Pause / Reduce` | Cash floor missed, capacity breach persistent, or no credible recovery path | 0-2 hrs/week | Existing client delivery, collections, risk containment | New offers, new funnels, new systems, speculative strategy |
| `Defend` | LL is needed for current income, but expansion evidence is weak | 2-4 hrs/week | Collections, delivery cleanup, minimum marketing maintenance, close warm high-fit leads | New platforms, broad content programs, low-confidence product build |
| `Hold / Harvest` | LL produces acceptable cash but does not improve scalability or client quality | 3-5 hrs/week | Keep profitable work, renew good clients, reduce low-fit load | New capacity-consuming service lines |
| `Invest` | Floor benchmarks are met and at least one channel/leverage proof is visible | 5-8 hrs/week | F02/F03 build, scoped systems, associate preparation, retainer packaging | Expansion that increases delivery load before controls exist |
| `Expand` | Target benchmarks are met and leverage/channel evidence is strong | 8-12 hrs/week | Deeper systems, selective marketing, delegation, premium-service preparation | Growth that breaches capacity or weakens client quality |

The default mode is `Defend`. LL must earn `Invest` or `Expand`; it does not
receive those modes by default.

Client delivery time is outside this table. These budgets apply only to
non-client founder time spent building LL as a business.

## Hard Decision Rules

Use these rules before applying judgment.

| Rule | If true | Required decision |
|---|---|---|
| Cash survival failure | Projected next-90-day LL collections cannot cover required owner/business cash need | `Pause / Reduce` or `Defend`; no speculative build |
| Capacity failure | ML1 exceeds 20 billable hrs/week for 4 of 6 consecutive weeks in 2026, or exceeds the 2027 monthly cap twice in a quarter | Reduce intake, narrow scope, or add delivery support before any expansion |
| Client-quality failure | More than 25% of active matters are low-fit, high-friction, under-floor, or outside ICP | Stop adding similar work; no new channel spend aimed at that profile |
| Channel failure | F02 and F03 produce no paid demand for two consecutive quarters after launch | No further major funnel investment until offer, ICP, or distribution is reset |
| Retainer-control failure | Any fintech retainer becomes open-ended legal department access or pushes total concurrent retainers above 3 | Stop selling retainers and reset scope/capacity before continuing |
| Leverage failure | Associate/supervised delivery creates more ML1 supervision burden than delivery relief for two consecutive quarters | No platform expansion; revert to controlled boutique assumptions |
| Economic failure | Effective realized rate or per-matter economics fall while workload rises | Increase price, narrow scope, or reduce low-fit work before investing more time |

## Quantitative Quarterly Scoring Gate

At each quarter end, score LL across five dimensions. Each dimension is scored
0, 1, or 2.

| Dimension | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cash | Quarterly collections below 75% of prorated annual floor, or >30 days cash gap expected | Quarterly collections 75%-99% of prorated annual target, with no near-term cash gap | Quarterly collections >=100% of prorated annual target |
| Capacity | ML1 exceeds weekly/monthly cap in >33% of measured periods | ML1 exceeds cap in 1%-33% of measured periods | ML1 exceeds cap in 0 measured periods |
| Client quality | <60% of active matters are ICP-fit and above floor | 60%-79% of active matters are ICP-fit and above floor | >=80% of active matters are ICP-fit and above floor |
| Channel proof | CAD 0 paid F02/F03 demand in quarter | CAD 1-CAD 14,999 paid F02/F03 demand, or CAD 25,000+ qualified pipeline | CAD 15,000+ paid F02/F03 demand, or 2+ active recurring F03 relationships |
| Leverage / independence | 0% of non-client build/delivery process reusable or delegated | 10%-24% of active delivery/build load handled through templates, systems, or approved delegation | >=25% of active delivery/build load handled through templates, systems, or approved delegation |

| Score | Mode |
|---:|---|
| 0-3 | `Pause / Reduce` |
| 4-5 | `Defend` |
| 6-7 | `Hold / Harvest` |
| 8-9 | `Invest` |
| 10 | `Expand` |

A hard decision rule overrides the score. For example, a score of 8 does not
authorize `Invest` if there is a current retainer-control or capacity failure.

## 2026 Benchmarks

2026 is a solo-platform validation year. The question is whether LL can sustain
the baseline without consuming all owner capacity.

| Benchmark | Floor | Target | Meaning |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Banked collections | CAD 200,000 | CAD 240,000 | LL remains a viable income engine |
| Owner revenue from LL | CAD 70,000 | CAD 80,000 | LL supports minimum owner economics |
| ML1 capacity | <=20 billable hrs/week in at least 10 of 13 weeks per quarter | <=20 billable hrs/week in 13 of 13 weeks per quarter | The business is not buying revenue with overload |
| F02 evidence | CAD 5,000+ paid F02 revenue or 3+ paid Health Checks by year-end | CAD 15,000+ paid F02 revenue or 6+ paid Health Checks by year-end | Core channel is moving beyond theory |
| F03 evidence | CAD 12,000+ paid F03 revenue or CAD 25,000+ qualified F03 pipeline by year-end | 2 active recurring F03 relationships or CAD 30,000+ paid/contracted F03 revenue | Specialist platform has real demand |
| Retainer capacity discipline | 0 retainer-control breaches and <=3 concurrent fintech retainers | 2-3 active or signed well-fit recurring fintech mandates | High-cash-flow work is controlled, not open-ended |
| Scope discipline | Basic bounded advisory only through `2026-12-31` | Same | Premium complexity is not absorbed before the delivery model can carry it |

### 2026 Gate

By `2026-12-31`, the decision is:

| Result | Conditions | 2027 posture |
|---|---|---|
| Green | CAD 240,000+ collections; owner revenue CAD 80,000+; quarterly score 8+ in Q4; no hard-rule override | `Invest` in 2027 plan and associate preparation |
| Yellow | CAD 200,000-239,999 collections or owner revenue CAD 70,000-79,999; Q4 score 6-7; no hard-rule override | `Defend` or `Hold / Harvest`; narrow the 2027 plan before authorizing expansion |
| Red | Below CAD 200,000 collections; Q4 score <=5; persistent overload; no paid F02/F03 evidence; or fintech retainer scope/capacity breach | `Pause / Reduce` or strict `Defend`; no 2027 expansion without revised model |

The minimum 2026 green-light evidence is not perfection. It is: LL can produce
baseline cash, avoid overload, and show at least one credible path beyond
reactive F01 work.

### 2027 Gate

By `2027-12-31`, the decision is:

| Result | Conditions | 2028 posture |
|---|---|---|
| Green - Controlled Boutique | CAD 340,000+ collections; owner revenue CAD 90,000+; Q4 score 8+; ML1 cap respected; associate optional or limited | Continue LL as a strong controlled boutique; do not force platform expansion |
| Green - Early Platform | CAD 400,000+ collections; associate CAD 100,000+ collections; F02/F03 paid demand CAD 120,000+ combined; Q4 score 9+; ML1 cap respected | Authorize selective `Expand` mode for 2028 |

The 2027 gate has two different green outcomes. A controlled boutique can be a
valid success. Platform expansion is authorized only if leverage is proven, not
merely because revenue increased.

## 2028 Benchmarks

2028 is the path-choice year. The question is whether LL should remain a
controlled boutique or receive deeper platform investment.

| Benchmark | Continue boutique | Expand platform |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue quality | Stable revenue with high realization and low friction | Stable revenue plus repeatable channels |
| Owner economics | Owner revenue at or above the 2028 plan | Owner revenue at or above plan with reduced ML1 proportional effort |
| ML1 capacity | ML1 workload sustainable | ML1 workload sustainable with delegation absorbing growth |
| Associate leverage | Optional or limited | Proven and economically accretive |
| F03 maturity | Specialist work profitable and controlled | Specialist retainers and adjacent premium work selectively scalable |
| Systems | Enough to preserve quality | Strong enough for non-ML1 delivery and supervision |
| Strategic choice | Path A acceptable | Path B evidence strong enough to pursue |

2028 expansion requires evidence that LL can grow without converting ML1 into
the hidden subsidy. If that evidence is absent, the correct answer is not
failure; it is controlled boutique consolidation.

## 2029 Benchmarks

2029 is the durability test. The question is whether LL is an enduring income
asset, an early platform, or a business that should be harvested more cautiously.

| Benchmark | Required signal |
|---|---|
| Owner economics | Owner revenue continues to rise without proportional ML1 effort |
| Enterprise value | Client relationships, systems, and delivery capacity are not wholly ML1-personal |
| Capacity | Growth does not require recurring capacity breach |
| Specialist position | F03 authority translates into profitable retained or premium work |
| Strategic optionality | LL can support continued operation, staged equity sale, or controlled harvest |

## Stop / Reduce Signals

LL should receive less founder investment if any of the following persist:

- collections miss the year floor without a credible recovery path;
- owner revenue does not justify the opportunity cost of ML1 time;
- the business requires sustained capacity breach to hit revenue targets;
- F02 and F03 remain content or planning exercises rather than paid demand;
- high-cash-flow retainers become open-ended service obligations;
- associate leverage creates more supervision burden than delivery relief;
- client quality declines in order to protect short-term cash.

## Immediate Actions By Mode

| Mode | Next 30 days |
|---|---|
| `Pause / Reduce` | Stop speculative build; review cash; close or price-reset weak matters; protect existing obligations |
| `Defend` | Limit LL build-time to cash protection, collections, and warm high-fit opportunities |
| `Hold / Harvest` | Keep profitable clients; cut low-fit work; avoid new service commitments |
| `Invest` | Fund only one or two named build priorities per quarter; define success measure before starting |
| `Expand` | Add capacity, systems, or channel investment only with a named capacity owner and stop rule |

No quarter may have more than two active LL build priorities unless LL is in
`Expand` mode.

## Portfolio Rule

Time invested in LL should be increased only when it improves at least one of:

- cash reliability;
- per-hour economics;
- client quality;
- delegation capacity;
- specialist authority;
- future exit or harvest value.

Time invested in LL should be reduced when it mainly produces planning activity,
unpaid complexity, or work that makes the firm more dependent on ML1 personal
capacity.

## Relationship To LLP-039

The fintech retainer model is one evidence source for this benchmark framework.
It can support the LL investment thesis if it produces controlled recurring
cash flow inside the approved capacity model.

It does not, by itself, justify expanding LL time investment. A portfolio of
retainers that creates scope creep, urgency load, or sustained ML1 capacity
breach counts against the investment thesis even if monthly cash flow is high.
