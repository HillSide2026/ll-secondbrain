# FinSure Progress Signal

Project ID: `HBP-011`
Stage: `Planning`

## Purpose

Record the current FinSure build as planning-stage evidence relevant to the
question of whether Matthew Holdings should pursue a narrower payment services
compliance service instead of a broad payments regulatory and compliance
agency.

## What FinSure Is

FinSure is a focused STR-drafting product built around a narrow compliance job:

- structured suspicious-activity intake
- deterministic red-flag detection
- readiness classification
- editable STR narrative generation
- missing-information prompts
- checklist and full draft-package export

Current headline/copy baseline:

- `FinSure: Review-Ready STRs in less than 60 Seconds`
- `FinSure generates Suspicious Transaction Report drafts so your team can review and submit faster.`

## Current Build Status

As of 2026-03-20:

- the product repo has been materially reworked around the STR use case
- validation passed for:
  - typecheck
  - unit tests
  - build
  - browser smoke coverage
- the application is deployed on Render at `https://finsure-w321.onrender.com`
- the intended public domain is `https://fintechlawyer.ca`
- the remaining launch blocker is HostGator DNS cutover from the current WordPress site to Render

## Why This Matters To HBP-011

FinSure is evidence that a narrower service concept may be more credible than a
generic agency concept.

The signal supports consideration of a payment services compliance service with
characteristics such as:

- practical MSB / FINTRAC workflow support
- structured drafting and reporting assistance
- focused compliance operations support rather than broad advisory sprawl
- a productized wedge that may support a service relationship

## What This Signal Does Not Prove

FinSure does not yet prove:

- repeatable customer demand
- a stable pricing model
- a full agency operating model
- a clean Levine Law / Matthew Holdings service boundary
- that a broad standalone consulting agency is the right path

## Planning Implication

The existence of FinSure should push `HBP-011` to evaluate a narrower payment
services compliance service path explicitly before endorsing a broad payments
regulatory and compliance agency model.
