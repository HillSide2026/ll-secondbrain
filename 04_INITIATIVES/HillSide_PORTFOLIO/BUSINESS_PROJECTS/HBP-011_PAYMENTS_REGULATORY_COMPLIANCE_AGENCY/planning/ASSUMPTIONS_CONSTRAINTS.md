# Assumptions and Constraints

Project ID: `HBP-011`
Stage: `Planning`

## Assumptions

- there is enough strategic signal to justify executing a bounded Payment Services Consulting Line
- at least one operating path can be defined clearly without collapsing into a vague services business
- the most valuable planning output is a cleaner decision on boundary, economics, and channel fit
- the agency must remain narrower than the initial broad agency concept

## Constraints

- planning must not blur Levine Law legal work and 175 consulting work
- no public launch, branding rollout, or client-facing commitment is authorized during planning
- no hiring or material operating spend is authorized during planning
- any path that cannot articulate a believable buyer/channel thesis should be treated as non-viable

## Locked Planning Direction

Planning has now narrowed the path to the Payment Services Consulting Line.

Currently locked:

- operate through `17513721 Canada Inc`
- use an agency business model
- use two entry points:
  - FinSure STR report
  - FINTRAC effectiveness review readiness lead magnet with paid consultation
    offer
- use the approved six-tier non-legal services ladder:
  - tier 1: FinSure STR report, consulting only
  - tier 2: Rhizome white label through `HBP-010`
  - tier 3: managed compliance, no CAMLO provision
  - tier 4: CAMS operational consulting, outsourced operations track
  - tier 5: licensing support, outsourced high-ticket track
  - tier 6: CAMLO sourcing, 1-year contract minimum
- keep Rhizome white-label work outside `HBP-011`

Still not locked:

- pricing model for tiers 2-5
- final tier 6 price; `$3,000 CAD/month` is a reference price and may be too low
- final service-package exclusions
- provider selection and commercial terms for the licensing track
- provider selection and commercial terms for the CAMS operational consulting
  track
- whether a second, infrastructure-focused service model should be offered

## Planning Boundary

This planning stage is limited to service-boundary framing, option comparison,
channel and economics assessment, and ML1 decision preparation.
