# Decision Frame

Project: `HBP-011` - Payments Regulatory & Compliance Consulting Agency

## Decision Question

Should Matthew Holdings build a standalone payments regulatory and compliance
consulting agency and, if so, what operating boundary is worth deeper planning?

## Candidate Paths

| Option | Description | Primary Upside | Primary Constraint |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Standalone agency | Build a distinct consulting agency under Matthew Holdings with a defined payments/compliance niche | clean venture thesis if differentiation is real | requires strong channel, boundary, and capability clarity |
| Adjacent consulting layer | Build a narrower consulting offer designed to complement Levine Law rather than stand fully alone | may benefit from adjacent expertise and channel access | overlap and governance boundaries may still be hard to manage |
| Internal capability only | Develop the capability as internal option value without launching an external agency yet | preserves learning without immediate market commitment | may not validate real market demand |
| Defer / no-go | Decline or pause the agency concept until the boundary and economics are materially clearer | preserves focus and avoids premature build-out | opportunity may be delayed or missed |

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | What must be true before Planning should open |
| --- | --- |
| Service boundary | The consulting offer is clearly separated from legal services and from an overly broad agency model |
| Differentiation | The offer has a credible niche or advantage beyond generic consulting positioning |
| Channel access | There is at least one believable path to buyer access and business development |
| Economics | The model can plausibly support margin after delivery and business-development effort |
| Operating burden | Minimum staffing, tooling, and delivery assumptions remain proportionate to the upside |
| Portfolio fit | The model does not create unmanaged conflict with Levine Law or other portfolio assets |

## Required Evidence Before Planning

- one preferred operating boundary is selected or the option set is narrowed tightly
- target buyer and initial offer set are described concretely
- business-development assumptions are defined at a minimum viable level
- minimum delivery model and capability needs are explicit
- no-go triggers are documented clearly enough for ML1 use

## Default Posture

Default posture is `hold` until the agency concept is clear enough to compare
boundary, channel fit, economics, and portfolio impact on a like-for-like basis.
