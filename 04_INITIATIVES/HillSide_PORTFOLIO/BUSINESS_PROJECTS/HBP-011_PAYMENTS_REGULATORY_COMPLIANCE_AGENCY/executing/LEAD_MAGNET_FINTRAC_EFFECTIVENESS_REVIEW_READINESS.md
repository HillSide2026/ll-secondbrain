# FINTRAC Effectiveness Review Readiness Lead Magnet

Project ID: `HBP-011`
Related Strategy Project: `HBP-014`

## Title

**Is Your MSB Ready for FINTRAC Effectiveness Review?**

A Practical Readiness Checklist for Canadian Money Services Businesses.

## Role In HBP-011

This is one of two entry points for the Payment Services Consulting Line. It is
meant to qualify existing Canadian MSBs for a paid pre-review readiness
assessment / remediation session.

HBP-011 entry points:

1. FinSure STR report
2. FINTRAC effectiveness review readiness checklist with paid consultation offer

The lead magnet complements the FinSure STR report and can create demand for:

- tier 3 managed compliance, no CAMLO provision
- tier 4 CAMS operational consulting
- tier 5 licensing support where readiness issues connect to licensing /
  registration gaps
- tier 6 CAMLO sourcing where governance gaps reveal a missing or unsuitable
  responsible person

## Relationship To HBP-014

HBP-014 is the 175 / Dominion / Andersen strategy project. HBP-011 is the
execution packet for the payment-services service line within HBP-014.

This lead magnet is not the full 175 strategy. It is a demand-generation and
qualification artifact for the payment-services bucket only.

Target audience:

- operators of existing MSBs

Goal:

Help MSBs assess whether their AML compliance program is genuinely operational
before commissioning an independent effectiveness review.

## Checklist Structure

### Section 1 - Governance

Checklist:

- Chief Anti-Money Laundering Officer formally appointed
- Compliance responsibilities documented
- Senior management oversight evidenced
- AML governance meetings documented
- Escalation procedures operational
- Compliance decisions recorded consistently

Common weakness:

Policies exist, but governance evidence is inconsistent or undocumented.

### Section 2 - Policies & Procedures

Checklist:

- AML policy reviewed within the last 12 months
- Procedures align with actual operational workflows
- High-risk customer procedures documented and operationalized
- Sanctions procedures operationalized
- STR escalation procedures documented
- Recordkeeping requirements mapped operationally
- Product risk reflected accurately

Common weakness:

Written procedures do not match day-to-day operations.

### Section 3 - Know Your Client / Know Your Business

Checklist:

- Customer onboarding workflows documented
- Beneficial ownership procedures implemented
- Business customer verification evidence retained
- Ongoing monitoring procedures operational
- Enhanced due diligence workflows documented
- Customer risk scoring methodology defined
- High-risk customer reviews evidenced

Common weakness:

Customer files are incomplete or inconsistently reviewed.

### Section 4 - Transaction Monitoring & Reporting

Checklist:

- Transaction monitoring rules documented
- Alert review workflows operational
- STR decision-making evidence retained
- Large cash / EFT reporting workflows tested
- Sanctions screening operational
- Escalation timelines documented
- Monitoring thresholds periodically reviewed

Common weakness:

Monitoring exists manually but cannot be evidenced consistently.

### Section 5 - Training

Checklist:

- AML training completed annually
- Training attendance records retained
- Role-specific training delivered where appropriate
- New employees trained during onboarding
- Refresher training documented
- Testing or comprehension validation completed

Common weakness:

Training records are incomplete or generic.

### Section 6 - Risk Assessment

Checklist:

- Enterprise-wide risk assessment documented
- Products / services risk evaluated
- Geographic exposure assessed
- Customer risk categories defined
- Delivery channel risks documented
- Third-party / vendor risks assessed
- Risk assessment updated regularly

Common weakness:

Risk assessments are static documents disconnected from operations.

### Section 7 - Recordkeeping & Auditability

Checklist:

- Client identification records retained properly
- Transaction records reproducible
- Compliance decisions documented
- STR / SAR supporting rationale retained
- Audit trails operational
- Record retention timelines enforced
- Regulatory requests reproducible efficiently

Common weakness:

Evidence exists across fragmented systems and cannot be reconstructed quickly.

### Section 8 - Independent Review Readiness

Checklist:

- Prior review findings remediated
- Testing evidence centralized
- Sample transaction files organized
- Policies mapped to operational controls
- Compliance metrics available
- Monitoring evidence exportable
- Operational walkthroughs reproducible

Common weakness:

MSBs prepare policies for the reviewer instead of demonstrating operational
effectiveness.

## Final CTA

### Before You Commission An Effectiveness Review

Most independent reviews do not fail because policies are missing.

They fail because:

- controls cannot be evidenced
- workflows are inconsistent
- operational practices diverge from documented procedures
