# Problem Statement — HBP-012 — Info Product

## The Problem

Early-stage founders and small business owners consistently operate without adequate
legal protection because the cost of engaging a lawyer to draft contracts is
prohibitive at their stage. A single drafted agreement from a commercial lawyer
typically costs $500–$2,500 or more. For a founder closing their first client deal
or bringing on a contractor, that cost is often skipped — leaving them exposed.

The result: founders sign contracts they don't understand, use templates they pulled
from the internet that don't reflect their jurisdiction or situation, or operate on
handshake agreements. When something goes wrong, the legal exposure is material and
the cost to fix it is far higher than prevention would have been.

## Why It Exists / Why Now

Founders are launching businesses constantly. Legal protection is not a new need —
but productized legal tools for early-stage operators at an accessible price point are
underprovided. Most legal content online is either free (and generic) or expensive
(and advisory). The gap between "free internet template" and "hire a lawyer" is where
this product lives.

For ML1 specifically: the templates in this pack have already been used in real
engagements. They are not generic — they reflect actual Canadian commercial practice.
Productizing them now requires minimal net production work and converts existing
intellectual capital into a sellable asset.

## Why Matthew Holdings Is Positioned to Solve It

ML1 has drafted these templates for paying legal clients. The commercial, contractor,
and service agreement structures in this pack are derived from real engagements — not
sourced from generic legal databases. The plain-English explanations are written by a
practicing lawyer, not a content writer.

This gives the product a credibility advantage over generic template sites: the buyer
is getting a cleaned-up version of what a lawyer actually uses, explained in plain
English, at a fraction of the cost of engagement.

## What Happens If the Problem Goes Unsolved

Founders who skip legal protection will eventually encounter one of the following:
- a dispute with a client, contractor, or partner where no enforceable agreement exists
- a contract signed without understanding the liability, IP, or termination clauses
- a business relationship that breaks down with no documented terms to fall back on

The cost of remediation — dispute resolution, litigation, or retroactive structuring —
typically exceeds the cost of prevention by an order of magnitude.
