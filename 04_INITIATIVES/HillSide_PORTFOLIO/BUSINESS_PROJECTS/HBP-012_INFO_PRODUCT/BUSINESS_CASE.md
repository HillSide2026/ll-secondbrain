# Business Case — HBP-012 — Info Product

## Strategic Rationale

Matthew Holdings holds intellectual capital in the form of contract templates drafted
for real legal engagements. This project converts that capital into a sellable digital
product with near-zero marginal cost per unit, requiring ~5–8 hours of one-time
production work and no ongoing delivery cost.

The strategic case is:
1. **Minimal investment, fast validation.** 5–8 hours of production + infrastructure
   setup. If it doesn't sell, the cost is contained. If it sells, it validates a
   repeatable revenue channel.
2. **New ICP with no current product.** Early-stage founders who can't afford counsel
   are not served by Levine Law's existing F2/F3 positioning. This product accesses
   that demand without creating conflict with the LL practice.
3. **Potential front-end for a higher-ticket backend.** Template buyers who need more
   help are warm leads for a contract review service ($500–$1,500) or retainer. That
   upsell path is unproven but structurally sound.

## Revenue Model

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Price per unit | $197 CAD |
| Stripe processing fee (est.) | ~$6.00 per transaction (2.9% + $0.30) |
| Net revenue per unit | ~$191 |
| Primary target (30 days) | 6 units = ~$1,182 gross / ~$1,146 net |
| Scale scenario (90 days) | 30 units = ~$5,910 gross / ~$5,730 net |

## Cost Estimate

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| ML1 production time (5–8 hrs) | Opportunity cost only — no cash outlay |
| VA support (formatting, cleanup) | Minimal — existing VA relationship |
| GHL (already subscribed) | $0 marginal cost |
| Stripe (per transaction) | ~$6 per unit (variable) |
| Total cash outlay | Near zero |

## Break-Even

Break-even is effectively the first sale — there is no fixed cash investment to
recover. The real break-even is ML1's time: 8 hours production + 4 hours setup
= 12 hours total. At $197/unit net ~$191, break-even on time is approximately
63 units at a notional $150/hr ML1 rate — this product is not a high-return use
of ML1 time at low volume. The case rests on low volume being sufficient to
validate, and scale or backend converting that picture.

## Backend / Downstream Value

The upsell path exists but is not formalized:
- **Contract review service:** Buyer pays $197 for templates, then pays $500–$1,500
  for ML1 to review a specific contract they're about to sign. Natural conversion
  from template buyer to review client.
- **Retainer:** Less likely at this ICP stage, but possible for founders who graduate
  to needing ongoing counsel.

Backend is out of scope for Phase 1. It becomes relevant only if the primary
success threshold ($1,000 in 30 days) is met.

## Alternatives Considered

| Alternative | Why Not Chosen |
|---|---|
| Free lead magnet (no price) | Doesn't validate willingness-to-pay; devalues content |
| Higher price ($497+) | Risk: ICP can't afford it; reduces conversion at launch |
| Lower price ($47–$97) | Risk: undervalues work; may recover later if $197 doesn't convert |
| Course or ebook format | Wrong fit for this ICP — they want done-for-you tools, not education |

## Recommendation

Proceed to Planning and launch. The risk-adjusted case is strong: minimal cash
outlay, one-time production investment, fast validation window, clear success
criteria, and contained failure cost. The 48-hour launch constraint is appropriate
— this project's primary risk is over-engineering, not under-preparation.
