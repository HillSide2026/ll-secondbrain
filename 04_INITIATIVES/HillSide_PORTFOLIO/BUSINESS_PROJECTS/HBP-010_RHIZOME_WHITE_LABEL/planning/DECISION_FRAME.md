# Decision Frame

Project: `HBP-010` - Rhizome White Label

## Decision Question

Should Matthew Holdings pursue a Rhizome-linked white-label path and, if so,
which structure is worth deeper planning?

## Candidate Paths

| Option | Description | Primary Upside | Primary Constraint |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| White-label distribution | Rhizome provides the underlying software while Matthew Holdings controls the branded go-to-market surface | fastest path to market if roles are clean | high dependency on partner and unclear client ownership risk |
| Services-led wrapper | Matthew Holdings uses Rhizome as underlying infrastructure but sells an advisory or managed-service wrapper | greater control over positioning and value capture | support and delivery complexity may increase materially |
| Referral or partnership lane | Keep the relationship light and avoid a formal white-label commitment | lowest dependency and build burden | limited economics and less control over user experience |
| Defer / no-go | Decline or pause the opportunity until the model is materially clearer | preserves focus and avoids premature lock-in | opportunity may be missed or delayed |

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | What must be true before Planning should open |
| --- | --- |
| Strategic fit | The opportunity improves portfolio position without overconcentrating on one external partner |
| Client and channel control | The ownership of client relationship, branding, onboarding, and support is explicit |
| Economics | Margin and effort are credible enough to justify the dependency created |
| Delivery boundary | Product, service, and compliance responsibilities are separated clearly |
| Exit and dependency protection | The structure includes realistic ways to unwind or reduce lock-in if the relationship underperforms |

## Required Evidence Before Planning

- one preferred structure is selected or the option set is narrowed tightly
- commercial responsibilities and client ownership are described concretely
- support, escalation, and delivery responsibilities are explicit
- minimum acceptable economics are defined
- no-go triggers are documented clearly enough for ML1 use

## Default Posture

Default posture is `hold` until the opportunity is concrete enough to compare
control, economics, and dependency exposure on a like-for-like basis.
