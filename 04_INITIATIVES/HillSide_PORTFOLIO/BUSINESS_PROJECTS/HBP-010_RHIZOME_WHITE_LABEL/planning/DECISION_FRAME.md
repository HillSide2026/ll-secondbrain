# Decision Frame

Project: `HBP-010` - Rhizome White Label

## Decision Question

Which Rhizome-linked structure should advance beyond planning?

## Candidate Paths

| Option | Description | Primary Upside | Primary Constraint | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| White-label distribution | Rhizome provides the underlying software while Matthew Holdings controls the branded go-to-market surface | fastest path to market if roles are clean | high dependency on partner and unclear client ownership risk | not selected |
| Services-led wrapper | Matthew Holdings uses Rhizome as underlying infrastructure but sells an advisory or managed-service wrapper | greater control over positioning and value capture | support and delivery complexity may increase materially | selected |
| Referral or partnership lane | Keep the relationship light and avoid a formal white-label commitment | lowest dependency and build burden | limited economics and less control over user experience | not selected |
| Defer / no-go | Decline or pause the opportunity until the model is materially clearer | preserves focus and avoids premature lock-in | opportunity may be missed or delayed | not selected |

## Planning Exit Criteria

| Criterion | Planning exit test |
| --- | --- |
| Strategic fit | The opportunity improves portfolio position without overconcentrating on one external partner |
| Client and channel control | The ownership of client relationship, branding, onboarding, and support is explicit |
| Economics | Margin and effort are credible enough to justify the dependency created |
| Delivery boundary | Product, service, and compliance responsibilities are separated clearly |
| Exit and dependency protection | The structure includes realistic ways to unwind or reduce lock-in if the relationship underperforms |

## Planning Exit Evidence

- one preferred structure has been selected
- commercial responsibilities and client ownership have been narrowed enough to proceed
- support, escalation, and delivery responsibilities are explicit enough to enter controlled execution
- minimum acceptable economics have been defined at a planning baseline
- no-go triggers and dependency concerns are documented for execution-stage refinement

## Decision Outcome

ML1 has decided to proceed.

Selected operating boundary:

- services-led wrapper

Executing scope:

- partner negotiation and commercial structure
- client, onboarding, support, and escalation boundary definition
- dependency protection and exit logic
- implementation planning for the Rhizome wrapper model

Excluded from `HBP-010`:

- FinSure product build-out
- payment services delivery execution
- CAMLO staffing or client-service appointment planning
- `HBP-011` service packaging or launch execution
