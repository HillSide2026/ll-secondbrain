# Project Charter

**Project:** Rhizome White Label  
**Project ID:** HBP-010  
**Project Path:** `04_INITIATIVES/HillSide_PORTFOLIO/BUSINESS_PROJECTS/HBP-010_RHIZOME_WHITE_LABEL`  
**Project Type:** Decision  
**Stage:** Initiating

## Sponsor

ML1

## Mission

Produce a controlled ML1 decision on whether a Rhizome-linked white-label path
should be pursued, deferred, declined, or restructured.

## Decision Question

What exactly are we deciding?

Should Matthew Holdings pursue a Rhizome-linked white-label path and, if so,
which structure should advance to formal planning?

## Real Options Under Consideration

1. white-label distribution
2. services-led wrapper
3. referral or partnership lane
4. defer / no-go

## Evaluation Criteria

- strategic fit with Matthew Holdings and broader portfolio direction
- client and channel control
- economic viability after dependency and support burden are considered
- delivery-boundary clarity across product, service, and compliance roles
- dependency protection and ability to unwind if the model underperforms

## Stage Plan

1. define the realistic structure options for the Rhizome opportunity
2. assess partner dependency, client ownership, and delivery boundary
3. apply the evaluation criteria to the option set
4. prepare an ML1 decision packet for next-step authorization

## Immediate Next Actions

- distinguish between white-label software, services wrapper, referral path, and hybrid options
- clarify who would own the client relationship, onboarding, support, and delivery
- identify the minimum economics needed for the model to be worth pursuing
- document what conditions would make the opportunity a no-go

## Risks

- the project could move too quickly into partner assumptions before the model is defined
- dependency on one external channel could be underestimated
- integration or customization work could be implied before it is intentionally approved

## Governance

ML1-controlled stage gate. No planning, build work, partner commitment, or
external launch activity is authorized until ML1 approves the transition out of
Initiating.
