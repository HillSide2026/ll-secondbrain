# Risk Scan

Project: `HBP-014` - 175 Dominion Andersen Service Line

## Strategic Risk Thesis

The core risk is not paperwork or brand usage. The core risk is that 175
mistakes access to the Andersen ecosystem for an actual business.

HBP-014 succeeds only if 175 converts a relationship-dependent opportunity into
a credible, repeatable, economically meaningful service line. The main failure
modes are channel politics, weak capability, diffuse positioning, non-converting
BD, and regulated-scope traps.

## Top Strategic Risks

| Risk | Category | Severity | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Andersen-channel dependence without channel control | Strategic | High | 175 depends on Andersen / Sidera access but does not control Andersen demand generation, internal referrals, client ownership, or deal routing. A friendly relationship may not become a reliable channel. |
| Sidera gatekeeper risk | Strategic | High | Sidera is valuable, but if it becomes the only practical gateway, 175 may be boxed into Sidera's priorities, pace, politics, and client base rather than building an independent Dominion platform. |
| Five service lines dilute the offer | Scope | High | Trade remedies, market access, data centers, mining, and payment services may be too broad for a young collaborating firm. The platform may look strategic internally but vague externally. |
| Credibility gap in trade remedies | Strategic | High | Trade remedies is technically demanding and deadline-driven. If 175 cannot credibly staff or supervise the work, the highest-value line could damage trust quickly. |
| Data centers and mining become narrative verticals, not mandates | Strategic | Medium / High | These verticals sound compelling, but without named buyers, live problems, and a concrete Andersen role, they remain relationship theatre rather than revenue. |
| Payment services creates regulated-scope exposure | Scope | High | Stablecoin adoption, licensing, and compliance advice can blur legal advice, consulting, regulated activity, and operational payment services. This line can create reputational and regulatory risk if not tightly scoped. |
| BD-to-revenue conversion failure | Financial | High | The relationship may generate ideas, meetings, and decks but no paid work. Without a qualification gate, 175 can spend scarce ML1 time on non-paying pipeline. |
| ML1 time becomes the bottleneck and the product | Strategic | High | If every service line depends on ML1 personally selling, scoping, delivering, and supervising, 175 does not build enterprise value; it becomes another personal-services wrapper. |
| Misaligned economics after Andersen deductions and delivery cost | Financial | Medium / High | USD headline rates may look attractive, but net receipts after Andersen deductions, FX, tax, subcontractors, and internal time may not justify the relationship burden. |
| Channel conflict with Levine Law | Scope | Medium / High | The same opportunity may look like Levine Law work, 175 consulting work, Andersen work, or a hybrid. If not governed, economics, conflicts, privilege, and client expectations can get messy. |
| Overbuilding Dominion Platform before conversion proof | Budget | Medium | Website, sub-brands, FlowSignal, D-AirPay, and collateral can consume attention before the business proves a repeatable source of qualified opportunities. |

## Risk Posture

HBP-014 should be treated as a high-upside, high-ambiguity strategic option,
not yet as an operating business. The project should not expand all five
service lines equally. Planning should force a sequence:

1. choose the first line most likely to convert;
2. define the minimum credible delivery bench;
3. test one Andersen-routed opportunity;
4. prove fee path and economics;
5. only then invest in broader platform build-out.
