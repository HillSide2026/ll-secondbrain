# Business Case

Project: `HBP-014` - 175 Dominion Andersen Service Line

## Case for the Project

`17513721 Canada Inc`, operating as Dominion Partners, has the opportunity to
become an Andersen Consulting collaborating firm with a focused international
trade advisory business line.

This is strategically meaningful because 175 can become more than a holding or
fee-receipt entity. It can own a repeatable business line, channel
relationships, service assets, and a pipeline of Andersen-routed opportunities.

## Service-Line Thesis

The business case rests on five service lines:

- trade remedies
- market access
- data centers
- mining
- payment services

These lines match existing Andersen relationship signals and Dominion platform
assets closely enough to justify a controlled HillSide project.

## Strategic Value

HBP-014 can create value by:

- turning Andersen relationship activity into a governed 175 business line
- giving Dominion Partners a clear service-line architecture
- converting matter-level BD signals into qualified opportunities
- supporting higher-value USD-denominated advisory economics
- building 175 enterprise value separate from pure ML1 personal goodwill

## Economic Logic

Current Andersen economics in the related matter records use a starting rate of
`USD 650/hour`, subject to management and royalty deductions. Even with those
deductions, the relationship may support materially stronger economics than
standard direct domestic work if realization and collection are achieved.

The project should not assume revenue until a qualified opportunity exists and
the fee path through Andersen / 175 is confirmed.

## Decision Rationale

Opening HBP-014 is justified because:

- the opportunity is already active enough to require governance
- the service-line taxonomy now needs a controlled source of truth
- the Andersen / Sidera relationship carries both upside and relationship risk
- 175 enterprise-value strategy should not be buried inside Levine Law matter
  records

## Do-Not-Do Boundaries

HBP-014 should not:

- make external brand or title claims beyond the collaborating-firm agreement
- present payment services as operational regulated activity unless properly
  authorized and scoped
- overstate trade-remedies capability
- treat 175 cash receipts as Levine Law receipts
- create unmanaged channel conflict inside Andersen or with Sidera
