# Risk Register

| Risk | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Andersen-channel dependence without channel control | Strategic | H | H | Treat Andersen as a channel option, not the business itself. Record who controls client access, fee routing, and next action for each opportunity. |
| Sidera gatekeeper risk | Strategic | M | H | Preserve Sidera trust while identifying non-conflicting Andersen allies. Do not let all service-line strategy depend on one relationship node. |
| Five service lines dilute the offer | Scope | H | H | Identify the core offer before external push. Select one priority service line for first push and keep other lines in watch / prepared posture until a real opportunity appears. |
| Trade-remedies credibility gap | Strategic | M | H | Identify specialist bench before advancing a live file. Do not accept deadline-driven work unless staffing and supervision are real. |
| Data centers remain a vertical narrative without buyer demand | Strategic | M | H | Require a named buyer or Andersen sponsor, specific problem, jurisdiction, and commercial reason for urgency before investing effort. |
| Mining remains relationship theatre | Strategic | M | H | Treat mining as the lowest-priority service line unless a named-company opportunity appears. Convert mining discussions into concrete mandates or park them. |
| Payment services creates regulated-scope exposure | Scope | M | H | Define whether work is legal advice, consulting, licensing support, compliance design, or operational payment services before any external pitch. |
| Stablecoin adoption work outruns regulatory clarity or client sophistication | Scope | M | H | Limit early work to advisory diagnostics, treasury architecture, and risk mapping. Avoid implementation commitments until licensing and counterparties are clear. |
| BD-to-revenue conversion failure | Financial | H | H | Enforce a qualification gate: client, problem, scope, decision-maker, timing, and fee path. Review stale opportunities after 60 days. |
| ML1 time becomes the bottleneck and product | Strategic | H | H | Define which tasks can be delegated to analyst / consultant / specialist roles and what must remain ML1-led. Measure ML1 hours per qualified opportunity. |
| Net economics disappoint after deductions and delivery cost | Financial | M | H | Model gross billing, Andersen deductions, subcontractor cost, FX, tax, ML1 time, and net 175 receipts before treating a line as attractive. |
| Channel conflict with Levine Law | Scope | M | H | For each opportunity, record client of record, engagement vehicle, privilege posture, conflict check, and cash path before work starts. |
| Dominion Platform overbuilds ahead of market proof | Budget | M | M | Build only assets needed to convert the next qualified opportunity. Defer sub-brand and tooling expansion until conversion evidence exists. |
| 175 enterprise value fails to separate from ML1 personal goodwill | Strategic | H | H | Delegate fee-earning work wherever possible. Require reusable assets, repeatable qualification rules, delegated delivery capacity, and relationship records owned by 175. |
