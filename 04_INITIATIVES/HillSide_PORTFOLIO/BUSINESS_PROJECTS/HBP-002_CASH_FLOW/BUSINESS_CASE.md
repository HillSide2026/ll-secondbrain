# Business Case

Project: `HBP-002` - Cash Flow

## Opportunity

Matthew needs one management-reporting layer that can measure all positive and
negative cash flow between Matthew and the key HillSide entities that affect
his capital position.

The case for treating this as a strategic project is not that it creates a new
external business line. It is that cash-flow visibility and discipline are a
core control layer for capital stewardship across the HillSide branch.

## Strategic Rationale

- better cash-flow visibility can improve capital-allocation timing and confidence
- the true sources and uses of Matthew's cash become comparable on one decision basis
- a governed reporting and review loop can strengthen discipline across HillSide
- cleaner definitions reduce handoff friction with accounting, bookkeeping, and external advisors

## Value Thesis

If this project succeeds, HillSide should gain:

- one monthly pack showing gross inflows to Matthew, gross outflows from Matthew, and net cash flow by entity and in aggregate
- explicit line-item treatment for `17513721 Canada Inc`, YellowBricks,
  Federal MSB (`17409052 Canada Inc`), Ontario MSB Corp (`1001494374 Ontario Corp`), and `Levine Professional Corporation` (`Levine Law`)
- consistent treatment of distributions, compensation, reimbursements, loans, capital contributions, and YellowBricks post-closing cash events
- a more reliable basis for deciding when to hold, reinvest, distribute, or reallocate capital

`Matthew Holdings` may still appear elsewhere in HillSide as ML1's internal
planning label for the layer between himself and `17513721 Canada Inc`, but it
is not a separate entity in this cash-flow model.

## Constraints

- this project should not expand into a full accounting-system replacement or a broad internal entity-performance dashboard program at initiation stage
- no automation or tooling build should be assumed before the reporting boundary is locked
- accounting records remain canonical even if this project creates an additional management view
- unrealized valuation gains or losses are not cash-flow metrics and must be reported separately if needed
- supporting entity-level cash-flow views may be used only to explain or reconcile Matthew-level cash movement

## Initial Recommendation

Proceed as a strategic internal-control and planning project, not as a finance
software build or a broad accounting transformation.

The next-stage question should be whether HillSide needs a lightweight monthly
close and reporting system, and what its minimum viable scope should be.

## Decision Standard

Proceed to Planning only if ML1 concludes that:

- the reporting boundary can be fixed clearly across Matthew and the five in-scope entities
- the Matthew-level cash-movement definitions are specific enough for CPA-style review
- the project can stay tightly scoped to internal control, planning, and reporting discipline
