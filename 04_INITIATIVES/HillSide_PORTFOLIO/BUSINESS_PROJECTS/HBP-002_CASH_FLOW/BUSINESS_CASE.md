# Business Case

Project: `HBP-002` - Cash Flow

## Opportunity

Matthew Holdings needs one management-reporting layer that can measure cash
generation and free cash flow across both the investment portfolio and the
operating businesses that Matthew owns or builds.

The case for treating this as a strategic project is not that it creates a new
external business line. It is that cash-flow visibility and discipline are a
core control layer for capital stewardship across the HillSide branch.

## Strategic Rationale

- better cash-flow visibility can improve capital-allocation timing and confidence
- portfolio assets and operating businesses become comparable on one decision basis
- a governed reporting and review loop can strengthen discipline across HillSide
- cleaner definitions reduce handoff friction with accounting, bookkeeping, and external advisors

## Value Thesis

If this project succeeds, HillSide should gain:

- one monthly pack showing portfolio cash flow and portfolio free cash flow
- entity-level cash flow and free cash flow for Levine Law, `175 / FinSure`, and future businesses
- explicit treatment of YellowBricks sale proceeds and residual post-closing cash movements
- a more reliable basis for deciding when to hold, reinvest, distribute, or reallocate capital

## Constraints

- this project should not expand into a full accounting-system replacement at initiation stage
- no automation or tooling build should be assumed before the reporting boundary is locked
- accounting records remain canonical even if this project creates an additional management view
- unrealized valuation gains or losses are not cash-flow metrics and must be reported separately if needed
- discretionary owner distributions and new investment deployments should be shown separately from free cash flow

## Initial Recommendation

Proceed as a strategic internal-control and planning project, not as a finance
software build or a broad accounting transformation.

The next-stage question should be whether HillSide needs a lightweight monthly
close and reporting system, and what its minimum viable scope should be.

## Decision Standard

Proceed to Planning only if ML1 concludes that:

- the reporting boundary can be fixed clearly across portfolio and operating businesses
- the metric definitions are specific enough for CPA-style review
- the project can stay tightly scoped to internal control, planning, and reporting discipline
