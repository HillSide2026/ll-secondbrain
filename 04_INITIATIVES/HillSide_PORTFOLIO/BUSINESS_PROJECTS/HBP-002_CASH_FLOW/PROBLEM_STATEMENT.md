# Problem Statement

Project: `HBP-002` - Cash Flow

## Problem

Matthew Holdings does not yet have a CPA-quality cash-flow measurement model
across its portfolio and operating businesses. Approximately CAD 1.6M of cash
and securities is under management, including 100% equity in Levine Law, but
the reporting boundary and metric definitions are not yet formalized well
enough to support confident capital decisions.

Current gaps:

- no formal split between portfolio cash flow / free cash flow and business cash flow / free cash flow
- no controlled list of in-scope entities and realized exit events, including Levine Law, `175 / FinSure`, and YellowBricks
- no defined month-end close, review, or variance discipline
- no decision-ready plan for how source records roll into management reporting

## Proposed Solution

Stand up a controlled initiation-stage strategic project to define the
cash-flow scope, metric definitions, control model, and planning boundary
before building a larger operating system around it.

## Impact of Not Solving

- capital deployment decisions may remain slower or lower-confidence than necessary
- portfolio assets and operating businesses may be compared on inconsistent bases
- realized proceeds, distributions, and free cash flow may be miscoded or missed
