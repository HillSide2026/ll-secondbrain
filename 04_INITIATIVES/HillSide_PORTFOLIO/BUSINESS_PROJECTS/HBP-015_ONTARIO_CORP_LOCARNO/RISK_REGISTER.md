# Risk Register

**Project:** Ontario Corp / Locarno
**Project ID:** HBP-015
**Stage:** Executing
**Governing policy:** POL-065 (Matthew Holdings Initiative Risk Policy)

---

## Schedule Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S-01 | FINTRAC standing has unresolved deficiencies — Track A readiness is delayed | Medium | High | Initiate verification as first executing task; no other workstream proceeds until confirmed |
| S-02 | ConnectPay declines to contract with a pre-revenue entity — Track B EMI milestone slips | Medium | High | Approach with minimum-commitment ask; secondary EMI outreach authorized from 2026-07-15 if ConnectPay is unclear |
| S-03 | RPAA acquisition-of-control rules require re-registration or formal buyer notification — sale outreach delayed | Low | High | Complete RPAA transfer analysis by 2026-07-15 before any sale outreach |
| S-04 | ConnectPay integration takes longer than expected — Track B Preferred gate misses 2026-08-30 | Medium | Medium | Scope integration to minimum viable; do not wait for ConnectPay to begin AML and ledger specification |
| S-05 | RPAA application processing time extends beyond expected window | Low | Medium | Apply early; do not make Track B Preferred gate contingent on RPAA issuance if registration is demonstrably in process |
| S-06 | HBP-013 capacity competition delays HBP-015 milestones | High | Medium | Explicit ML1 sequencing decision on HBP-013 vs HBP-015 priority at each milestone |
| S-07 | Rhizome arrangement (HBP-010) stalls — Track B compliance software undefined past budget finalization date | Medium | Low | Sumsub is the defined fallback; cost differential between Rhizome and Sumsub must be in the budget by 2026-07-31 |

## Financial Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F-01 | EMI contract terms include exclusivity, non-assignment clauses, or punitive exit fees — transaction economics degraded for any buyer | Medium | High | Transferability terms are non-negotiable; walk away from any EMI that cannot provide assignment optionality and change-of-control protection |
| F-02 | ConnectPay monthly minimums are higher than expected — pre-revenue carrying cost erodes net proceeds | Medium | Medium | Cost ceiling to be established at budget finalization 2026-07-31; reject terms above ceiling |
| F-03 | Self-build cost overrun reduces net proceeds on sale | Medium | Medium | Self-build scope defined before any build commences; no scope expansion without ML1 approval |
| F-04 | Track B build costs in aggregate exceed incremental sale premium over Track A | Medium | Medium | Track A floor ($50k) is always-ready; Track B build stops if cost-to-premium ratio deteriorates |
| F-05 | Distribution build investment exceeds the sale premium it creates | Medium | Medium | Distribution approach must not require a live customer base before sale; a credible documented path is the standard |
| F-06 | Buyer negotiates sale price below working value marker — transaction economics below plan | Medium | Medium | Working value markers are planning assumptions; confirm buyer demand before fixing price expectations |
| F-07 | No buyer found at Track B/C value — forced to transact at Track A floor | Medium | Medium | Track A is always-ready at $50k; do not carry Track B costs indefinitely if buyer demand does not materialize |

## Scope Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SC-01 | Project drifts into building and operating a live BaaS or payments business under Locarno branding | Low | High | Scope review at every milestone; Locarno product layer supports entity sale, it does not launch an operating business |
| SC-02 | EMI integration requirements expand beyond minimum viable posture — bespoke build demanded by ConnectPay | Medium | Medium | Define minimum integration scope before outreach; resist requests for custom workflows, proprietary connectors, or non-standard API patterns |
| SC-03 | Distribution plan requires a live customer base before sale rather than a documented path — scope expands into product launch | Low | High | Distribution Plan confirmed 2026-06-14; scope of distribution work is a credible documented path, not a launch |
| SC-04 | Track C sponsor-bank requirement expands scope to include operational history before bank will engage | High | Medium | Track C proceeds only if a sponsor bank engages on documented terms; no operational history build authorized for Track C alone |

## Strategic Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ST-01 | Buyer market for Track B/C is thinner than expected — no buyer can be identified at the working value markers | Medium | High | Track A at $50k is always-ready; Track B/C build is not committed until buyer demand signal exists |
| ST-02 | HBP-013 (Granville/174) transacts first, saturating the same buyer pool | Low | Medium | Buyer profiles differ: 174 is federal, Ontario Corp has no Canadian director requirement; pools partially overlap but are not identical |
| ST-03 | Regulatory environment changes — RPAA amendments or FINTRAC rule changes reduce entity value to buyer | Low | High | Monitor regulatory developments; RPAA registration is the primary value asset and travels with entity on share sale |
| ST-04 | Locarno product thesis becomes obsolete before sale due to market or technology shift in payments infrastructure | Low | Medium | Distribution Plan and product layer are meant to improve sale value, not build a durable product; accelerate sale timeline if obsolescence risk increases |
