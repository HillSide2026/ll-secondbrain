# Risk Scan

## Scope Risks

- the project may be too vague if the 2026 program boundary is not defined
  clearly
- the project may try to govern Levine Law execution work instead of acting as
  a HillSide oversight project
- the project may overlap with separate HillSide projects in an unmanaged way
- the project may target cash collected and ML revenue without a controlled monthly review model
- the project may drift outside approved practice areas, approved solution
  frames, or approved Levine Law strategy

## Schedule Risks

- planning may stall if 2026 priorities are not narrowed early enough
- portfolio sequencing may become harder if Levine Law timing assumptions are
  left implicit

## Resource Risks

- Levine Law management time may be diluted across too many strategic threads
- other HillSide initiatives may compete for the same attention and decision
  capacity
- ML1 capacity may be exceeded if the cash-collected and ML-revenue targets are pursued through
  reactive or low-fit docket growth rather than approved lanes

## Governance Risks

- the project may remain a shell without becoming decision-ready for ML1
- monthly measurement ownership may stay unclear if accounting support and ML1
  roles are not documented explicitly
- metrics may stay too weak for meaningful planning review if the packet does
  not define program-level measures

## Current Risk Posture

The biggest immediate risk is scope ambiguity. The project should define a
bounded 2026 Levine Law oversight and expertise-building program rather than
act as a vague wrapper for the whole firm or a shadow execution system.
