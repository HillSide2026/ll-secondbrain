# Problem Statement

Levine Law needs a controlled way to define and govern its 2026 operating
program.

Right now, the project shell exists, but there is no single governed packet
that answers the core initiation questions:

- whether 2026 is being run against a real banked-cash target
- how monthly collections, margin, capacity, and client-quality controls are
  meant to be reviewed
- how ML1-docketed fee collections are meant to be tracked separately
- which approved practice areas and solution frames define the accepted work
- how Levine Law resource use should be sequenced against other HillSide work

That creates three immediate problems:

- strategic priorities can remain too vague for disciplined planning
- management attention can be spread across too many parallel efforts
- HillSide portfolio decisions can be made without a clear Levine Law program
  boundary

This project exists to establish a controlled 2026 Levine Law program packet
before planning and execution decisions are made.

If the project is not formalized, Levine Law risks carrying 2026 as an
informal mix of targets, docket work, and strategy references rather than a
governed cash-flow and expertise-building program.
