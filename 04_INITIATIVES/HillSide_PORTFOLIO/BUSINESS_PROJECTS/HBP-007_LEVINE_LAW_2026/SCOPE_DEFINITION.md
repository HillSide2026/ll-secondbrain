# Scope Definition

**Project:** Levine Law 2026
**Project ID:** HBP-007
**Stage:** Planning

---

## In Scope

### Financial Goals

- Cash collected by LL reaches at least CAD 240,000 by `2026-12-31`
- Revenue to ML reaches at least CAD 80,000 by `2026-12-31`

### Monthly Oversight

- Monthly oversight pack covering collections, margin, capacity, and
  client-quality controls — produced and reviewed every month in 2026
- ML1-docketed fee collections tracked separately each month, excluding
  fees billed for work done by other fee earners

### Lane Discipline

- All docket work stays inside the three approved practice areas:
  Corporate, Contracts, and Payment Services
- Work is delivered through approved solution frames documented in the
  canonical solution registries
- Intake that falls outside approved lanes is declined or referred

### Expertise Compounding

- Repeated delivery of approved solutions in approved lanes builds
  durable practical experience and expertise by `2026-12-31`
- Year-end review identifies where expertise has accumulated

### HillSide Oversight

- HBP-007 acts as a HillSide oversight project for the 2026 Levine Law
  operating year
- HillSide portfolio sequencing and resource decisions that touch Levine
  Law are informed by this project's program boundary

---

## Out of Scope

- Marketing build-out (governed separately)
- Matter-level operations (governed by Levine Law matter doctrine)
- Direct governance of Levine Law execution projects
- Staffing approvals inside underlying Levine Law execution work
- Practice area expansion or strategy changes (require separate ML1 approval)
- Governing HBP-010 (Rhizome) or HBP-011 (Payments Agency) — these are
  separate HillSide projects

---

## Scope Boundary Rule

Any proposed addition to scope requires ML1 approval before it is treated
as in-scope for planning or execution purposes.

Changes to the approved practice area stack, approved solution frames, or
the governing Levine Law strategy (`LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`) are outside
HBP-007's authority and require a separate approval process.
