## Client Risks

Client risks are the primary source of risk for this project. Intake volume,
matter timing, and payment are entirely client-controlled. The project has
no mechanism to force demand or accelerate payment.

| ID | Risk | P | I | Score | Mitigation | Owner |
|----|------|---|---|-------|-----------|-------|
| RC-01 | Insufficient demand — not enough client work arrives to sustain CAD 20,000 collected per month | 3 | 5 | 15 | ICP-01 / ICP-02 intake filter maintained; Levine Law marketing and referral relationships active; monthly shortfall reviewed promptly | ML1 |
| RC-02 | Client non-payment — clients do not pay invoices on time or at all, depressing cash collected regardless of work done | 4 | 5 | 20 | Retainer and payment terms enforced at intake; outstanding accounts reviewed monthly; escalation to collections where warranted | ML1 |
| RC-03 | Client concentration — too much of the collection target depends on a small number of clients; loss of one creates a material shortfall | 3 | 4 | 12 | Client mix reviewed in monthly client-quality review; over-reliance on any single client flagged | ML1 |
| RC-04 | Fee disputes — clients dispute invoices, delay payment, or demand write-downs, reducing actual collections below billed amounts | 3 | 3 | 9 | Clear engagement letters and scope definitions; billing practices reviewed for dispute-minimization | ML1 |
| RC-05 | Matter timing variability — matters open and close unpredictably; collection timing does not align with monthly targets | 5 | 3 | 15 | Monthly target is a planning rate only; cumulative year-end total is what matters; variance expected and monitored | ML1 |
| RC-06 | Client quality drift — accepting clients outside ICP-01 / ICP-02 profile generates work that does not pay well or creates disproportionate management cost | 3 | 3 | 9 | ICP filter enforced at intake; monthly client-quality review; off-strategy intake flagged in oversight pack | ML1 |
