# Business Case

## Why This Project Exists

Levine Law is a major operating business within the wider HillSide context, but
there is not yet a governed 2026 operating-program packet that makes the cash-
flow goal, monthly control set, and lane discipline explicit.

This project exists to convert that gap into a controlled initiation packet so
ML1 can decide whether a formal planning stage should begin.

## Strategic Need

The 2026 Levine Law program should not remain an informal collection of goals.
HillSide needs a structured view of:

- how Levine Law generates real cash flow in 2026
- how monthly collections, margin, capacity, and client-quality are reviewed
- how ML1-docketed fee collections are tracked separately
- which approved practice areas and approved solution frames should carry that
  work
- how the year compounds practical experience and expertise instead of just
  producing reactive one-off work
- how Levine Law resource use interacts with other HillSide projects

## Expected Value of Formalizing the Project

- stronger management clarity around the 2026 collection target
- stronger monthly control over margin, capacity, and client-quality
- better sequencing across Levine Law and the wider HillSide portfolio
- clearer distinction between approved work and off-strategy intake
- cash flow plus compounding expertise in approved legal lanes
- cleaner ML1 decision-making about what deserves planning and later execution

## Cost of Not Formalizing

- Levine Law may chase 2026 cash flow without controlled lane discipline
- time and management energy may be spread across too many unranked efforts
- HillSide may make adjacent project decisions without a clear Levine Law
  program boundary
- experience and expertise may accrete randomly instead of in approved practice
  areas and solution frames

## Core 2026 Business Thesis

The business case for HBP-007 is straightforward:

- generate cash flow
- continue to accrete experience and expertise

More specifically, the governed 2026 objective is to collect at least
CAD 240,000 of banked cash by `2026-12-31`, while tracking ML1-docketed fee
collections separately and compounding practical expertise in Levine Law's
approved work lanes.

## Approved Operating Stack for This Project

This project should not invent net-new practice lanes or strategy. It should
operate inside the existing Levine Law stack already documented elsewhere in the
repo:

- approved practice areas: Corporate, Contracts, and Payment Services
- approved solution frames: canonical solution registries under
  `02_PLAYBOOKS/CORPORATE/WORKFLOWS/solutions/`,
  `02_PLAYBOOKS/CONTRACTS/WORKFLOWS/solutions/`, and
  `02_PLAYBOOKS/FINANCIAL_SERVICES/SOLUTIONS/`
- approved strategy layer: the Levine Law Firm Strategy and Business Plan in
  `LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`, including boutique corporate-law positioning,
  ICP-01 / ICP-02 logic, and F01 -> F02 -> F03 sequencing
- changes to this approved stack require ML1 approval

## Initial Program Themes to Test in Planning

- monthly banked-cash path to CAD 240,000 by `2026-12-31`
- monthly margin, capacity, and client-quality control design
- ML1-docketed fee-collection measurement, excluding fees billed for work done
  by other fee earners
- which approved practice areas and solution frames should carry the year
- capacity and intake discipline needed to hit the target without overload
- where Levine Law should deliberately build more experience and expertise in
  approved lanes
- governance boundaries relative to HillSide-adjacent projects

## Explicitly Out of Scope

- marketing build-out
- matter-level operations
- direct governance of Levine Law execution work

## Initiation Decision Standard

The project should move to `Planning` only if ML1 believes the 2026 Levine Law
program is important enough, bounded enough, and distinct enough from routine
firm operations to justify a formal planning packet.
