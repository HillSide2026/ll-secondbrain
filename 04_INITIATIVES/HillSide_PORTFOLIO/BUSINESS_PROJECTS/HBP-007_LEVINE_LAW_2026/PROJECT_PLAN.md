# Project Plan

**Project:** Levine Law 2026
**Project ID:** HBP-007
**Stage:** Planning

---

## Execution Model

Levine Law 2026 is a demand-driven operating year. Intake, solution mix,
and payment timing are determined by client demand — not by internal
sequencing decisions. The project does not attempt to control what work
arrives or when clients pay.

The execution model is:

- operate inside approved practice areas and solution frames as demand
  arrives
- apply ICP-01 / ICP-02 intake filter to keep client quality on-strategy
- target CAD 20,000 collected per month as the working collection rate
- review actual vs target monthly and adjust intake posture if needed
- maintain the monthly oversight pack each month regardless of volume

## Approved Execution Framework

Work is delivered inside:

- approved practice areas: Corporate, Contracts, Payment Services
- approved solution frames: canonical solution registries
- approved strategy: `LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY` (ICP-01 / ICP-02,
  F01 → F02 → F03 sequencing)

No sequencing preference is applied across solutions. Demand determines
what gets delivered. Lane discipline prevents off-strategy drift.

---

## Constraints

- Intake timing, solution mix, and payment timing are client-controlled
- HBP-007 cannot accelerate or engineer collections — it can only track
  them and maintain lane discipline
- Monthly oversight is the primary execution control available to this
  project
