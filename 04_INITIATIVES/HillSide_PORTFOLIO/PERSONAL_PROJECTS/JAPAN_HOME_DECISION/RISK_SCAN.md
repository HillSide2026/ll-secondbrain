# Risk Scan

## Identified Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Decision is driven by idealization rather than disciplined criteria | Medium | High | Define explicit rationale, guardrails, and disconfirming questions |
| Cross-border legal, tax, or financing complexity is underestimated | Medium | High | Identify top diligence questions before any execution step |
| Capital or liquidity is overcommitted | Medium | High | Set hard capital-allocation and liquidity boundaries early |
| Strategic: the Japan home budget is not yet formalized inside an overall wealth plan, and the overall wealth plan is not yet usable enough to anchor the decision | High | High | Formalize the Japan home budget within `BUSINESS_PROJECTS/HBP-001_WEALTH_MANAGEMENT/` and stop treating broker-led pseudo-planning as a substitute for a real wealth plan |
| Ambiguity persists and continues draining attention | High | Medium | Force an explicit go / defer / no frame with milestones |
| The asset does not fit actual usage or long-term plans | Medium | High | Define use-case thesis and success conditions before deeper planning |

## Assumptions and Dependencies

- the Japan home question is live enough to justify dedicated project attention
- disciplined decision quality depends on surfacing the highest-risk unknowns
- the eventual answer may depend on facts not yet gathered
- the Japan home budget should be set within the overall wealth plan rather
  than on a standalone basis
- the current overall wealth-planning layer is underdeveloped and has not been
  made usable by prior broker relationships that presented themselves as
  wealth-planning support
