# Success Criteria

## Definition of Success

An explicit go, defer, or no decision is reached on the Japan home question
using written criteria, documented guardrails, and a defined next-step rule.

## Key Metrics

| KPI | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Decision status | Whether the project has reached a clear go / defer / no outcome |
| Capital-allocation ceiling | Defined maximum capital exposure ML1 is willing to tolerate |
| Liquidity guardrail | Defined minimum liquidity that cannot be impaired by the decision |
| Critical diligence gaps | Number of top-risk legal, tax, financing, or operating questions still unresolved |
| Decision-date adherence | Whether major decision milestones are reached on schedule |

## Targets

- define why a Japan home is being considered
- define explicit capital and liquidity guardrails
- formalize the Japan home budget inside the overall wealth plan
- define the top critical diligence questions
- record a clear go, defer, or no decision rather than leaving the matter ambient

## Measurement Method

- written decision memo or equivalent project note
- milestone review against stated guardrails
- periodic review of unresolved diligence questions

## Timeline for Evaluation

- weekly or biweekly during active review periods
- milestone-based review until a decision is locked

## Initiation Exit Criteria

Initiation should be considered complete when:

- the project purpose, scope, and constraints are documented
- the decision boundary is explicit
- key risks and stakeholder needs are documented
- ML1 can make a clean `Initiating -> Planning` decision
