# Project Charter

**Project:** Japan Home Decision
**Project ID:** `JAPAN_HOME_DECISION`
**Project Path:** `04_INITIATIVES/HillSide_PORTFOLIO/PERSONAL_PROJECTS/JAPAN_HOME_DECISION`
**Project Type:** Decision
**Program Branch:** Personal
**Stage:** Planning

## Project Owner

ML1

## Purpose

Turn the potential acquisition of a home in Japan into a governed decision
process with explicit rationale, guardrails, and next-step rules rather than a
loose background possibility.

## Objectives

- define the real strategic rationale for a Japan home
- determine whether the correct 2026 outcome is pursue, defer, or decline
- define acceptable capital-allocation, liquidity, and complexity boundaries
- formalize any Japan home budget within the overall wealth plan rather than
  treating it as a standalone asset decision
- identify the highest-value diligence questions that must be answered before a
  real decision

## Scope

### In Scope

- statement of purpose for why a Japan home is being considered
- decision criteria and non-negotiables
- capital-allocation and liquidity guardrails
- Japan home budget alignment with the overall wealth plan
- top legal, tax, financing, and operating questions
- decision rule and next-step logic

### Out of Scope

- entering into a purchase agreement during initiating stage
- treating lifestyle aspiration alone as sufficient diligence
- turning this packet into a live acquisition file before a decision is made

## Deliverables

- written decision frame
- decision criteria and guardrails
- Japan home budget rule aligned with the overall wealth plan
- top-risk diligence question set
- explicit go / defer / no decision path
- next-step map if pursuit is approved later

## Timeline

- planning refinement: 1-2 weeks
- planning work: 2-6 weeks after approval
- downstream acquisition work: only after a separate ML1 decision

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Owner | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Project Owner | ML1 | Decision-making, rationale definition, and final direction |
| Advisor | Optional lawyer, tax advisor, realtor, lender, or local contact | Provide issue-specific diligence support if used |

## Assumptions

- the Japan home question is real enough to affect 2026 planning
- the decision touches both lifestyle design and capital allocation
- cross-border legal, tax, financing, and operating facts could materially
  change the answer
- ML1 currently reports `CAD 900,000` in a high-interest savings account and
  `CAD 560,000` in managed equities as at `2026-04-03`, which changes the
  liquidity analysis but does not remove the need for a formal wealth-plan
  anchor

## Constraints

- limited capital and liquidity
- cross-border complexity
- incomplete on-the-ground information
- risk of idealizing the opportunity without enough diligence
- the overall wealth plan is not yet operationally usable enough to set a
  Japan home budget cleanly

## Stage Plan

1. define the strategic reason for considering a Japan home
2. define decision criteria and guardrails
3. identify the critical diligence questions
4. prepare the project for a clean planning-exit decision

## Immediate Next Actions

- define the use-case thesis for a Japan home
- define the maximum acceptable capital and complexity exposure
- formalize the Japan home budget within
  `BUSINESS_PROJECTS/HBP-001_WEALTH_MANAGEMENT/`
- identify the highest-risk unknowns
- define what would count as a real go / defer / no decision
- prepare the planning packet for ML1 review

## Risks

- the decision is romanticized rather than disciplined
- capital is overcommitted to a non-core asset
- legal or tax complexity is underestimated
- the decision remains ambiguous and keeps draining attention

## Governance

ML1-controlled stage gate. Planning and downstream execution are not authorized
until ML1 approves transition out of Planning.
