# Risk Register

| Risk | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Inconsistent habits undermine the system | Behavior | High | High | Use a simple monthly system with low-friction controls and scheduled reviews |
| Overspending erodes surplus cash flow | Cash flow | Medium | High | Implement expense tracking, spending categories, and monthly variance review |
| Income disruption slows savings and investing | Income | Medium | High | Build and maintain a 3-6 month emergency fund |
| Baseline data is incomplete or inaccurate | Measurement | Medium | High | Fix a practical opening baseline and improve data quality iteratively |
| Market volatility creates discouragement or reactive changes | Investing | Medium | Medium | Use long-term allocation logic and avoid short-term reaction rules |
| The destination stays vague and the system optimizes for generic wealth accumulation | Direction | Medium | High | Complete the vision exercise and define the desired-lifestyle target before executing |
| Basic expenses and desired lifestyle expenses are not separated | Modeling | Medium | High | Define distinct thresholds for independence and freedom |
| Net-worth goals overshadow asset-income coverage | Strategy | Medium | High | Track passive / portfolio income coverage ratios alongside net worth |
| Lifestyle inflation outruns stage progression | Lifestyle | Medium | High | Use the expense-alignment exercise and periodic vision refresh to keep spending intentional |
| Planning remains theoretical and never becomes operational | Governance | Medium | High | Require dashboard, cadence, and gate packet readiness before executing |

## Highest-Priority Planning Risks

- cash-flow opacity: without a clean baseline, the system cannot be trusted
- destination ambiguity: without a defined life target, the machine may optimize
  for numbers without meaning
- behavior drift: if the process is too heavy, it will not be sustained
- false optimism: long-horizon targets may read as real without a credible path

## Risk Response Posture

- prefer simple controls over complex financial optimization
- prefer explicit stage definitions over vague "doing better" language
- prefer asset-income coverage measures alongside net-worth growth
- prefer review discipline over constant strategy changes
- prefer long-term compounding posture over short-term performance chasing
