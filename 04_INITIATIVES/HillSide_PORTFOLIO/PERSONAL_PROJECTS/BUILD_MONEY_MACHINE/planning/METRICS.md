# Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target / Direction | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Savings rate | Percentage of income saved or invested | 15-25% minimum | Monthly |
| Monthly surplus | Income minus total expenses | Must be positive in normal months | Monthly |
| Investment contribution amount | Amount contributed to wealth-building assets | Increasing or stable positive cadence | Monthly |
| Expense ratio | Expenses as a percentage of income | Lower over time, within defined budget guardrails | Monthly |
| Basic living expense target | Monthly cost of essentials required to live safely | Explicit and current baseline for independence math | Quarterly / Annual |
| Desired lifestyle expense target | Monthly cost of the life ML1 intentionally wants to fund | Explicit and current baseline for freedom math | Quarterly / Annual |
| Passive / portfolio income coverage of basics | Passive plus portfolio income divided by basic living expenses | Move toward 100% | Monthly / Quarterly |
| Passive / portfolio income coverage of desired lifestyle | Passive plus portfolio income divided by desired lifestyle expenses | Move toward 100% over the long term | Quarterly / Annual |
| Emergency-fund coverage | Months of core expenses held in reserve | 3-6 months | Monthly / Quarterly |
| Net worth | Total assets minus total liabilities | Upward long-term trend toward 2030 / 2040 goals | Monthly / Annual |
| Highest stage currently qualified | Highest money-machine stage supported by evidence | Upward over time, without false promotion | Monthly / Quarterly |
| Review completion | Whether monthly review was completed on schedule | Must be true | Monthly |

## Metric Rule

Metrics should stay small and operational. If the dashboard becomes too broad to
support fast monthly review, it is too complex for this project.

## Measurement Notes

- income, expenses, savings, and investment contributions should come from a
  repeatable monthly close process
- basic living expenses and desired lifestyle expenses should be tracked
  separately so independence and freedom are not blurred together
- net worth should be tracked on a consistent basis rather than estimated
  differently each month
- long-horizon targets should be reviewed annually, but the monthly dashboard
  should still show whether the system is moving in the right direction

## Baseline Target Set

- save and invest at least 15-25% of income
- build and maintain a 3-6 month emergency fund
- sustain a consistent monthly surplus
- reach net worth of at least CAD 2,000,000 by `2030-12-31`
- reach net worth of at least CAD 4,000,000 by `2040-12-31`

## Five-Stage Qualification Frame

| Stage | Qualification Test | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Financial Stability | Bills are paid on time, survival debt is not increasing, and a 1-3 month cushion exists | This is the first operating threshold of the money machine |
| Financial Security | Emergency-fund coverage reaches 3-6 months and there are no immediate financial threats | Adds resilience and protection to the system |
| Financial Independence | Asset-driven cash flow can cover basic living expenses | Basic-expense coverage is the key threshold |
| Financial Freedom | Asset-driven cash flow can cover desired lifestyle expenses | Expands from necessity coverage to choice |
| Financial Abundance | Wealth continues growing beyond lifestyle needs and allows contribution, expansion, or legacy deployment | This is the highest-stage expression of the system |

## Stage Progression Rule

The project is in scope to build the system that moves through all five stages.
Early executing-stage work will likely focus on stability and security first,
while independence, freedom, and abundance remain longer-horizon outcomes that
must still be represented in the planning model.

## Second-Layer Calculation Set

- define the financial-independence number from basic living expenses
- define the freedom number from desired lifestyle expenses
- track the gap between current passive / portfolio income and both thresholds
- use the monthly review to decide whether the system is tightening control,
  building resilience, or progressing toward independence and freedom
