# Scope Statement

Project ID: `BUILD_MONEY_MACHINE`
Stage: `Planning`

## Planning Scope Objective

Turn the approved initiation concept into an execution-ready personal money
system that can reliably produce monthly surplus cash flow, build a durable
savings and investment process, and support the long-horizon net-worth targets
of CAD 2,000,000 by `2030-12-31` and CAD 4,000,000 by `2040-12-31`, while
providing a governed path through the five money-machine stages and translating
a defined lifestyle vision into financial operating rules.

## Five-Stage Progression Framework

| Stage | Working Definition | Planning Implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Financial Stability | Bills are paid on time, survival debt is avoided, and a 1-3 month cushion exists | Build the control layer first: visibility, bill discipline, spending rules, and first buffer |
| Financial Security | A 3-6 month reserve exists and immediate threats are covered | Add protection, resilience, and liquidity rules |
| Financial Independence | Asset-driven income can cover basic living expenses | Build investing and asset-cash-flow logic that targets necessity coverage |
| Financial Freedom | Asset-driven income can support the desired lifestyle | Expand from survival coverage to lifestyle choice |
| Financial Abundance | Wealth exceeds lifestyle needs and supports contribution and legacy | Add expansion, giving, and legacy capacity to the model |

## In Scope

- vision definition for life when money is no longer the primary constraint
- current-state income, expense, savings, debt, and net-worth baseline
- cash-flow tracking system and monthly dashboard structure
- expense-management and budgeting rules
- expense-alignment review between spending and stated values / vision
- emergency-fund policy and liquidity guardrails
- income-engine classification across active, portfolio, and passive / leveraged income
- savings and investment contribution system
- target asset-allocation approach for long-term wealth building
- financial-independence number and desired-lifestyle number
- gap analysis from current asset income to future basic and lifestyle coverage
- qualification rules for each of the five money-machine stages
- monthly review, quarterly adjustment, and annual evaluation cadence
- path-modeling from current baseline toward `2030-12-31` and `2040-12-31`
  net-worth targets

## Out of Scope

- high-risk speculation
- short-term trading
- get-rich-quick strategies
- replacing `BUSINESS_PROJECTS` governance for any real venture or external
  operating business
- turning this folder into a general life-admin or task-dump system
- tax, legal, or investment-product advice beyond ML1's own planning decisions

## Core Planning Outputs

- a written vision definition covering ideal day, environment, relationships,
  and work pattern once money is no longer the main constraint
- a baseline money map: income, fixed expenses, variable expenses, savings, debt,
  and net worth
- a monthly money dashboard
- a savings-rate and surplus-control system
- an investment contribution and allocation plan
- a basic-expense target, desired-lifestyle target, and "enough" model
- an income-engine map and gap-analysis model
- a five-stage qualification model for stability, security, independence,
  freedom, and abundance
- an explicit target path to the 2030 and 2040 net-worth milestones
- a repeatable review cadence that can operate in executing stage

## Boundary Rule

This project governs a personal wealth-building operating system. It can define
cash-flow discipline, savings rules, investing cadence, and review structure.
It cannot serve as the operating packet for a separate venture once the work
becomes a true HillSide business project.

## Planning -> Executing Readiness Conditions

Executing should not open until:

- the current-state baseline is documented
- the life-vision and desired-lifestyle definition are documented
- the monthly dashboard fields are fixed
- the savings and investment rules are explicit
- the emergency-fund policy is defined
- the independence number and gap model are explicit
- the five-stage qualification rules are explicit
- the long-horizon targets are tied to a credible tracking model
