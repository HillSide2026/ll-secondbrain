# Risk Scan

## Identified Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Indecision keeps housing unresolved | High | High | Force explicit criteria, milestones, and review cadence |
| Housing costs weaken cash-flow discipline | Medium | High | Define hard affordability and liquidity guardrails before choosing a path |
| Chosen option fits cost but not lifestyle or stability needs | Medium | High | Define non-negotiables and compare options against them |
| Strategic: the housing budget is not yet formalized inside an overall wealth plan, and the overall wealth plan is not yet usable enough to anchor the decision | High | High | Formalize the housing budget within `BUSINESS_PROJECTS/HBP-001_WEALTH_MANAGEMENT/` and stop treating broker-led pseudo-planning as a substitute for a real wealth plan |
| Market noise creates false urgency or unnecessary delay | Medium | Medium | Use a criteria-led process rather than reactive timing |
| Fragmented information causes weak decisions | Medium | Medium | Keep decision logic and supporting notes inside one project packet |

## Assumptions and Dependencies

- the Toronto housing issue is active enough to justify dedicated project attention
- personal cash-flow realities can be translated into usable housing guardrails
- decision quality depends on clear criteria rather than ambient intuition alone
- the housing budget should be set within the overall wealth plan rather than
  on a standalone basis
- the current overall wealth-planning layer is underdeveloped and has not been
  made usable by prior broker relationships that presented themselves as
  wealth-planning support
