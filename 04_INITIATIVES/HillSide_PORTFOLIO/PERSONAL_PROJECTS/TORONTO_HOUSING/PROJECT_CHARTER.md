# Project Charter

**Project:** Toronto Housing
**Project ID:** `TORONTO_HOUSING`
**Project Path:** `04_INITIATIVES/HillSide_PORTFOLIO/PERSONAL_PROJECTS/TORONTO_HOUSING`
**Project Type:** Decision
**Program Branch:** Personal
**Stage:** Planning

## Project Owner

ML1

## Purpose

Turn the unresolved Toronto housing question into a governed personal project
with explicit decision criteria, affordability guardrails, and a defined path
to either new owned housing in Toronto or leaving Toronto.

## Objectives

- determine whether the correct 2026 direction is to move to new owned housing
  in Toronto or leave Toronto
- define acceptable monthly cost, liquidity, and flexibility guardrails in
  light of ML1's reported `CAD 900,000` in a high-interest savings account and
  `CAD 560,000` in managed equities as at `2026-04-03`
- formalize the housing budget inside the overall wealth plan rather than
  treating housing as a standalone capital-allocation decision
- compare realistic housing paths instead of letting the issue remain ambient
- execute a realistic housing direction with next steps, including ownership in
  Toronto or leaving Toronto

## Scope

### In Scope

- ownership-in-Toronto versus leave-Toronto decision framing
- housing criteria and non-negotiables
- monthly cost, liquidity, and flexibility guardrails
- housing-budget alignment with the overall wealth plan
- option-set framing for realistic Toronto paths
- decision logic and next-step sequence
- execution-path preparation for the selected direction

### Out of Scope

- signing a lease or agreement before planning work is complete
- purchasing real estate through this initiation packet
- turning this project into a generic life-admin folder

## Deliverables

- written ownership-versus-leaving-Toronto decision frame
- decision-criteria set
- monthly cost, liquidity, and flexibility guardrails
- housing-budget rule aligned with the overall wealth plan
- option-comparison frame
- selected direction and execution-path sequence

## Timeline

- planning refinement: 1-2 weeks
- planning work: 2-6 weeks after approval
- downstream execution: only after a separate ML1 decision

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Owner | Responsibility |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Project Owner | ML1 | Decision-making, criteria definition, and final direction |
| Advisor | Optional realtor, broker, lawyer, or trusted advisor | Specific market, transaction, or judgment support if used |

## Assumptions

- Toronto housing is a live 2026 issue rather than a background preference
- housing choices affect cash flow, stability, and focus
- the core path set is already narrowed to ownership in Toronto or leaving
  Toronto
- ML1 currently reports `CAD 900,000` in a high-interest savings account and
  `CAD 560,000` in managed equities as at `2026-04-03`, which changes the
  liquidity analysis but does not eliminate the need for guardrails

## Constraints

- affordability and liquidity limits
- market conditions and timing noise
- limited time and attention
- uncertainty about the best fit between stability, flexibility, and cost
- the overall wealth plan is not yet operationally usable enough to set the
  housing budget cleanly

## Stage Plan

1. define the ownership-versus-leaving-Toronto decision frame
2. define monthly cost, liquidity, and flexibility guardrails
3. compare the realistic paths inside that narrowed option set
4. prepare the project for execution of the selected direction

## Immediate Next Actions

- document the current Toronto housing pain points and constraints
- define the target monthly housing-cost envelope for owned Toronto housing
- define the liquidity and flexibility rules that must survive either path
- formalize the housing budget within
  `BUSINESS_PROJECTS/HBP-001_WEALTH_MANAGEMENT/`
- compare ownership in Toronto versus leaving Toronto
- prepare the project for execution of the selected direction

## Risks

- indecision keeps the issue unresolved
- cash or liquidity is overcommitted to the wrong housing path
- the selected option solves cost but not stability or lifestyle fit
- market noise creates false urgency or delay

## Governance

ML1-controlled stage gate. Planning and downstream execution are not authorized
until ML1 approves transition out of Planning.
