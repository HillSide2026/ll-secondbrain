# Problem Statement

## Problem Description

Toronto housing remains an unresolved personal operating issue, which creates
avoidable uncertainty around stability, cost structure, and day-to-day focus.

## Current State

- there is no standalone project governing the Toronto housing question
- the core 2026 choice is to move to new owned housing in Toronto or leave
  Toronto
- housing criteria and tradeoffs are not yet fixed in one place
- monthly cost, liquidity, and flexibility constraints are not yet translated
  into a disciplined decision frame

## Impact of the Problem

- ambient decision drag and reduced focus
- uncertainty in personal cash-flow planning
- higher risk of reactive or poorly framed housing decisions
- weaker alignment between living situation and 2026 priorities

## Root Causes

- housing has not previously been governed as its own project
- decision criteria and non-negotiables have not been made explicit
- the issue competes with other priorities without a dedicated review lane

## Why This Matters

Housing is not a background detail. It affects stability, cost, attention, and
the quality of the broader 2026 operating environment. This project exists to
turn the ownership-in-Toronto versus leave-Toronto question into a governed
decision and execution path.
