# 17409052 Canada Inc EMI and Partnership Options

## Purpose

This document compares the currently identified EMI and partnership paths for
`17409052 Canada Inc`.

Sponsor-bank search is tracked separately under Ontario MSB Corp / Locarno
(`1001494374 Ontario Corp`). This document does not treat the sponsor-bank
search as a 174 workstream.

The question is not whether the entity has interesting relationship leads.

The question is whether any relationship improves 174 as a sale asset by making
the payments infrastructure package more buyer-readable, transferable, and
diligence-ready.

## Governing Standard

174 value increases only when a relationship becomes buyer-readable
infrastructure.

Useful infrastructure should provide, or credibly evidence, some combination of:

- direct or clearly visible EMI relationship;
- payment-account capability;
- inbound and outbound payment capability;
- domestic and cross-border payment capability;
- safeguarding visibility;
- reconciliation feeds;
- documented onboarding requirements;
- clear fees, minimums, and limits;
- no exclusivity;
- no punitive termination rights;
- assignability or clean change-of-control treatment; and
- ability for a buyer to inherit or replace the relationship without destroying
  sale value.

Vague partnership language does not itself improve the 174 sale package.

## Interpretation

### EUR 12,000 EMI Offer

The EUR 12,000 EMI offer is potentially the strongest 174-specific path because
it may convert the entity from a federal MSB with a concept note into a federal
MSB with a priced EMI access path.

That can improve sale value if the offer produces a diligence artifact that a
buyer can understand.

The payment should not be treated as ordinary software spend.

It should be treated as an option premium for infrastructure credibility.

The premium is worth considering only if the terms preserve optionality. Higher
cost may be acceptable where it buys speed, credibility, and transferability.
Higher cost is not acceptable if it buys lock-in, exclusivity, hidden reseller
access, or a relationship that must be restarted on sale.

### Kwiikpay Referral Agreement

The signed Kwiikpay referral agreement belongs first to `17513721 Canada Inc`.

It may be useful for 175's commercial channel strategy.

It does not automatically improve 174.

For 174, the referral agreement matters only if it can support the sale package
by showing a real route to payment services, client referrals, or future
infrastructure access. If the agreement is only an introduction or referral
economics document, it should remain outside the 174 value stack.

### Kwiikpay Undefined Partnership

The undefined Kwiikpay partnership is not yet an asset.

It is a diligence question.

The key issue is whether Kwiikpay is offering:

- referral economics;
- white-label or software access;
- program-manager access;
- direct or indirect EMI access;
- payment-account capability;
- client onboarding infrastructure;
- safeguarding visibility; or
- some combination of these.

Until the role is defined in writing, Kwiikpay should not be counted as part of
174's completion gate.

For preferred 174 sale-readiness, Kwiikpay must be established as a 174
infrastructure path. A 175 referral agreement alone is not enough.

## Decision Questions

Before ML1 approves payment or reliance, each path should answer:

1. Which entity is contracting: `17409052 Canada Inc` or `17513721 Canada Inc`?
2. Is the relationship direct EMI access, reseller access, referral economics,
   software access, or only loose partnership language?
3. What payment flows are supported?
4. Who onboards clients?
5. Who holds or safeguards funds?
6. What bank or safeguarding structure is visible?
7. What API, reporting, and reconciliation feeds are available?
8. What setup fees, monthly fees, minimums, and limits apply?
9. Is there exclusivity?
10. Can the arrangement be assigned, transferred, terminated, or continued after
    a sale or change of control?
11. Would a buyer treat the relationship as useful infrastructure or as a
    relationship that must be rebuilt?

## Recommended Near-Term Posture

Do not pay the EUR 12,000 EMI fee until the provider delivers enough written
terms to test sale value.

Use the EUR 12,000 offer as negotiation leverage:

- ask Kwiikpay to define the partnership in writing;
- ask the EMI provider to clarify transferability, exclusivity, and
  change-of-control treatment;
- compare the EMI offer against at least one other EMI candidate if available;
  and
- preserve the ability for a buyer to inherit or replace the relationship.

Kwiikpay should be treated as useful but unproven for 174 until the relationship
is defined beyond referral language.

## Current Working Conclusion

The EUR 12,000 EMI offer is the only currently identified option that may
directly strengthen the 174 sale package.

The Kwiikpay referral agreement is real, but currently belongs to 175's
commercial channel rather than 174's infrastructure stack.

The Kwiikpay partnership may become relevant to 174, but only after its legal,
commercial, technical, and regulatory role is documented.

Sponsor-bank work should remain with Ontario MSB Corp / Locarno unless ML1
separately creates and approves a 174 sponsor-bank track.
