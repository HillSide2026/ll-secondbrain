# Locarno Sponsor-Bank Search

## Purpose

This document tracks the sponsor-bank search for `1001494374 Ontario Corp`
within Project Locarno.

The sponsor-bank search belongs to Ontario MSB Corp / Locarno, not to
`17409052 Canada Inc` / Granville.

## Asset Boundary

| Entity | Role | Bank / EMI posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `1001494374 Ontario Corp` | Ontario MSB Corp / Locarno | Sponsor-bank search home. Direct EMI plus direct sponsor-bank path. |
| `17409052 Canada Inc` | Federal MSB / Granville | EMI-led value-stack path. No current sponsor-bank search unless ML1 separately approves one. |
| `17513721 Canada Inc` | Matthew Holdings | Parent / commercial channel entity. Kwiikpay referral agreement currently sits here unless moved or extended. |

## Search Objective

The goal is to identify a sponsor-bank path that can make Locarno more than a
pure MSB shell.

Current state: no concrete sponsor-bank prospect has been identified.

The target is a directly referenceable sponsor-bank relationship that improves:

- buyer diligence;
- valuation;
- durability;
- regulator confidence;
- EMI portability;
- acquirer attractiveness; and
- banking dependency transparency.

## Target Structure

```text
1001494374 Ontario Corp
   ->
Direct EMI agreement
   ->
Direct sponsor-bank sponsorship agreement
   ->
Safeguarding / settlement accounts
```

The relationship does not need to begin with a Tier 1 bank.

It must be sufficiently direct, documented, and buyer-readable.

## Target Sponsor-Bank Profile

Preferred attributes:

- sponsor-friendly;
- fintech-accommodative;
- operationally flexible;
- willing to work with MSB / PSP infrastructure;
- comfortable with EMI-aligned safeguarding;
- capable of producing useful diligence correspondence; and
- willing to discuss transferability, change of control, and migration.

Potential categories to research:

- sponsor-friendly UK or European banks;
- smaller Baltic or Central European sponsor banks;
- EMI-aligned safeguarding banks;
- EMI-bank hybrid platforms; and
- fintech infrastructure providers with directly referenceable bank partners.

Reference examples already noted in the Locarno strategy include:

- Solaris;
- ClearBank;
- Banking Circle;
- B4B;
- Modulr-partner bank structures; and
- smaller Baltic or Central European sponsor banks.

Additional reference example:

- Sygnum, as a global digital asset banking group founded on Swiss and
  Singapore heritage.

These are not current prospects. They are reference points for the kind of
institution or structure that may fit the Locarno sponsor-bank search.

## Diligence Evidence Needed

The sponsor-bank search should produce evidence that can be placed into a buyer
or bank diligence folder.

Minimum useful evidence:

- named bank or bank-path identified;
- written counterparty correspondence;
- description of supported flows;
- safeguarding / settlement account structure;
- account naming and visibility;
- reconciliation reporting;
- underwriting requirements;
- AML / sanctions diligence expectations;
- crypto exposure position, if any;
- customer geography limits;
- transaction profile limits;
- onboarding sequence;
- estimated fees, minimums, and timeline;
- change-of-control treatment;
- assignment or transition treatment; and
- exit / migration rights.

## Questions for Sponsor-Bank Candidates

1. Would the bank contract directly with `1001494374 Ontario Corp`, or only
   through an EMI / program manager?
2. If indirect, can the bank relationship still be named, referenced, and
   diligence-visible?
3. What customer geographies and transaction types are acceptable?
4. What crypto or stablecoin exposure is prohibited, restricted, or acceptable?
5. Who holds customer funds?
6. How are funds safeguarded or segregated?
7. What reconciliation and reporting are available?
8. What AML, sanctions, transaction-monitoring, and governance documents are
   required before onboarding?
9. What would trigger termination?
10. Can the relationship survive sale, assignment, or change of control?
11. Can a buyer replace the bank without rebuilding the entire platform?

## ML1 Decision Posture

Sponsor-bank outreach should be advanced only if ML1 is pursuing the Locarno
infrastructure-asset track.

If ML1 is pursuing only the pure-shell sale, the sponsor-bank search should be
kept light and should not create cost, complexity, or diligence friction.

If ML1 is pursuing the infrastructure-asset track, the sponsor-bank search is a
core workstream and should be run in parallel with the direct EMI workstream.

## Current Working Conclusion

Locarno's sponsor-bank search is a value-building workstream for
`1001494374 Ontario Corp`.

It is not part of the current 174 / Granville EMI option analysis.

There is currently no concrete sponsor-bank prospect. The next step is to
convert the market-reference list into actual outreach targets or to pause the
search if ML1 is prioritizing the pure-shell sale.

The sponsor-bank relationship becomes valuable only when it is direct or
directly referenceable, documented, and capable of being understood by a buyer
as part of a transferable regulated payments infrastructure layer.
