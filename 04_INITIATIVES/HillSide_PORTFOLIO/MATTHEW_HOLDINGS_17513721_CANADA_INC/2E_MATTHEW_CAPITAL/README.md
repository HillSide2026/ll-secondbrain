# Matthew Capital

Matthew Capital is a planned new holding corporation in the broader HillSide
structure. It is part of the approved Q4 Target Restructuring core scenario
for Q4 2026.

It is separate from Kendal Securities. Kendal Securities is the planned
dealer-registration entity. Matthew Capital is the planned holding corporation.

It is not yet incorporated.

---

## Trigger

Matthew Capital will be incorporated before Kendal Securities is capitalized.

The plan is tied to the sale of `17409052 Canada Inc` (174 / Granville).

---

## Role

Matthew Capital will acquire shares of `17513721 Canada Inc` (Matthew Holdings
/ 175).

After Granville is sold and proceeds flow into 175, 175 will distribute the
intended capital to Matthew Capital by dividend or other approved corporate
mechanism. Matthew Capital will then capitalize Kendal Securities directly.

---

## Relationship to Kendal Securities

Kendal Securities will be a direct subsidiary of Matthew Capital and will hold
funds invested by Matthew Capital to support dealer-registration criteria.
Kendal Securities is expected to pursue exempt market dealer registration, with
restricted dealer remaining possible.

Matthew Capital will not be the dealer-registration entity.

---

## Q4 Target Restructuring Sequence

1. Incorporate Matthew Capital.
2. Matthew Capital acquires shares of 175.
3. Sell Granville.
4. Sale proceeds flow into 175.
5. 175 distributes intended capital to Matthew Capital by dividend or other
   approved corporate mechanism.
6. Incorporate Kendal Securities as a direct subsidiary of Matthew Capital.
7. Matthew Capital capitalizes Kendal Securities.
8. Kendal Securities holds the funds for dealer-registration criteria and
   proceeds toward EMD registration, with restricted dealer remaining possible.

---

## 175-Level Assessment Parameters

Upon sale of 174 and / or Ontario MSB Corp, and before approval and
implementation of the Q4 Target Restructuring, 175 must assess whether capital
is better used for Kendal Securities, passive investing through Matthew
Capital, or strengthening 175's own enterprise value.

The assessment parameters are:

- target gross revenue of `EUR 100,000`;
- gross revenue may come from the sale of 174 alone or from the combined sale
  of 174 and Ontario MSB Corp; and
- the investment case for Kendal Securities must be weighed against passive
  investing alternatives through Matthew Capital, including private equity or
  real estate opportunities, and against strengthening 175's own enterprise
  value.
