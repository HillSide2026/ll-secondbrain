# LL Finance Invariants

## Purpose

Record durable finance truths and constraints for Levine Law.

Invariants are not tactical policies. They are facts or principles that should remain true across financing, banking, asset/liability, acquisition, and shareholder-distribution decisions.

## Invariants

### FI-01 — Payment First

Payment security and collection timing are primary finance facts, not afterthoughts.

Revenue that is unpaid, unsecured, delayed, or difficult to collect has lower finance value than revenue that is paid first, secured, or reliably collectible.

### FI-02 — Trust Funds Are Not Operating Cash

Funds held in trust may reduce collection risk, but they are not operating cash until professional and accounting conditions for transfer are satisfied.

Finance analysis must distinguish trust balances from available operating liquidity.

### FI-03 — Levine Law Finances WIP and AR Until Cash Is Collected

When work is performed before payment, Levine Law is financing the client through WIP and AR.

This creates working-capital strain even if the work is eventually billable or profitable.

### FI-04 — Liquidity Is a Survival Constraint

Profitability does not guarantee survival. The firm must be able to meet obligations when due.

Liquidity failure can occur even in a profitable business if cash arrives too late or obligations are too fixed.

### FI-05 — Optionality Has Value

Cash reserves, clean banking relationships, unused credit, low fixed obligations, and flexible capacity create option value.

Option value matters for downturns, opportunities, acquisitions, lawyer recruitment, property decisions, and strategic pivots.

### FI-06 — Levine Law Is a Portfolio

The firm is a portfolio of matters, clients, fee earners, capital commitments, liabilities, and cash claims.

Finance must consider concentration, volatility, correlation, collectability, margin, timing, and resilience, not only aggregate revenue.
