## Analytical Notes

**Owner salary as % of revenue:**
- At $200k revenue: 36% of revenue to owner salary
- At $225k revenue: 32%
- At $250k revenue: 29%
Owner salary is fixed at $72k regardless of scenario — the firm does not break even on owner compensation until revenue exceeds approximately $175k–$180k (owner salary alone + overhead floor).

**Client acquisition budget vs. current F01 spend:**
- F01 (Google Ads) currently runs at ~$1,500/month = $18,000/year
- Scenario 1 acquisition ceiling is $16,000 — F01 spend alone is at or above the Scenario 1 acquisition budget ceiling
- Scenario 2–3 ceilings ($18,000–$25,000) provide some room for F02 build costs and referral network development alongside F01

**Margin:**
- Scenario 1 margin ($20k–$30k) is thin relative to owner salary risk
- Scenario 3 margin ($35k–$50k) is the more sustainable target if the firm is to build reserves or invest in staffing (setter, senior lawyer)

**Key open items (not in source document):**
- Actual 2025 revenue vs. these scenario targets — needed to calibrate which scenario is realistic
- F02 build costs (landing page, GHL config) — likely absorbed within client acquisition budget
- Setter and senior lawyer costs — would increase Direct Cost / COGS materially; not yet modeled
