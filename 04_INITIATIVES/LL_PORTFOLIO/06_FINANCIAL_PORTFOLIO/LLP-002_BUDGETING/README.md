# LLP-002_BUDGETING — Budgeting and Scenario Management

## Purpose

Govern Levine Law's budget scenarios and financial-constraint modeling so ML1 can review revenue, spend, margin, and operating assumptions without turning the packet into price-setting or execution authority.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Budget scenarios and assumption framing
- Revenue, cost, acquisition, overhead, and margin modeling
- Advisory financial constraints used by ML1 for review
- Links between budget artifacts and downstream strategy consumers

### Out of Scope

- Accounting fact maintenance
- Price-setting, discount approvals, or profitability declarations
- Auto-applying budget outputs into operations or sales

## Current Foundation

The 2026 budget model starts from the approved **margin-calibrated base case**:

- required revenue: approximately `CAD 239,459`;
- target profit margin: `20%`;
- projected base-case profit: approximately `CAD 47,892`;
- annual operating control target: `CAD 250,000` collected revenue.

The scenario matrix, margin-calibrated base case, and Game 2026 scorecards are
different views of one budgeting system, not unrelated budgets.

The approved base case remains the operative baseline unless ML1 records a
specific reason, explanation, and justification for using a different baseline.
Sensitivity scenarios are review tools; they do not replace the approved
baseline by implication.
