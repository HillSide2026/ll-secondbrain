# Risk Scan

Project ID: LLP-008
Project Path: 03_FIRM_OPERATIONS/LLP-008_CLOSING

## Top 5 Risks
1. Strategic risk: the project may define a closing model that does not match how fulfillment actually ends in practice.
2. Scope risk: closing may be confused with legal completion, archive handling, or broader matter-offboarding work that belongs elsewhere.
3. Schedule risk: closure evidence requirements may be harder to define because multiple systems and workflows contribute to closeout.
4. Budget risk: closeout controls may become too manual or too heavyweight for routine use.
5. Financial risk: weak closure controls may allow trust, billing, or record issues to persist after work is treated as closed.

## Key Assumptions
- Fulfillment closing can be defined as an administrative end-state separate from substantive legal completion.
- The required closeout evidence can be gathered from existing workflows and systems.
- ML1 wants an explicit closure gate rather than implicit closeout behavior.
- Archive handling can remain downstream from fulfillment closing rather than being merged into it.
- The closing packet can stay operational and bounded without becoming a catch-all for every end-of-matter task.
