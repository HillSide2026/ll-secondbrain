# Business Case

Project ID: LLP-032
Project Name: Corporate Clerk

## Opportunity

Levine Law may eventually be able to create a safer and more economic delivery
layer by staffing a reliable, reasonably well-trained corporate clerk.

The open question is not just whether the role sounds useful. The real
questions are:

- what work would justify the role
- what supervision would make it safe
- and when the firm should actually staff it

## Strategic Rationale

- repeated lower-level corporate execution work may be stratifiable
- ML1 time should not stay indefinitely tied to work that does not require the
  same level of judgment
- a clerk role may be a prerequisite for scalable recurring corporate services
- staffing too early or without a bounded role would create avoidable risk

## Value Thesis

If LLP-032 succeeds, Levine Law should gain:

- a real corporate-clerk role hypothesis
- explicit task and supervision boundaries
- a timing and activation logic rather than ad hoc hiring pressure
- better alignment with entity-management and supervision projects

## Constraints

- this stage does not authorize hiring
- this stage does not authorize task delegation
- this stage does not authorize role doctrine to become live operations
- planning should not assume the role is economically justified without a real
  test
- any future staffing decision remains ML1-only

## Initial Recommendation

Proceed to Planning only if ML1 wants a real staffing decision packet rather
than a generic role concept.

Planning should focus on:

- role architecture
- timing and activation thresholds
- training and supervision requirements
- economics and risk posture

## Decision Standard

Proceed beyond initiation only if ML1 concludes that:

- the role question is real and timely
- the likely work body is large enough to justify deeper design
- the project can stay bounded to staffing logic rather than collapsing into
  premature HR or operations implementation
- the likely value of role clarity exceeds the cost of further project work
