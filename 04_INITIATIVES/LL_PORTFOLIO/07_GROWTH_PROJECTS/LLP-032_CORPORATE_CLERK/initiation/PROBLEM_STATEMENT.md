# Problem Statement

Levine Law does not yet have a reliable, reasonably well-trained corporate
clerk capability.

As a result, ML1 remains responsible for a large share of administrative,
records, deadline, and lower-level corporate execution work that may not all
require lawyer judgment but still requires quality control.

The firm therefore faces a real staffing question:

- should a corporate clerk role be added
- what work should sit with that role
- what training and supervision would make it reliable
- and when would the workload justify staffing it

This creates five practical problems:

- ML1 time is consumed by work that may be stratifiable
- there is no clear activation trigger for staffing the role
- quality risk rises if delegation happens informally instead of through a
  bounded role design
- the economics of hiring versus continuing to absorb the work are unclear
- entity-management ambitions become harder to scale safely without a trained
  non-lawyer execution layer

Without a disciplined staffing and timing framework, Levine Law risks hiring
too early, hiring too late, or delegating the wrong work to the wrong role.
