# Risk Scan

## Top 5 Risks
1. Strategic risk: the project may be misread as performance surveillance rather than supervision support.
2. Scope risk: the project may drift into broader people-management or operational design instead of staying focused on supervision controls.
3. Strategic risk: the framework may prioritize what is easy to count instead of what is actually decision-useful.
4. Financial risk: hidden supervision burden may continue to distort staffing and leverage assumptions if the framework is too weak.
5. Schedule risk: delay in this project may stall dependent staffing and delegation projects.

## Key Assumptions
- partner supervision can be framed as support and control rather than
  enforcement
- the framework can stay subordinate to judgment and context
- the project can stay narrow enough to help dependent projects without trying
  to solve all performance management
- ML1 wants supervision capacity to be made explicit before leverage expands
- better visibility should improve planning decisions rather than add noise
