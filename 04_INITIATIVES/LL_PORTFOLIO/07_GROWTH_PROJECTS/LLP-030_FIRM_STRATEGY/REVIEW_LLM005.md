## 1. Channel Sequencing Risk

**Stated sequence:** F01 funds operations → F02 launches Q2 and grows to 40–50% of revenue by Q4 → F03 activates Q3 and grows to 20–30% by Q4.

**Q2 F02 launch: conditional, not assured.**
All preconditions are open items: Health Check scope defined, price confirmed, landing page live, GHL intake configured, accountant outreach begun. F03 is explicitly gated on the Health Check landing page being live. A six-week slip in F02 build compresses the entire channel ramp by one quarter.

**Q3 F03 activation: optimistic.**
Blog/SEO does not generate organic traction in the same quarter it activates. The Q3 F03 contribution target of 10–20% of revenue is only achievable through direct outreach or existing network, not SEO. These are different resource demands and the plan does not distinguish between them.

**The Q4 F02 math requires the accountant network to be active by Q2–Q3.** To produce $9K–$12K/month from F02 in Q4 requires 4–6 Health Checks per month plus downstream fractional counsel conversions. Accountant referral networks require two to four months of relationship-building before producing volume. The plan has no Q1 accountant outreach milestone, so the Q2 launch cannot create Q3 referral flow.

| Risk | Severity | Description |
|---|---|---|
| F02 build delays block F03 activation | High | F03 Blog/SEO gate is explicitly the Health Check landing page. Any F02 delay cascades directly to F03. |
| Accountant network requires lead time not modeled | High | No Q1 outreach planned. Q2 launch cannot produce Q3 volume without prior relationship work. |
| F03 SEO/outreach ambiguity | Medium | SEO does not convert in quarter of activation. Q3 F03 revenue requires outreach mode, not SEO mode. |
| Revenue gap in F01-to-F02 transition | Medium | If F01 declines from 70–80% to 40–50% before F02 is producing at 20–30%, there is a total revenue shortfall in Q3. |

## 2. Capacity Bottleneck Analysis

**The ceiling:** ML1 ~20 billable hours/week, frequently breached. Growth exceeding this ceiling is not authorized before staffing.

**Does the revenue ramp require exceeding the ceiling before staffing is authorized? Yes.**

Working through the Q3–Q4 math:
- Q3 midpoint target: $21K/month. F01 at 40–50% = $8.4K–$10.5K. F02 at 20–30% = $4.2K–$6.3K. F03 at 10–20% = $2.1K–$4.2K. To produce $21K total at the top of this range requires all three channels performing at their targets simultaneously — which puts ML1 at or above the stated 20h/week limit.

**The structural contradiction:** The plan prohibits growth that exceeds the ceiling before staffing. The Setter trigger is sustained $20K+/month. The Senior Lawyer trigger is $300K+ annualized. The plan therefore authorizes no staffing relief until the revenue levels that require exceeding the ceiling are already achieved and sustained. ML1 must breach the ceiling first, sustain it for [X] consecutive months (undefined), and only then initiate a hire that takes additional time to become operational. The ceiling violation is a precondition for relief, not a trigger to prevent it.

## 3. WIP and Load

By Q2, ML1 must simultaneously: run F01 production at full throughput (still 70–80% of revenue), build the F02 entry offer, configure GHL, begin accountant outreach, and seed F03 authority content. By Q3, all three channels are in active production or ramp simultaneously.

**Can these run without one starving the others? No — not at full execution fidelity.**

The F02 build and F03 content work is non-billable. A conservative estimate for Q2 non-billable F02 work alone is 20–30 hours. At 20h/week billable, any non-billable investment trades directly against revenue in the same week. The plan does not quantify this demand anywhere.

**The starvation risk is asymmetric.** F01 will not be starved — it is the survival channel. F03 will be starved first because it has the longest payback horizon and no immediate revenue pressure. The realistic outcome is that F03 operates two to four months behind its stated activation timeline, with Q4 rather than Q3 as the effective start point.

## 4. Resource Collision

Every channel-building and BD task in Q2–Q3 requires ML1's legal judgment — it cannot be delegated to contractors.

| Demand | When | Hours (est.) |
|---|---|---|
| F02 Health Check offer definition and scope | Q2 concentrated | 10–15h non-billable |
| F02 accountant referral outreach | Q2 ongoing into Q3 | 4–8h/month non-billable |
| F03 authority content production | Q3 ongoing | 2–4h/month per piece |
| F03 payments entry offer definition | Q3 | 5–10h non-billable |
| Template and workflow documentation (delegation prep) | Q2–Q3 | 8–12h non-billable |

These demands concentrate in Q2–Q3 — exactly when billable throughput must also be increasing from $11K to $21K/month. The collision is not between two different resource pools. It is ML1's legal judgment being demanded by delivery, BD, and marketing simultaneously, with no slack and no relief mechanism.

## 5. Verdict

**The sequencing logic is sound. The capacity model is not.**

F01 → F02 → F03 is the right architecture. The strategic exclusions are well-drawn. The problem is the implicit assumption that ML1 can execute three phases of channel transition at full billable throughput, with non-billable build investment layered on top, inside a ceiling the plan acknowledges is frequently breached, with staffing relief only arriving after the breach is already sustained for an undefined period.

**Five specific findings requiring ML1 attention:**

1. **FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is a stub.** The revenue ramp is a target, not a forecast. No validated average matter value for F01. No conversion rate data. No P&L. No break-even. The capacity analysis above works from stated targets, not modeled assumptions.

2. **The Setter trigger [X] is undefined.** The consecutive-months threshold for Setter activation is blank. If [X] = 2 months and Q3 hits $20K+, Setter activation begins Q3 and could be operational by Q4. If [X] = 4 months, it does not begin until Q4 and does not help in 2026. This determines when ML1 gets relief from intake load.

3. **No Q1 accountant outreach milestone.** Q1 milestones are entirely internal. A Q2 F02 launch via accountant referral requires relationships with a two-to-four month build time. Without Q1 outreach, Q2 launch produces Q3 volume at the earliest, making Q3 F02 contribution targets difficult. A specific Q1 milestone should be: begin introductory accountant contact list and first outreach.

4. **Non-billable hour demand is not quantified.** The plan has no estimate of how many hours F02 build, F02 BD, F03 content, and template work will consume. A rough estimate suggests 30–50 non-billable hours required in Q2 alone. Without this, the capacity model cannot close.

5. **No downside scenario for the F01-to-F02 revenue transition.** If F02 is six weeks behind and F03 is not yet contributing, Q3 revenue may fall short of $20K–$22K even with F01 running at plan. A downside scenario for the transition gap should be modeled before financial projections are treated as operating targets.

---

The target is achievable. The plan needs a real financial model, a defined Setter trigger, Q1 accountant outreach initiated, and a quantified non-billable hour budget before it can be used as an operating plan rather than a strategic sketch.
