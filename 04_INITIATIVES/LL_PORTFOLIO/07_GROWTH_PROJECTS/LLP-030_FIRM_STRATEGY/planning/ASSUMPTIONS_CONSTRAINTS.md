# Assumptions and Constraints

Project ID: `LLP-030`
Project Path: `04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/07_GROWTH_PROJECTS/LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`
Stage: `Planning`

## Core Assumptions

1. The current Levine Law planning baseline remains `CAD 240,000` of annual
   revenue supporting `CAD 80,000` of owner compensation.
2. `F01` remains the present funding source, but it is not the intended future
   portfolio mix.
3. The real problem with `F01` is profitability and fit quality, not simple
   absence of revenue.
4. `F02` and `F03` remain strategic target channels whose roles are defined in
   LLP-030 even where their real-world operating proof is incomplete.
5. Andersen work is credited to Levine Law in the 2026 operating picture even
   though fee receipts land in `17513721 Canada Inc`.
6. First delivery leverage should be modeled around an associate-lawyer layer,
   with any senior-lawyer layer treated as a later and separate scenario.
7. `2026` remains a no-core-hire year unless ML1 changes the business-plan
   posture explicitly.
8. The current strategy, business plan, and financial-model documents are
   substantive work product and should be refined rather than discarded.

## Hard Constraints

1. LLP-030 does not authorize execution by itself.
2. LLP-030 must remain a strategic control project, not a generic planning
   archive.
3. LLP-030 cannot contradict the approved `LLP-002` budget baseline without
   explicit ML1 direction.
4. LLP-030 cannot silently rewrite `LLP-025`, `LLP-011`, `LLP-012`, or
   `LLP-033`; those packets must be aligned through explicit review.
5. LLP-030 cannot assume Andersen cash and Andersen operating credit are the
   same thing; the cross-entity treatment must remain explicit.
6. LLP-030 cannot treat the current `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` stub as solved merely
   because revenue targets exist in the business plan.
7. Hiring logic in LLP-030 must remain subordinate to the current `2026`
   no-core-hire posture unless ML1 changes that posture.

## Current Open Planning Limits

- exact profitability thresholds for channel viability are not yet fully
  modeled
- the financial model still needs more validation on matter-value and
  conversion assumptions
- the execution status of `F02` and `F03` is still partly a planning claim
  rather than a fully normalized operating fact
- the cross-project alignment pass is not yet complete

## Implication

LLP-030 can still function as an active planning-stage control project, but it
must present its remaining uncertainty honestly rather than hiding it behind
high-level strategy language.
