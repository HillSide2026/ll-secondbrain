# Levine Law - Firm Architecture

## Status

Draft architecture scaffold for translating the Levine Law mission, vision,
values, and 2030 vision into an operating firm design.

This artifact belongs to `LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`. It is upstream of business

## 1. Purpose

**Definition:** The fundamental reason Levine Law exists beyond simply making a
profit.

Canonical detail: `PURPOSE.md`.

Levine Law exists to make serious business operators stronger, clearer, and
better protected by turning legal complexity into structured business judgment.

---

## 2. Vision

**Definition:** The long-term picture of what the world looks like if Levine
Law's purpose is fulfilled.

Levine Law's vision is expressed as a year-by-year ladder from 2026 to 2030.
Each year describes the firm the system is trying to build toward.

### Vision: 2026

Levine Law builds the controlled platform and launches the specialty layer.

By the end of 2026, Levine Law has established itself as an emerging Tier 5
specialist boutique in Ontario payments-services legal work. F01 continues to
fund operations, F03 becomes the primary specialist growth hypothesis, and F02
remains a light referral or intake channel unless the evidence shows better
economics.

The firm proves that serious legal work can be delivered through a more
controlled model: defined intake, scoped engagements, stronger client filters,
better collections discipline, and early standardized solutions for payments,
MSB, AML, and corporate-governance work.

### Vision: 2027

Levine Law consolidates specialist authority and deepens the specialist book.

By the end of 2027, the firm is a recognized reference point for defined
payments/MSB/RPAA/AML matters while remaining a disciplined Tier 5 boutique.
F03 produces repeat matters, referral flow, and the first durable ongoing
specialist counsel relationships.

The firm narrows the matter mix, formalizes delivery standards, and activates
leverage only if the bottleneck is persistent and the economics justify it.
Quality, client fit, and capacity discipline remain more important than volume.

### Vision: 2028

Levine Law converts specialist credibility into a larger, stronger Tier 5
platform.

By the end of 2028, F02 and F03 are both repeatable channels, F01 is secondary
or optional, and the firm has deeper authority inside its niche. The practice
has documented systems, standardized solutions, and enough delivery maturity to
decide whether selective leverage can be added without breaking capacity
discipline, niche positioning, or quality.

This is the year the firm resolves the identity question: controlled boutique
or early platform.

### Vision: 2029

Levine Law becomes a systematized specialist firm with client relationships
that are no longer entirely Matthew-personal.

By the end of 2029, the firm has a functioning delivery spine, a clearer
subscription base, stronger client renewal patterns, and enough management
visibility to run through systems rather than urgency. Standardized regulatory
and corporate solutions are mature enough to support supervised delivery by
others while preserving legal judgment.

The firm is known not only for Matthew's expertise, but for a recognizable
Levine Law method: clear rules, precise scopes, practical tools, reliable
delivery, and calm execution.

### Vision: 2030

Levine Law is a respected Tier 5 fintech corporate law boutique known for
subscription-based corporate and regulatory solutions, trusted long-term client
relationships, and practical digital tools.

The firm is recognized by serious fintech operators, entrepreneurs, executives,
investors, and adjacent advisors as a quality boutique offering Bay Street
discipline without Bay Street detachment.

By the end of 2030, serious fintech and regulated-business operators use
Levine Law as part of their business infrastructure, not only as emergency
counsel. Clients have visible corporate records, contracts, obligations,
renewal calendars, and regulatory next steps. The firm's FINTRAC, RPAA, CARF,
AML, and corporate-governance solutions are standardized enough to deliver
quickly and precise enough to deserve trust.

Most new work comes from renewals, referrals, and advisor confidence. The firm
is respected for discretion, admired for clarity, and remembered for results.
The business is systematized enough that quality does not depend entirely on
Matthew's memory, urgency, or personal intervention.

## 3. Mission

**Definition:** What Levine Law does now, who it serves, and how it creates
value.

Levine Law helps serious fintech and growth-stage business operators structure,
govern, protect, and scale their companies through precise corporate and
regulatory legal services, subscription-based counsel, and practical systems
that turn legal complexity into business clarity.

The firm serves entrepreneurs, executives, investors, and adjacent advisors who
need serious legal judgment, clear delivery, and commercially useful answers.

Levine Law creates value by:

- identifying the rules that matter before they become constraints
- translating corporate, contract, governance, AML, payments, and regulatory
  issues into clear business decisions
- delivering scoped legal products, subscriptions, and advisory relationships
  with transparent expectations
- building repeatable systems so clients receive reliable service, not
  personality-dependent improvisation
- protecting client confidence while helping them grow, transact, finance,
  comply, and scale

## 4. Values

**Definition:** The non-negotiable principles for how the team behaves, makes
decisions, and treats clients and each other while executing the mission.

The firm's values are:

- **Sincere Abundance** - We are generous with knowledge, attention, tools, and
  practical judgment. We look for win-win outcomes and create bright futures for
  clients, team members, and the Levine Law community while protecting firm
  capacity and standards.
- **Quality Service** - Service is personal. We take responsibility for the
  quality of our advice, the clarity of our communication, and the reliability
  of our work. We value action over intention.
- **Guaranteed Delivery** - We do what we say we will do. Commitments are made
  carefully, owned clearly, tracked visibly, and escalated early when risk
  appears.
- **Massive Value** - We aim to give more than we take. Clients should receive
  legal judgment, structure, and practical direction worth more than the fee.

Behavioral rules:

- unclear ownership is unacceptable
- missed deadlines must be surfaced before they become surprises
- service quality is measured by useful outcomes, not effort alone
- generosity must coexist with discipline; abundance is not writing off time

## 9A. Matter Quality

A good Levine Law matter fits the target client profile, is properly funded,
has bounded delivery risk, improves per-hour economics, and strengthens the
firm's strategic position.

A bad matter is not just a low-fee matter. It is any matter that consumes
capacity without fit, funding, repeatability, or strategic return.

Matter quality is assessed across five dimensions:

- **Fit** - the client and problem align with ICP-01 or ICP-02, or ML1 approves
  a clear exception.
- **Funding** - the matter is supported by an adequate retainer, clear payment
  terms, or a deliberate credit decision.
- **Repeatability** - the work uses or improves a workflow, template,
  checklist, toolkit, or delivery pattern the firm can reuse.
- **Strategic return** - the matter strengthens the firm's desired position,
  referral network, authority, or long-term client base.
- **Delivery risk** - the work is scoped, bounded, and capable of being
  delivered without uncontrolled urgency, scope creep, or quality risk.

Capacity should not be spent on matters that fail these tests unless ML1 makes
an explicit exception.

## 12. Financial Architecture

The firm should operate at healthy scale against concrete annual controls:

| Year | Base-case collected revenue target | Minimum acceptable committed MRR | Goal committed MRR | Operating savings reserve target |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2026 | CAD 240K-250K | CAD 0-12K/month | CAD 12K/month | CAD 35K |
| 2027 | CAD 325K-375K | CAD 15K/month | CAD 29K/month | CAD 50K |
| 2028 | CAD 425K-500K | CAD 18K/month | CAD 33K/month | CAD 65K |
| 2029 | CAD 550K-625K | CAD 22K/month | CAD 36K/month | CAD 80K |
| 2030 | CAD 750K | CAD 29K/month | CAD 40K/month | CAD 100K |

The reserve target is the minimum cash held in the operating savings account.
It is intended to approximate two months of firm burn at the relevant scale,
rounded upward for control purposes.

The MRR columns distinguish the acceptable floor from the desired architecture.
Both must be measured as **committed monthly recurring revenue**, not hoped-for
retainers.

Minimum acceptable MRR is the control threshold. If committed MRR is below that
level, the year is financially fragile and requires corrective action.

Goal MRR is the desired architecture. It describes the recurring-revenue base
the firm is trying to build so the practice becomes less dependent on one-off
project conversion.

Goal architecture:

- 2026: one Tier 2 retainer at CAD 12K/month
- 2027: two Tier 2 retainers at CAD 14.5K/month
- 2028: two Tier 2 retainers at CAD 16.5K/month
- 2029: two Tier 2 retainers at CAD 18K/month
- 2030: two Tier 2-equivalent retainers at CAD 20K/month

If committed MRR is below target, the gap is treated as a management risk, not
ignored. The firm must identify the replacement path:

- signed project work
- collectible WIP
- high-probability proposals
- referral pipeline
- pricing increase
- reduced spending
- narrower owner-compensation or reinvestment plan

Additional financial controls:

- zero accounts over 60 days
- WIP reviewed monthly before it becomes stale
- invoices issued on a predictable cadence with clear narratives
- overdue accounts followed up promptly and consistently
- visibility into utilization, realization, collection, MRR, reserve balance,
  and system-level metrics

Financial performance is not the only measure of success, but it is the
evidence that the model is working.
