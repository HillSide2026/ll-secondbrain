# LLP-030 Firm Strategy — Governance Stress Test (LLM-006)

> Advisory output. ML1 approval required before any action is taken.
> Reviewed: 2026-03-22

**Current approved stage**: Initiation (approved 2026-03-19). FIRM_STRATEGY.md and BUSINESS_PLAN.md are substantially drafted. FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is a stub.

---

## 1. Approval and Authorization Gaps

**1A — FIRM_STRATEGY.md and BUSINESS_PLAN.md are not ML1-approved as final documents.**
Both carry `status: draft`. The APPROVAL_RECORD.md records only Initiation approval; Planning gate is "Not yet submitted." The initiation approval covers the project structure — it does not approve the strategic content now drafted into these files. FIRM_STRATEGY.md Section 7 self-asserts governing authority over all downstream strategic projects. That authority is not operative until ML1 approves the content at a Planning gate. Any project treating Section 7 constraints as binding is doing so without a valid approval record.

**1B — Matter value floor is explicitly unresolved and load-bearing.**
FIRM_STRATEGY.md Section 5 states the floor is "an open item to be defined as part of this project." FINANCIAL_MODEL.md confirms average matter value (F01) is "Unknown — Required — open item." This is the enforcement mechanism for the most operationally significant exclusion in the strategy. Intake qualification gates in LLP-011 and LLP-012 that reference this exclusion cannot be correctly applied until the floor is set by ML1. The exclusion exists in the strategy but cannot be enforced.

**1C — Andersen cross-entity crediting rule is unresolved.**
FINANCIAL_MODEL.md identifies a live planning risk: Levine Law may need Andersen Service Line economics credited to support the CAD 80,000 owner compensation target, but Andersen fee receipts land in a separate entity. The $240K revenue target and owner comp figures in BUSINESS_PLAN.md may be materially understated or internally inconsistent until this is resolved. RISK_SCAN.md correctly logs this as R-06 (Medium/High), but no ML1 decision on the crediting rule exists in any artifact reviewed. Any project using BUSINESS_PLAN.md financial targets as a governing baseline is relying on an unvalidated number.

**1D — F01 spend ceiling ($16K/year) is not traceable from this folder.**
FIRM_STRATEGY.md Section 7 states F01 spend above this ceiling is not authorized, but the figure is not traced to an approved artifact within LLP-030. This may be recorded in LLP-002 budget artifacts. The cross-reference should exist in FIRM_STRATEGY.md to make the constraint traceable.

## 2. Doctrine Drift

**2A — Stage classification mismatch.**
The project is listed as Initiating in all charter artifacts, but FIRM_STRATEGY.md and BUSINESS_PLAN.md are substantively complete planning documents, not initiation-stage stubs. Two of the three primary deliverables have advanced beyond the authorized stage without a Planning gate approval on record. Recovery path is straightforward: submit to Planning gate with ML1 approving current content.

**2B — FIRM_STRATEGY.md self-grants governing authority before approval.**
The document cannot authorize itself. In draft state, Section 7 constraints are advisory. The risk is that downstream agents or processes treat them as operative based on the document's own assertion rather than an external approval record.

No substantive contradictions were found within or between the three documents. Priority ordering is consistent across all files.

---

## 3. Metric and KPI Gaps

**3A — Deferral to LLP-025 and FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is structurally sound but currently produces an ungoverned execution layer.**
FIRM_STRATEGY.md correctly notes that horizon statements are planning targets, not approved KPI baselines. The problem: FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is a stub. Until both are complete and approved, the quantitative targets in the business plan (quarterly revenue ramps, channel contribution percentages, capacity thresholds) have no measurement infrastructure behind them.

**3B — Capacity ceiling is stated but not governed as a metric.**
The 20-billable-hours/week ceiling and the 12–18 concurrent matters ceiling are planning figures, not governed metrics. No measurement method, monitoring mechanism, or breach-response protocol is defined. The capacity threshold table in BUSINESS_PLAN.md identifies triggers and actions but does not specify who measures, how often, or what the escalation path is.

## 5. Verdict

**LLP-030 is not sufficiently governed to authorize downstream project execution against it in its current state.**

Document quality is not the problem. FIRM_STRATEGY.md and BUSINESS_PLAN.md are coherent, internally consistent, and operationally specific. The problem is approval status and three unresolved items that are load-bearing for execution.

**Three blockers before this strategy can govern:**

1. **No content approval on record.** Planning gate has not been submitted. Projects nominally subordinate to this strategy (LLP-025, LLP-011, LLP-012) are currently executing without a formally approved governing document.

2. **Matter value floor is undefined.** The most operationally significant exclusion cannot be applied. Intake decisions made without it are not compliant with the strategy's intent.

3. **FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is a stub and the Andersen crediting question is open.** The $240K target and $80K owner comp figures are unvalidated.

**Path to authorization**: (a) ML1 approves FIRM_STRATEGY.md and BUSINESS_PLAN.md content at Planning gate; (b) ML1 defines the matter value floor; (c) ML1 decides the Andersen crediting rule; (d) FINANCIAL_MODEL.md is completed. Items (b) and (c) are prerequisites for (d).
