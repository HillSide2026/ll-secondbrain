# Problem Statement

Project ID: LLP-030
Project Path: 07_GROWTH_PROJECTS/LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY
Stage: Initiating

## Core Problem

Levine Law has a 2026 budget and a collection of active marketing, funnel, and operations projects — but no governing document that answers:

1. **What is the firm trying to become?** No Firm Strategy exists. There is no statement of what kind of firm LL is building toward (sustainable boutique? reputation-first practice? sellable asset?), what it is optimizing for (margin, lifestyle, growth, sellability, reputation), or what the 3–5 year horizon looks like.

2. **How does the firm get there?** No Business Plan exists. There is no sequenced plan connecting marketing investments, staffing decisions, and capacity constraints to target outcomes. Individual projects are planned; the firm is not.

3. **What is the financial model?** The LLP-002 budget documents revenue assumptions, but there is no P&L projection linking marketing spend → matter volume → revenue → margin. The budget asks "can we afford this?"; the financial model asks "if we do this, what happens to the bottom line?"

Without these three documents, the firm is executing tactics without a strategy.

## Current State

- **Active projects** (LLP-011, LLP-012, LLP-025) are making real decisions about channel investment, funnel architecture, and ICP definition — but without a governing frame specifying what the firm is optimizing for.
- **"Strategic projects"** folder contains projects that are treated as optional or secondary rather than as execution of a deliberate plan.
- **2026 target** is now CAD 240,000 of banked cash, with current modeled owner compensation of CAD 80,000 (`$72k` salary + `$8k` bonus) — but the plan still does not answer what must happen to reach it, and what margin looks like at each stage.
- **Staffing decisions** (setter, senior lawyer) are modeled in LLP-012 and LLP-011 but have no firm-level capacity plan or financial impact model to anchor them.
- **ICP definitions** (ICP-01, ICP-02) exist in the marketing strategy — but there is no firm-level positioning statement that ICP definitions are derived from.

## What Is Missing

### 1. Firm Strategy

The Firm Strategy must answer:
- What kind of firm is Levine Law in 3–5 years? (Sustainable boutique? Scalable practice? Reputable specialist? Sellable asset?)
- What is the firm optimizing for — and in what priority order?
  - Margin (net income as % of revenue)
  - Lifestyle (ML1 hours, capacity, sustainability)
  - Revenue growth (top-line trajectory)
  - Reputation (market positioning, authority)
  - Sellability (equity value, transferable client relationships)
- What does the firm explicitly not do? (Strategic exclusions)
- What is the 3-year goal state?

### 2. Business Plan

The Business Plan must answer:
- What 2026 operating target is the firm actually governing against, and over what timeline?
- What is the acquisition architecture (F01 → F02 → F03) and how does it generate that revenue?
- What is the capacity model — how many matters can ML1 carry, and when does staffing (setter, senior lawyer) become necessary?
- What is the staffing and resourcing roadmap?
- What are the quarterly milestones?

### 3. Financial Model

The Financial Model must answer:
- At a given marketing spend (e.g., $1,500/month F01 Google Ads), how many matters does the firm need to generate, at what average matter value, to hit the margin target?
- What is the break-even for each budget scenario?
- What does the P&L look like under each scenario (best/base/worst)?
- How does the financial picture change as F02 is added and F01 is wound down?

## Impact of Inaction

Without a Firm Strategy:
- Individual project decisions are made without a governing frame — channel choices, ICP refinements, and staffing decisions may optimize for different goals
- There is no authoritative answer to "is this worth doing?" at the portfolio level

Without a Business Plan:
- Milestones cannot be validated — no one knows if the current pace of project execution is consistent with hitting 2026 revenue targets
- Staffing decisions (setter, senior lawyer) have no financial or capacity model to anchor them

Without a Financial Model:
- Revenue targets float; margin impact of decisions (e.g., adding a senior lawyer at $X cost) is not modeled before committing
- Budget scenarios are not connected to actions

## Desired State

- `FIRM_STRATEGY.md` is approved by ML1 and governs all strategic project authorization
- `BUSINESS_PLAN.md` sequences the path from current state to target scenario with resource requirements
- `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` projects P&L under base and scenario assumptions, linked to the acquisition architecture
- All active marketing and operations projects have been reviewed for alignment with the approved Firm Strategy
