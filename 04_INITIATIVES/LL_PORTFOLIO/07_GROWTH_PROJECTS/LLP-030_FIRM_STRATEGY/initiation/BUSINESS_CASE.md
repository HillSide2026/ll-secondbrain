# Business Case

Project ID: LLP-030
Project Path: 07_GROWTH_PROJECTS/LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY
Stage: Initiating

## Core Question

Should Levine Law maintain a dedicated Firm Strategy and Business Plan project
as a governed decision layer, rather than continuing to let growth, staffing,
and acquisition decisions emerge piecemeal from individual projects?

## Strategic Opportunity

- convert a set of active tactics into a coherent firm-level plan
- anchor marketing, staffing, and capacity decisions to one approved frame
- create a clearer bridge from the 2026 operating target to the longer-range
  firm model
- reduce the risk that downstream projects optimize for different goals
- make tradeoffs about margin, owner compensation, workload, and growth more
  explicit before new commitments are made

## Why This Matters Now

- Levine Law already has active funnel, staffing, and operations work, but
  those projects still depend on a governing answer to what kind of firm is
  being built
- the current LL baseline of `CAD 240,000` revenue supporting about
  `CAD 80,000` to Matthew only becomes strategically useful if it is connected
  to a real acquisition, capacity, and margin model
- new staffing scenarios, funnel sequencing, and cross-entity revenue-credit
  questions are now arising faster than ad hoc judgment should be carrying them
- without a governed strategy packet, the firm risks treating strategic work as
  commentary rather than as decision infrastructure

## Conditions for a Positive Business Case

- `FIRM_STRATEGY.md`, `BUSINESS_PLAN.md`, and `FINANCIAL_MODEL.md` materially
  improve downstream decisions rather than duplicating existing documents
- the project creates a usable governing frame for `LLP-025`, `LLP-011`,
  `LLP-012`, staffing assumptions, and future leverage decisions
- the business plan can translate high-level goals into decision thresholds for
  acquisition, capacity, staffing, and spend
- the financial model clarifies margin and contribution implications before the
  firm adds fixed cost or expands delivery
- the outputs remain authoritative enough to shape portfolio decisions without
  collapsing into day-to-day operations

## Conditions That Defeat the Case

- the project becomes an open-ended strategy narrative with no practical
  decision use
- the business plan merely restates project work already captured elsewhere
- the financial model fails to answer concrete questions about staffing,
  acquisition efficiency, or owner economics
- downstream projects continue to ignore the approved strategy frame
- the project is treated as permission for execution rather than as a governed
  strategic control layer

## Initial Recommendation

Proceed with LLP-030 as an active strategic control project.

The project must remain narrow and decision-useful:

- Firm Strategy answers what the firm is optimizing for and what it is not
  trying to become
- Business Plan answers how the 2026 and medium-term targets are meant to be
  reached
- Financial Model answers what those choices do to revenue, margin, and owner
  outcomes

This project should not become a generic planning archive. Its value is in
turning firm-level judgment into a reusable governing frame for active LL
decisions.

## Decision Standard

Proceed beyond initiation only if ML1 concludes that:

- a governed firm-level strategy layer is still genuinely needed
- the current outputs are specific enough to improve real decisions
- the project remains clearly separated from operating execution
- the likely value of firmer strategic control exceeds the cost of maintaining
  the packet
