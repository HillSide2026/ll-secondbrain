# Levine Law - Practice

## Definition

Practice is the operational translation of Levine Law's values and mission.

It answers the question: **How does this firm work every day?**

---

## Practice Promise

Clients should feel protected, informed, and in control.

The firm does not sell commodities. It sells structured legal judgment,
reliable execution, and business clarity.

---

## Daily Operating Standard

In practice, this means:

- every matter has a clear owner, scope, next step, and delivery path
- every recurring service has defined deliverables, pricing logic, and renewal
  expectations
- every standardized solution has intake criteria, scope boundaries, workflow,
  templates, quality control, pricing, and follow-on pathway
- every important deadline, filing, renewal, and client obligation is recorded
  in a system rather than held in someone's head
- every team member understands the systems that make the firm run and owns
  their role within those systems
- routine work is automated or templated where possible
- human expertise is reserved for judgment, risk, strategy, and client trust
- meetings are short, structured, and outcome-focused
- communication is plain, documented, and useful

---

## Financial Operating Standard

Financial hygiene is part of service reliability.

In practice, this means:

- retainers are requested before work begins or before trust balances become
  inadequate
- WIP is reviewed before it becomes stale
- invoices are issued on a predictable cadence with clear narratives
- overdue accounts are followed up promptly and consistently
- the operating savings account meets or exceeds the annual reserve target in
  `FIRM_ARCHITECTURE_E_MYTH.md`
- monthly recurring revenue is tracked against the annual minimum acceptable
  and goal targets in `FIRM_ARCHITECTURE_E_MYTH.md`
- if committed MRR is below target, the gap must be covered by identified
  project work, WIP conversion, or a revised acquisition plan
- work is paused, narrowed, or escalated when payment risk threatens service
  reliability

## Matter Quality Standard

A good Levine Law matter fits the target client profile, is properly funded,
has bounded delivery risk, improves per-hour economics, and strengthens the
firm's strategic position.

A bad matter is not just a low-fee matter. It is any matter that consumes
capacity without fit, funding, repeatability, or strategic return.

Matter quality is assessed across five dimensions:

- **Fit** - the client and problem align with ICP-01 or ICP-02, or ML1 approves
  a clear exception
- **Funding** - the matter is supported by an adequate retainer, clear payment
  terms, or a deliberate credit decision
- **Repeatability** - the work uses or improves a workflow, template,
  checklist, toolkit, or delivery pattern the firm can reuse
- **Strategic return** - the matter strengthens the firm's desired position,
  referral network, authority, or long-term client base
- **Delivery risk** - the work is scoped, bounded, and capable of being
  delivered without uncontrolled urgency, scope creep, or quality risk

Capacity should not be spent on matters that fail these tests unless ML1 makes
an explicit exception.

---

## Practice Exclusions

Levine Law does not build its practice around:

- unclear ownership
- unmanaged scope creep
- unfunded work
- vague client expectations
- matters that cannot be documented, invoiced, and collected cleanly
- volume growth that damages quality or capacity
