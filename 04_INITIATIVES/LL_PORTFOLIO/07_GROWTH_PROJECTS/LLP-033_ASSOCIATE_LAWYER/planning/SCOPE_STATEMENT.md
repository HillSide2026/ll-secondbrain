# Scope Statement

Project ID: `LLP-033`
Project Path: `04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/07_GROWTH_PROJECTS/LLP-033_ASSOCIATE_LAWYER`
Stage: `Planning`

## Planning Use

Use this file to freeze what LLP-033 is actually designing: a bounded delivery
lawyer model with explicit recruitment, supervision, monitoring, and analytics
controls.

## In Scope

- role architecture for a mid-level but competent lawyer operating under ML1
  supervision
- evaluation of both contractor-style and employee-style engagement structures
  as planning options
- recruitment design:
  candidate profile, seniority band, sourcing channels, interview structure,
  practical assessment, and decision criteria
- supervision design:
  delegated matter eligibility, review checkpoints, sign-off boundaries, client
  communication limits, escalation triggers, and file-assignment rules
- monitoring design:
  weekly review cadence, supervision-load tracking, exception logging, and
  service-quality visibility
- analytics design:
  unit-economics formulas, delegated-matter contribution tests, turnaround and
  rework metrics, and activation thresholds
- initial economic focus on whether repeatable `F01` work can remain
  financially attractive when fulfilled by a non-ML1 lawyer under supervision
- definition of the minimum activation packet ML1 needs before any live launch

## Out of Scope

- actual hiring, contracting, or onboarding before ML1 approval
- unsupervised delegated legal work
- broad HR policy or people-operations system design unrelated to this role
- partner-layer design
- unrestricted delegation across all Levine Law matters
- automatic activation for specialist `F03` matters or novel/high-risk files
- rewriting the locked `2026` budget assumptions as part of this project

## Initial Matter-Boundary Direction

Planning starts from the assumption that, if activation occurs, the first
delegable work should come from repeatable, lower-variance file types rather
than the highest-judgment or highest-risk matters.

Initial likely candidates for evaluation:
- repeatable corporate/commercial maintenance work
- repeatable contract drafting or review work
- bounded remediation work arising from standardized diagnostics

Initial likely exclusions:
- bespoke disputes
- major transactions
- unscripted high-stakes client strategy
- specialist regulatory work requiring ML1-led judgment until explicitly
  approved otherwise

## Required Planning Outputs

- frozen role hypothesis and candidate profile
- frozen matter-eligibility and supervision model
- frozen monitoring and exception-reporting framework
- frozen economics test for delegated delivery
- clear ML1 activation gate recommendation

## Gate Criteria for Executing Authorization

- the role's scope and exclusions are explicit
- delegated matter eligibility is specific enough to avoid silent scope creep
- supervision doctrine is explicit enough to defend quality and risk posture
- monitoring and analytics can surface failure quickly
- Levine Law has demonstrated at least three consecutive months at or above
  `CAD 20,000` in monthly revenue before activation is recommended
- economics are explicit enough for ML1 to make a bounded go/no-go decision
