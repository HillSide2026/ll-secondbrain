# Assumptions and Constraints

Project ID: `LLP-033`
Stage: `Planning`

## Assumptions

- Levine Law can likely identify a bounded set of repeatable matters that do
  not require ML1 to perform every drafting step personally
- a mid-level but competent lawyer can execute part of that bounded matter set
  if supervision and review are explicit
- not every `F01` matter is delegable; the first activation set must be a
  filtered subset rather than the full channel
- ML1 will remain the supervising lawyer and final quality-control owner
- planning can rely on historical matter and revenue data even though no
  contracting lawyer existed in `2025`
- the best first economic test is delegated `F01` work because it is the most
  evidence-rich historical acquisition channel

## Constraints

- the current `LLP-030` business plan still says `2026` has no core hires;
  this project may prepare a role and an activation gate, but it does not by
  itself override that posture
- no live hiring, engagement, or file delegation is authorized during planning
- no profitability claim is valid until lawyer cost, direct matter cost,
  acquisition cost, and supervision burden are modeled together
- planning must not assume every marketing dollar belongs to the same matter
  population without explicit source mapping
- high-risk, novel, dispute-heavy, or specialist-regulatory matters may not be
  silently swept into the first delegation set
- supervision cannot be treated as free; ML1 review time is part of the real
  delivery model

## Locked Planning Direction

Currently locked:

- the planning packet is organized around recruitment, supervision,
  monitoring, and analytics
- the central economics question is whether a delegated delivery lawyer can
  fulfill a bounded set of Levine Law work while preserving contribution
- the first candidate matter population for analysis is the `25-...` matter
  cohort with `Stream` and anything `Rousseau Mazzuca LLP` excluded
- through the end of October `2025`, that narrowed cohort shows approximately
  `CAD 68,802.92` billed and `CAD 67,746.32` paid
- through full-year `2025`, the booked P&L shows approximately
  `CAD 25,189.02` in digital marketing expense and `CAD 25,946.90` in total
  marketing expense
- no contracting lawyer existed in `2025`, so the historical data is a
  revenue and matter-mix baseline, not proof of delegated-delivery economics
- no activation recommendation should proceed unless Levine Law first shows at
  least three consecutive months at or above `CAD 20,000` in monthly revenue

## Still Not Locked

- contractor versus employee structure for the first role
- exact seniority band
- compensation structure
- exact matter-eligibility matrix
- activation timing
- whether the `CAD 20,000` three-month dependency has actually been satisfied
- supervision-load threshold that would make the model uneconomic

## Planning Boundary

This stage is limited to role design, eligibility design, supervision design,
monitoring design, economics design, and ML1 gate preparation.
