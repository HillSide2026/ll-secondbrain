# Risk Register

Project ID: `LLP-033`
Stage: `Planning`

## Category Rule

As a Levine Law strategic project, LLP-033 must use only the canonical
categories permitted by `POL-063` and `POL-064`:
- `Scope`
- `Schedule`
- `Budget`
- `Financial`
- `Strategic`

| Risk ID | Risk | Category | Likelihood | Impact | Planned Control | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| R-01 | Delegated legal work produces quality variance or malpractice exposure beyond Levine Law's acceptable tolerance | Strategic | Medium | High | Define matter-eligibility exclusions, mandatory review points, and sign-off rules before activation | ML1 |
| R-02 | The initial delegated-matter boundary is wrong, causing the wrong matter types to be delegated first | Scope | Medium | High | Create an explicit eligible-matter list and exclusion list; no silent expansion | ML1 |
| R-03 | Delegated `F01` work fails the economic test once lawyer cost, direct matter cost, acquisition cost, and supervision time are added | Financial | High | High | Use a contribution model that includes acquisition cost, direct lawyer cost, direct matter cost, and supervision load | ML1 + financial modeling support |
| R-04 | ML1 supervision load offsets the expected capacity gain from the role | Strategic | High | High | Measure review time, rework rate, and turnaround before treating delegation as true leverage | ML1 |
| R-05 | Recruitment yields lawyers who are technically capable but not a fit for Levine Law standards or operating style | Strategic | Medium | Medium | Use a candidate scorecard, practical assessment, and work-sample review | ML1 |
| R-06 | Client trust or Levine Law brand signal erodes if delegated delivery is unclear or poorly framed | Strategic | Medium | High | Define client-communication boundaries and disclosure posture before launch | ML1 |
| R-07 | Monitoring design fails to surface underperformance early enough for ML1 to intervene | Strategic | Medium | High | Define weekly review cadence, exception log, and escalation triggers in the planning packet | ML1 |
| R-08 | The role is activated before scope, economics, and governance gates are satisfied | Scope | Medium | Medium | Tie activation to an explicit ML1 gate decision rather than narrative momentum | ML1 |

## Highest-Leverage Planning Risks

- `R-03`: economics fail once the full cost stack is modeled
- `R-04`: supervision load cancels the expected capacity gain
- `R-01`: quality or legal risk exceeds the firm's tolerance

## Planning Rule

No activation recommendation is credible unless the packet gives ML1 a real
way to detect and stop failure early.
