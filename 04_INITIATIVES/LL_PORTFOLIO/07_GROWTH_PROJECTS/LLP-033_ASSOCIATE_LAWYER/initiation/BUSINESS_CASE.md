# Business Case

Project ID: `LLP-033`
Project Path: `04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/07_GROWTH_PROJECTS/LLP-033_ASSOCIATE_LAWYER`
Stage: `Initiation`

## Core Question

Can Levine Law add a supervised delivery lawyer in a way that increases
capacity and preserves margin, quality, and control?

## Strategic Opportunity

- extend delivery capacity beyond ML1's current single-lawyer ceiling
- make repeatable lower-variance legal work delegable under supervision
- reduce key-person dependence in recurring delivery
- create a viable path for future scale that does not rely on ML1 personally
  fulfilling every file

## Why This Matters Now

- `F01` has generated meaningful revenue, but the current platform remains
  heavily dependent on ML1 for execution
- the firm's medium-term strategy contemplates selective leverage once control
  and economics are clear
- if delegated delivery cannot be made safe and economically sound, the firm
  needs to know that before treating growth as realistic

## Conditions for a Positive Business Case

- a clear role definition exists
- only a bounded set of matter types is initially delegable
- supervision doctrine is explicit and defensible
- monitoring and analytics can surface failure early
- economic modeling shows that delegated delivery improves contribution rather
  than merely adding cost

## Conditions That Defeat the Case

- supervision load simply shifts the bottleneck without real capacity gain
- quality or client trust deteriorates under delegation
- the role only works if low-value matters are subsidized by ML1 time
- economics fail once lawyer cost, supervision time, and acquisition cost are
  included

## Current Business-Case Direction

The planning hypothesis is that a mid-level but competent lawyer may be able to
fulfill a bounded portion of repeatable Levine Law work, beginning with
eligible `F01` matters, but that this should not be treated as approved until
the planning packet proves the supervision model and economics.
