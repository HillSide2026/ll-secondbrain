# Stream Client Strategic Intelligence

Source: ML1 strategic session 2026-05-19.

Stream Ventures Limited is the current anchor retainer client. This file records
strategic intelligence about Stream's trajectory and the implications for ML1's
role as the relationship scales.

Stream is bootstrapped — not VC-backed. All growth is organic and must be
profitable. Stream plans to sell (ML1-confirmed 2026-05-19).

**Product:** Fiat and stablecoin cross-border rails (B2B). Stream operates both
fiat and stablecoin payment infrastructure for cross-border movement.

---

## Scale Scenario: £1B Processing

### Company Size

At 0.5-1.5% blended margin (FX spread + fees), £1B in processing volume generates
approximately £5M-£15M in revenue. At 20-35% EBITDA margins: £1M-£5M EBITDA.

Because Stream is bootstrapped and profitable, headcount will be lean: approximately
60-100 employees at this revenue profile. 300 employees at £15M revenue is not
viable without significant burn. The lean headcount is a feature, not a gap.

### Legal Function at £1B

| Function | Who holds it |
|---|---|
| MLRO (UK) | Full-time in-house — separate from external counsel |
| CAMLO (Canada) | Full-time in-house — ML1's current role transitions out |
| General Counsel / Head of Legal | In-house hire, likely triggered at £200M-£300M processing volume |
| AML / sanctions team | Dedicated, separate from legal |
| US MTL counsel | External (40-50 state licences; managed by GC) |
| EU, UK expansion counsel | External, managed by GC |

ML1's current CAMLO and external advisory role evolves from an operational compliance
function into a senior regulatory strategy relationship — advising on enforcement
exposure, expansion licensing, and jurisdictional risk across the full licence stack.

### Licence Stack at £1B

| Jurisdiction | Licence |
|---|---|
| Canada | FINTRAC MSB registration; RPAA PSP registration; provincial MSB (BC, Quebec, others) |
| UK | FCA Authorised Payment Institution (API) or Electronic Money Institution (EMI) under PSR 2017 |
| EU | EMI or PI authorisation in one member state (EU passporting) |
| US (if in scope) | FinCEN MSB; state Money Transmission Licences (40-50 states); NMLS |

Without the US: 5-15 licences. With the US: the MTL stack alone is 40-50 licences
and typically £1M-£3M in legal and state fees to acquire.

## ML1 Role Scenarios

### Preferred: Senior External Counsel / Deepening Retainer (Scenario A)

The relationship stays external but deepens in substance. Stream scales; ML1 evolves
from CAMLO + regulatory advisor to regulatory strategist + jurisdictional supervisor
(supervising US MTL counsel, EU counsel, UK FCA counsel as required).

ML1 holds the whole picture and manages specialists in each jurisdiction. Canadian
regulatory depth (FINTRAC, RPAA, CAMLO infrastructure) plus payments-specific
expertise is rare. US payments lawyers know MTL mechanics; they do not know the
Canadian or cross-border regulatory layer. That gap is ML1's structural advantage.

By 2029: Tier 3 retainer (CAD $29,500/month, ~CAD $354K/year) plus significant
project work. If Stream reaches £1B, the retainer model may need a bespoke structure
above Tier 3.

**ML1 does not want single-employer concentration risk.** The external structure
preserves LL economics and avoids the employment and residency complications of
an in-house role at a UK-headquartered company. This is the scenario to build toward.

### Ruled Out: Group Head of Legal In-House (Scenario B)

Stream hires ML1 as Group Head of Legal or GC. ML1's British passport removes UK
immigration friction. Canadian regulatory depth plus payments expertise fits the
profile a cross-border PSP needs.

**Ruled out by ML1 on concentration risk grounds.** Single-employer concentration
is exactly the risk LL is built to avoid. Do not revisit unless ML1 reopens.

### Possible But Unlikely: Independent Director (Scenario C)

Stream asks ML1 to join the board as an independent director. The FCA expects
payment company boards to have members with genuine regulatory expertise. ML1's
inside knowledge of Stream's compliance architecture and risk profile is exactly
the profile a PSP board needs as it scales. British passport removes friction for
UK-connected board positions.

**The structural conflict:** Director and CAMLO/external legal advisor are
incompatible roles. A director owes fiduciary duties to the company; a CAMLO
has independent regulatory reporting obligations that can run against the company's
interests. Cannot hold both simultaneously. If a directorship materialised, the
legal and CAMLO work would have to go elsewhere — a significant revenue trade-off
unless the director compensation or equity upside is substantial.

Independent director fees at a private company this size: £30K-£80K/year — below
the retainer economics. The role only makes financial sense if Stream is preparing
for a transaction and the board seat carries equity upside at exit.

ML1 assessment: less unlikely than it appears, but the economic trade-off only
stacks up in a transaction context.

## Equity and Exit

### Transaction Thesis

Stream plans to sell. ML1-confirmed 2026-05-19.

### Valuation Range at £1B Processing

| Buyer type | Multiple | Implied value |
|---|---|---|
| Financial buyer (PE) | 8-12x EBITDA | £15M-£60M |
| Strategic (large processor, bank) | 5-8x revenue | £25M-£120M |
| Platform play (Visa, Mastercard, Nuvei) | premium for licence stack + corridors | £100M-£200M |

**Realistic range: £40M-£120M.** The upper end requires a strategic buyer who
values the regulatory infrastructure (FCA authorisation, FINTRAC, RPAA, correspondent
banking relationships) and specific payment corridors, not just volume. The licence
stack has standalone value — building it costs real money and time; acquiring it via
M&A commands a premium.

### Equity: The CAMLO Conflict Gates Everything

A CAMLO with equity in the company has a financial interest in its continued operation
that can colour every reporting judgment — STR filing, suspicious transaction assessment,
regulatory escalation. FINTRAC and the FCA both require MLRO/CAMLO independence. Equity
cannot be held simultaneously with the CAMLO role.

The equity conversation only opens after the CAMLO role ends or is transferred to
someone else. That transition is a natural leverage moment — when Stream needs to
replace ML1 as CAMLO, the negotiation of that transition is where equity or a
success fee should be raised.

### Preferred Instrument: Success Fee

A transaction fee or success fee (contractual right to a defined payment on a sale
or listing event) is cleaner than equity for ML1's situation:

- No ongoing holding conflict with the advisory or CAMLO role
- Participates in exit upside without equity governance complications
- Does not require ML1 to step back from the legal/advisory role during the
  holding period
- Simpler to negotiate than a shareholder agreement with put options and
  liquidity rights

| Exit value | 0.5% fee | 1% fee | 2% fee |
|---|---|---|---|
| £40M | £200K | £400K | £800K |
| £80M | £400K | £800K | £1.6M |
| £120M | £600K | £1.2M | £2.4M |

Right moment: when the CAMLO role is being restructured, or when Stream signals
it is preparing for a transaction.
