# Rate Posture 2026-2029

## Purpose

Define the planning rate ladder for LLP-039 as fintech retainer scope matures.

This document does not change client-facing rates by itself. It provides a
minimum / middle / maximum model for future ML1 pricing decisions.

## Rate Bands

| Band | Hourly Rate | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Minimum | CAD 480/hour | Current standard reference rate; floor for non-premium work |
| Middle | Varies by year | Midpoint planning rate for bounded fintech/regulatory work |
| Maximum | CAD 850/hour | Ceiling for premium specialist fintech/regulatory work; not to be exceeded in this planning horizon |

The maximum planning rate is CAD 850/hour.

## Year-by-Year Rate Posture

| Year | Greenlit Scope | Minimum | Middle | Maximum | Rate Decision Posture |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 2026 | Basic bounded advisory only | CAD 480/hr | CAD 510/hr | CAD 550/hr | Use existing rates; do not rely on premium-rate work to make the model viable |
| 2027 | Basic scope plus controlled premium triage | CAD 555/hr | CAD 600/hr | CAD 650/hr | Introduce higher-rate quoting only for tightly scoped exceptions or separate projects |
| 2028 | Select premium adjacent matters if delivery spine is proven | CAD 630/hr | CAD 690/hr | CAD 750/hr | Use formal specialist-rate band or fixed-fee equivalents for bounded premium projects |
| 2029 | Mature premium specialist model if Path A/B evidence supports it | CAD 680/hr | CAD 780/hr | CAD 850/hr | Maximum rate ceiling available only for proven premium specialist work |

## Retainer Interaction

The hourly rate ladder does not expand retainer scope.

For 2026:
- CAD 12,000/month mandate remains capped at 6 hours/week.
- CAD 20,000/month mandate remains capped at 10 hours/week.
- CAD 20,000/month buys more weekly capacity, not a higher scope category.
- Premium adjacent matters are not active retainer work during 2026.

For 2027-2029:
- higher rates may support separately scoped premium work;
- higher rates do not authorize crisis, exam, remediation, launch, securities,
  tax, or embedded-compliance work unless that scope has been separately
  greenlit;
- fixed-fee pricing may be preferable to open hourly pricing where the service
  is repeatable and bounded.

## Decision Rules

1. Use the minimum rate for basic or standard work unless a specialist rate is
   justified by scope, urgency, expertise, or market position.
2. Use the middle rate only where the work is clearly fintech/regulatory and
   delivery is bounded.
3. Use the maximum rate only for high-value, premium specialist work that is
   narrow, urgent or strategically significant, and not likely to become an
   uncapped operating role.
4. Do not use higher rates to rescue bad-fit work. Decline, defer, refer, or
   separate-scope instead.
5. Do not exceed CAD 850/hour in this planning horizon without a separate ML1
   pricing decision.
