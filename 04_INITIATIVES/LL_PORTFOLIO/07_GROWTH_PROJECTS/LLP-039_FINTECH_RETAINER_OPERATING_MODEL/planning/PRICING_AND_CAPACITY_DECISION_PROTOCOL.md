# Pricing and Capacity Decision Protocol

## Purpose

Define how Levine Law decides which fintech retainer mandates to sell, accept,
defer, reprice, rescope, or decline.

This protocol is not a general approval protocol. It governs the commercial and
capacity decision: how many high-cash-flow fintech retainers can be carried at
once, at what price, and under what scope/capacity conditions.

## Governing Premise

Levine Law currently cannot safely handle more than three fintech retainer
mandates concurrently.

Levine Law also faces existential business risk if it has fewer than two active
fintech retainer mandates.

The operating target is therefore narrow:

| Zone | Active Fintech Retainers | Decision Posture |
|---|---:|---|
| Existential risk | 0-1 | Acquisition and conversion priority; avoid over-optimizing scope before securing mandate count |
| Minimum viable base | 2 | Stabilize delivery, protect renewals, continue selective acquisition |
| Target operating band | 2-3 | Accept only mandate mixes that fit weekly capacity and strategic quality |
| Capacity breach | 4+ | Do not accept without additional delivery support, offboarding, deferral, or revised capacity model |

## Mandate Types

| Tier | Monthly Retainer | Scope | Weekly Capacity Limit | Current Decision Status |
|---|---:|---|---:|---|
| Tier 1 | CAD 8,000 | To be determined | To be determined | Not ready for sale until scope/capacity are defined |
| Tier 2 | CAD 12,000 | Core bounded fintech advisory scope | 6 hours/week | Approved planning reference tier |
| Tier 3 | CAD 20,000 | Same scope envelope as Tier 2 | 10 hours/week | Approved premium-capacity planning tier |

The CAD 20,000 mandate buys more reserved weekly capacity. It does not buy a
broader substantive scope, crisis coverage, regulatory-exam support, product
launch counsel, securities advice, tax advice, or embedded compliance ownership.

## 2026 Scope Constraint

Through December 31, 2026, LL should only accept basic bounded advisory
mandates under this model.

Premium adjacent work should not be accepted as active retainer work during
2026. The correct decision is to defer, refer, waitlist, or separate-scope later
unless ML1 expressly approves an exception.

## Capacity Decision Rules

### Rule 1 - Minimum Retainer Base

If active fintech retainer count is below two, LL is in existential-risk mode.

Required response:
- prioritize qualified retainer conversion;
- avoid accepting low-quality non-retainer work that consumes strategic capacity;
- protect existing retainer renewals;
- review pipeline weekly until two active retainers are secured.

### Rule 2 - Maximum Concurrent Retainer Count

LL must not carry more than three active fintech retainer mandates concurrently
under the current solo-owner model.

A fourth concurrent retainer is prohibited unless ML1 first approves at least
one of:
- additional delivery support;
- offboarding or non-renewal of an existing retainer;
- deferral/waitlist start date;
- materially narrower scope/capacity for one mandate;
- a revised capacity model.

### Rule 3 - Weekly Capacity Controls

Mandate count is necessary but not sufficient. Weekly reserved capacity must
also fit within ML1's current capacity.

Current planning reference:
- ML1 billable capacity ceiling: approximately 20 hours/week.
- CAD 12,000 mandate: maximum 6 hours/week.
- CAD 20,000 mandate: maximum 10 hours/week.
- CAD 8,000 mandate: TBD.

Decision implication:
- `3 x CAD 12,000` is the reference target: 18 hours/week.
- `2 x CAD 20,000` reaches the full 20-hour/week ceiling before other work.
- `1 x CAD 20,000 + 1 x CAD 12,000` uses 16 hours/week and is likely the
  cleanest mixed premium/reference configuration.

### Rule 4 - Scope Boundary Before Acceptance

No retainer should be accepted until the client-facing scope and excluded work
triggers are clear enough to prevent the client from treating the mandate as an
uncapped fractional legal department.

For CAD 12,000 and CAD 20,000 mandates, excluded work remains separate-scope
even where unused weekly capacity exists.

### Rule 5 - Pricing Does Not Cure Bad Fit

Higher price does not make a bad mandate safe.

Decline, defer, or separate-scope the mandate if the expected work pattern
involves:
- crisis response;
- regulatory exams, investigations, enforcement, or remediation;
- embedded compliance ownership;
- regulator, supervisor, bank, or FI interface beyond ordinary document support;
- new product launch support;
- new jurisdiction entry;
- securities or tax advice;
- repeated urgent turnarounds;
- client resistance to scope boundaries.

### Rule 6 - Intake Decision Matrix

| Current State | Proposed New Mandate | Default Decision |
|---|---|---|
| 0 active retainers | Any qualified retainer | Strong pursue, but do not waive scope discipline |
| 1 active retainer | Qualified CAD 12,000 or CAD 20,000 | Strong pursue; reaching 2 active retainers is stabilizing |
| 2 active retainers | High-fit mandate inside weekly capacity | Selective accept if mandate mix remains sustainable |
| 3 active retainers | Any additional mandate | Defer, waitlist, or replace only |
| Any count | Work outside retainer scope | Separate-scope, decline, or refer |
| Any count | Tier 1 CAD 8,000 before scope/capacity defined | Hold until Tier 1 design is approved |
| Any count during 2026 | Premium adjacent matter | Defer, refer, waitlist, or later separate-scope unless ML1 approves exception |

## 2026 Client Scope Decision

For 2026, accept only fintech clients that fit the basic bounded advisory model.

| Client Type | Default 2026 Decision |
|---|---|
| Operating Canadian fintech / payments / MSB / PSP with recurring advisory questions | In scope if not in crisis |
| Established MSB with internal compliance owner | In scope for advisory support; not for outsourced compliance or exam response |
| Embedded finance operator | In scope for bounded MSB/RPAA/banking advisory; not for program redesign or launch execution |
| Digital-asset or stablecoin payment-flow operator | Limited in scope for MSB/RPAA/payment characterization; securities/tax/token issuance excluded |
| Cross-border expansion or Canada market-entry client | Usually defer or separate-scope; narrow threshold advice only |
| Client in exam, enforcement, remediation, incident, de-risking, or launch mode | Out of scope for 2026 retainer |

| Mix | Monthly Revenue | Weekly Capacity | Decision Read |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 1 x CAD 12,000 | CAD 12,000 | 6 hours | Existential-risk state; insufficient retainer base |
| 2 x CAD 12,000 | CAD 24,000 | 12 hours | Minimum viable base; still acquisition-positive |

| 2 x CAD 20,000 | CAD 40,000 | 20 hours | Revenue-strong but capacity-tight; no additional material work |
| 1 x CAD 20,000 + 2 x CAD 12,000 | CAD 44,000 | 22 hours | Not currently sustainable without support or narrower capacity |
| 3 x CAD 20,000 | CAD 60,000 | 30 hours | Not currently sustainable |

## Review Cadence

Review mandate capacity monthly and before any new retainer is accepted.

Minimum review inputs:
- active mandate count;
- mandate mix;
- weekly capacity promised;
- actual weekly usage by client;
- urgent-request frequency;
- separate-scope conversion;
- late payment or collection friction;
- renewal risk;
- non-retainer matter load.

## Decision Output

Each proposed mandate should receive one of five decisions:

| Decision | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Accept | Fits price, scope, capacity, client quality, and mandate-mix constraints |
| Accept with condition | Requires narrowed scope, start-date deferral, payment condition, or separate-scope carveout |
| Waitlist | Good fit, but current mandate count/capacity is full |
| Separate-scope only | Valuable work, but not safe inside recurring retainer |
| Decline / refer | Poor fit, outside expertise, or likely to damage capacity/economics |
