# Client Scope 2026

## Purpose

Define which fintech clients are in scope for Levine Law's 2026 retainer model.

This document narrows the broader Funnel 3 ICP to the subset LL can safely serve
through December 31, 2026 under the basic bounded-scope mandate model.

## 2026 Operating Rule

Until December 31, 2026, LL should only sell and deliver basic bounded fintech
advisory mandates.

LL should not accept premium adjacent matters inside the retainer during 2026.
Out-of-scope work may be issue-spotted, deferred, referred, or separately
scoped later, but it should not become active 2026 retainer delivery unless ML1
expressly approves an exception.

## In-Scope 2026 Client Profile

The best-fit 2026 client is an operating fintech, payments, MSB, PSP, embedded
finance, or digital-asset business that needs recurring external legal advisory
support but does not need LL to become its operating compliance function.

In-scope clients generally have:

- an existing operating business or clearly defined product model;
- payments, MSB, PSP, AML/KYC, RPAA, banking, embedded finance, or digital-asset
  regulatory questions;
- internal management, compliance, product, or operations owners who remain
  responsible for execution;
- enough commercial maturity to pay monthly in advance;
- willingness to accept written scope boundaries and weekly capacity limits;
- no active regulatory crisis requiring immediate response;
- no expectation that LL will act as CAMLO, compliance officer, product owner,
  bank-relations manager, or embedded general counsel.

## In-Scope Work Pattern

The 2026 retainer is appropriate where the client needs bounded support such as:

- short-form legal advice;
- regulatory issue spotting;
- MSB/RPAA/FINTRAC trigger analysis;
- digital-asset payment-flow characterization;
- review of business-model descriptions or regulatory positioning materials;
- ordinary bank-facing support materials;
- selected policy, terms, or standard-form documentation refinement;
- triage of whether a question should become a later separate-scope project.

## Out-of-Scope 2026 Client Profiles

The following client situations are not good fits for 2026 retainer intake:

- client is in an active regulatory exam, investigation, enforcement process, or
  remediation crisis;
- client needs immediate incident, breach, outage, de-risking, or emergency
  response;
- client is launching a new product and needs launch counsel rather than bounded
  advisory support;
- client is entering a new jurisdiction and needs full market-entry counsel;
- client needs securities, investment-dealer, adviser, portfolio-manager, token
  issuance, or tax advice;
- client expects LL to run compliance operations or manage regulatory execution;
- client lacks an internal owner for compliance/product/business decisions;
- client resists payment in advance, usage caps, or separate-scope boundaries.

## 2026 ICP Map

| Broader F3 Profile | 2026 Treatment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Canadian fintech founder / operator | In scope if operating and not in crisis | Best fit where questions are recurring, bounded, and management owns execution |
| Established MSB / compliance head | In scope if advisory-only | Good fit for recurring FINTRAC/RPAA/banking questions; not for exam response or compliance outsourcing |
| Embedded finance operator | In scope if bounded advisory | Good fit for MSB/RPAA/bank-support questions; not for program redesign or launch execution |
| Crypto wallet / custody operator | Limited in scope | In scope for payments/MSB/RPAA/banking triage; securities/custody-regulatory work must be excluded or referred |
| Stablecoin infrastructure operator | Limited in scope | In scope only for payment-flow/MSB/RPAA characterization; securities/tax/token issuance issues excluded |
| Cross-border expansion VP | Usually defer / separate-scope | Full Canada market-entry or launch support is outside 2026 basic scope; narrow threshold advice may be considered |

## Intake Gate

A 2026 fintech retainer candidate should pass these gates before acceptance:

1. The client fits one of the in-scope business types.
2. The requested support is basic bounded advisory, not crisis, launch,
   enforcement, remediation, or embedded compliance work.
3. The client accepts the applicable weekly capacity limit.
4. The client pays monthly in advance.
5. The mandate mix leaves LL within the two-to-three-retainer operating band.
6. The work can be delivered without exceeding ML1's available weekly capacity.

## 2026 Default Decision

If there is doubt about whether a client is inside the 2026 scope, default to:

- accept only the bounded advisory component;
- carve out premium or urgent work;
- defer or waitlist non-basic work;
- refer securities/tax/specialist work;
- require ML1 approval before any exception.
