# Risk Register

## Summary

LLP-039 has two opposing risks:

1. Too few retainers creates existential business risk.
2. Too many retainers creates delivery, quality, scope, and capacity risk.

The current operating band is two to three active fintech retainer mandates.

## Risks

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Severity | Mitigation | Trigger |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| R-01 | Fewer than two active fintech retainers creates existential business risk | Medium | High | High | Prioritize qualified retainer conversion; protect renewals; review pipeline weekly while below two | Active fintech retainer count is 0 or 1 |
| R-02 | More than three concurrent retainers overwhelms ML1 capacity | Medium | High | High | Hard cap at three under solo-owner model; waitlist, defer, offboard, or add support before accepting a fourth | Proposed mandate would create four active retainers |
| R-03 | Mandate mix exceeds weekly capacity even if client count is acceptable | Medium | High | High | Test every mandate mix against weekly capacity before acceptance | Mix exceeds approximately 20 hours/week promised capacity |
| R-04 | CAD 20,000 tier is mistaken for broader scope rather than higher weekly capacity | Medium | High | High | Engagement language must state same scope envelope as CAD 12,000, with 10-hour weekly cap | Client expects crisis, launch, exam, remediation, or embedded compliance support inside retainer |
| R-05 | CAD 8,000 tier is sold before scope and capacity are defined | Medium | Medium | Medium | Do not sell Tier 1 until scope/capacity are approved | Proposal or sales conversation treats CAD 8,000 tier as available |
| R-06 | Scope leakage converts retainers into unpaid separate-scope work | High | High | High | Request triage; separate-scope triggers; overage approval before work; monthly usage review | Repeated work outside defined scope or weekly cap |

| R-12 | Broader F3 ICP produces clients outside 2026 delivery capacity | Medium | Medium | Medium | Apply `CLIENT_SCOPE_2026.md` before accepting a retainer | Candidate fits marketing ICP but not 2026 delivery scope |

## Control Rules

- Below two active fintech retainers: acquisition priority increases.
- At two active fintech retainers: stabilize and continue selective acquisition.
- At three active fintech retainers: stop default acquisition; only replace,
  waitlist, or accept if a current mandate is ending.
- Above three active fintech retainers: not approved under current solo-owner
  model.
- Any CAD 20,000 mandate must be monitored as a 10-hour/week capacity
  commitment, not merely as a higher-price version of the CAD 12,000 mandate.
- Through December 31, 2026, basic bounded advisory scope is the default
  maximum delivery scope.

## Monthly Review Questions

1. Are there at least two active fintech retainers?
2. Are there more than three active fintech retainers?
3. What is the current mandate mix?
4. How many weekly hours are promised across the mandate mix?
5. How many weekly hours were actually used by each mandate?
6. Which requests should have been separate-scoped?
7. Which mandate, if any, should be repriced, narrowed, paused, or offboarded?
8. Is the pipeline strong enough to replace a lost retainer without panic intake?
