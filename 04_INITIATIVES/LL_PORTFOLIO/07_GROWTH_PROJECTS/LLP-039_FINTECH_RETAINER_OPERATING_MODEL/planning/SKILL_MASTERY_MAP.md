# Skill Mastery Map

## Purpose

Identify the skills Levine Law must master to make the fintech retainer both valuable to clients and economically safe for the firm.

Skills are divided into:
1. Core retainer skills - included or routinely available within the bounded monthly advisory model.
2. Premium adjacent skills - strategically important, but generally separate-scope work.
3. Operating-model skills - internal capabilities needed to protect the economics.

---

## A. Core Retainer Skills

These skills support the monthly advisory retainer if bounded by capacity and scope.

| Skill | What LL Must Master | Output Type |
|---|---|---|
| Payments regulatory issue spotting | Identify legal/regulatory touchpoints in client payment flows | Short-form advice, issue list |
| FINTRAC trigger analysis | Analyze STR, LCTR, EFTR, TPR, registration, KYC, and recordkeeping trigger questions based on client facts | Email advice, short memo |
| RPAA perimeter triage | Identify whether activities may fall inside payment-function or PSP analysis | Issue analysis, classification note |
| Digital asset business-model characterization | Characterize crypto/digital asset activities for financial-services regulatory touchpoints | Risk-framing note |
| Banking relationship support | Prepare legal/regulatory positioning for bank onboarding and account maintenance | Business model description, bank-facing memo |
| Commercial and banking agreement review | Review agreements for regulatory, operational, termination, compliance, and risk allocation issues | Mark-up, comments, issue list |
| Policy and terms refinement | Improve selected policies, terms, and standard forms for regulatory alignment | Drafting comments, revised clauses |
| Short-form legal writing | Deliver concise, high-value answers without overbuilding bespoke memos | Email, 1-3 page issue analysis |
| Regulatory risk framing | Translate uncertain regulatory facts into practical legal risk categories | Risk register, decision note |

## B. Premium Adjacent Skills

These are strategically important because they create high-value adjacent work, but they should generally trigger separate scope, additional fee, or a standalone project.

| Skill | Why It Matters | Default Commercial Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Regulatory exams and supervisory response | High stakes, timeline pressure, document intensity, regulator interface | Separate scope |
| Enforcement and exposure management | Adversarial or quasi-adversarial; high risk; bespoke strategy | Separate scope |
| Remediation planning | Can consume sustained operational/legal capacity | Separate scope |
| Incident and crisis management | Urgent, unpredictable, high-intensity work | Separate scope / premium response fee |
| Safeguarding breach response | Material client/end-user funds risk and regulator/bank exposure | Separate scope |
| Operational outage / third-party failure response | Legal, operational, contractual, and client communications complexity | Separate scope |
| Banking de-risking response | Time-sensitive, existential client risk | Separate scope |
| New product launch review | Cross-functional, fact-heavy, often changes regulatory characterization | Separate scope |
| New jurisdiction entry | Requires jurisdiction-specific analysis and often external counsel | Separate scope |
| Material business-model change | Rewrites prior risk assumptions and classification analysis | Separate scope |
| RPAA safeguarding design | Essential for PSPs that hold, settle, clear, or control funds; requires structure-level advice | Separate scope |
| Client funds flow mapping | Necessary for safeguarding, settlement, clearing, insolvency, and trust/beneficial interest analysis | Separate scope unless narrow triage |
| Bank/deposit-taking perimeter analysis | High consequence where product design approaches deposit-taking or stored-value risk | Separate scope |
| Securities perimeter analysis | Excluded from standard retainer; requires securities-law treatment | Excluded / separate counsel unless ML1 approves |
| Tax analysis | Excluded from standard retainer | Excluded / external tax counsel |

## C. RPAA Safeguarding Skill Cluster

This is a priority premium skill cluster because fintech PSPs that hold, settle, clear, or otherwise control funds may need advice that exceeds ordinary retainer triage.

| Sub-Skill | Mastery Requirement | Retainer Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Funds flow mapping | Identify who receives, holds, controls, settles, clears, or instructs movement of funds | Narrow triage included; full map separate |
| End-user funds characterization | Distinguish operational funds, client/end-user funds, settlement funds, reserves, and float | Separate if structure-level |
| Safeguarding obligation analysis | Analyze whether safeguarding concepts apply and what legal risk follows | Separate if beyond issue spotting |
| Account structure review | Review bank/FI account setup, pooling, segregation, beneficial interest, and reconciliation concepts | Separate scope |
| Insolvency and claims risk framing | Identify what happens to funds if PSP, bank, processor, or intermediary fails | Separate scope |
| Clearing and settlement chain analysis | Understand intermediary roles and timing gaps in settlement/clearing | Separate scope |
| Safeguarding policy review | Review client policies for alignment with legal/regulatory expectations | Separate unless narrow comments |
| Bank-facing safeguarding explanation | Prepare materials explaining the client's funds handling and control model | Separate if bank-critical |

## D. Operating-Model Skills

These are internal skills LL must master to make the mandate mix profitable.

| Skill | Operating Requirement |
|---|---|
| Scope triage | Classify each request as included, overage, deferred, or separate-scope within one intake pass |
| Time-boxed advice | Deliver useful answers within controlled time budgets |
| Template reuse | Use standard issue maps, memo shells, mark-up checklists, and client update formats |
| Overage conversion | Turn scope leakage into approved additional fees instead of unpaid work |
| Renewal review | Evaluate each client for yield, behavior, scope discipline, strategic value, and concentration risk |
| Client education | Teach clients what belongs inside the retainer and what requires separate scope |
| Knowledge capture | Convert recurring questions into reusable internal playbooks |
| Mandate-mix capacity measurement | Track actual time against each mandate type's weekly cap and against total ML1 weekly capacity |

## E. Mastery Sequence

| Phase | Focus | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Core retainer triage and short-form advice | CAD 12,000 and CAD 20,000 mandates can be delivered safely inside weekly caps |
| Phase 2 | Template and checklist standardization | Actual usage trends below promised weekly capacity where possible |
| Phase 3 | RPAA / FINTRAC / banking premium-scope conversion | Excluded work becomes monetizable rather than absorbed |
| Phase 4 | Safeguarding and funds-flow deepening | LL can price higher-value PSP funds-flow and safeguarding projects |
| Phase 5 | Renewal/offboarding discipline | Portfolio remains high-quality despite concentration risk |
