# Problem Statement

Levine Law's managing lawyer has limited billable capacity: approximately 20 billable hours per week. At the current nominal law-firm rate of CAD 480/hour, the firm's annual owner-billed capacity is approximately CAD 499,200 before realization and collections leakage.

The firm has identified a potentially superior model: fintech clients may buy
monthly recurring advisory mandates for payments, digital asset, FINTRAC, RPAA,
banking, and regulatory-risk advisory support.

The current mandate set has three price points: CAD 8,000/month, CAD
12,000/month, and CAD 20,000/month. The CAD 8,000/month scope remains to be
determined. The CAD 12,000/month mandate uses the core bounded fintech advisory
scope with a maximum of 6 hours/week. The CAD 20,000/month mandate uses the
same scope envelope as the CAD 12,000/month mandate, but with a maximum of 10
hours/week.

The economic challenge is to determine the number and mix of mandates that can
be concurrently sustained within ML1's approximately 20 billable hours/week
capacity while preserving scope discipline, client value, effective yield, and
room for non-retainer work.

Without a governed operating model, the retainer may drift into:
- uncapped fractional general counsel service;
- embedded compliance function;
- client management or operations support;
- regulatory exam, incident, launch, or remediation work without separate fees;
- slow-payment working capital exposure; and
- concentration risk without renewal and offboarding discipline.

## Core Problem

LL needs to convert fintech regulatory expertise into repeatable, bounded,
high-value recurring advisory mandates while protecting owner capacity and
preserving premium economics across the mandate mix.
