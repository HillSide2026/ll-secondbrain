# LLP-038 — Delivery Spine

## Purpose

Define and build the operational structure through which Levine Law delivers legal services: a standardized, templated workflow from intake through output and close, scoped in hours per service type, and built from the ground up to support supervised delegation to an associate lawyer.

The Delivery Spine is the structural precondition for:
- credible leverage (LLP-033 associate model)
- quality-consistent delivery at volume
- the 2027 associate hire decision (which requires demonstrably repeatable, spec-able, reviewable work before hiring is justified)
- eventual firm assetization and the equity sale objective (LLP-030 Priority 6)

Without the Spine:
- hiring will reduce income rather than increase it
- F02 cannot be delegated even partially
- unit economics under leverage cannot be measured
- the Path A vs Path B decision in 2028 will be made blind

---

## What the Delivery Spine Is

The Spine is a four-stage structure that applies consistently across all service lines:

```
Intake → Diagnosis → Output → Close
```

| Stage | What it defines |
|---|---|
| **Intake** | Qualification gate, engagement scope, hours budgeted, matter setup, client communication template |
| **Diagnosis** | Structured fact-gathering and issue-identification protocol, per service type |
| **Output** | Deliverable definition, template set, review checklist, ML1 sign-off gate |
| **Close** | Reporting letter, billing trigger, client debrief structure, follow-on conversion logic |

Each stage must be:
- **bounded** — hours capped and documented
- **templated** — standard documents and checklists exist
- **reviewable** — ML1 can QA in ≤20% of original delivery time
- **delegable** — associate can execute with defined supervision protocol

---

## Scope

### In Scope

- Delivery Spine definition for F02 (Corporate Health Check, all three tiers)
- Delivery Spine definition for standard F01 corporate/commercial matter types (the repeatable subset)
- Delivery Spine definition for early F03 entry matters (MSB registration, RPAA registration)
- Hours budget and scope cap per service type
- Template inventory and gap analysis (what exists vs. what is needed)
- Supervision and review protocol for each stage
- Delegation eligibility classification per matter type

### Out of Scope

- F01 reactive matters that are non-repeatable or one-off
- Litigation support or adversarial matters
- Actual associate hiring (governed by LLP-033)
- Marketing or acquisition architecture (governed by LLP-011, LLP-012, LLP-013)
