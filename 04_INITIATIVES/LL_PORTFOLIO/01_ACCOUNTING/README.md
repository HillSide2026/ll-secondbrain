# 01_ACCOUNTING — Accounting

## Purpose

Govern Levine Law accounting work across bookkeeping, management accounting, and budgeting boundaries.

## Scope

Accounting has three distinct layers:

1. **Bookkeeping** - what was booked, billed, collected, reconciled, paid, or held in trust.
2. **Management accounting** - internal analysis of how accounting facts behave as a law-firm business system.
3. **Budgeting** - approved baseline, targets, scenarios, and forecast/control models.

### In Scope

- Bookkeeping records
- Historical financial statements
- Reconciliations
- Invoices, payments, expense logs
- Management accounting lenses, including revenue security, recurring vs one-off revenue, revenue timing, trust-funded revenue vs unsecured AR, matter-level profitability, lawyer production, WIP conversion, and collection reliability
- Budgeting boundaries and accounting control handoffs

### Out of Scope

- Pricing logic
- Recommendations
- Optimization
- Unsupported assumptions about the future
- Treating management accounting or budgeting artifacts as bookkeeping records

## ML1 Authority Statement

ML1 is the sole authority for any interpretation or decision derived from accounting records.

**Rule:** Bookkeeping, management accounting, and budgeting must be labelled and kept distinct.

## Explicit Prohibitions

The System must NOT:

- Treat bookkeeping records as strategy by implication
- Treat management accounting classifications as legal/accounting conclusions without ML1 review
- Recommend pricing based on costs
- Optimize based on accounting patterns
- Mix bookkeeping facts, management accounting interpretation, and budgeting models without labelling the layer

## Critical Boundary

- **Bookkeeping = records and historical facts**
- **Management accounting = internal classification and performance analysis**
- **Budgeting = approved baseline, targets, scenarios, and forward controls**
- **They may connect, but they must be labelled and not collapsed into one another.**
