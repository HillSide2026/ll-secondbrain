# Management Accounting — Revenue Timing and Security Taxonomy

## Purpose

Classify law-firm revenue by the sequence of work, invoicing, payment, security, and trust treatment.

This taxonomy is not a chart-of-accounts classification. It is a management accounting lens for understanding revenue security, revenue timing, cash risk, and collection reliability.

## Taxonomy

| Type | Sequence | Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unsecured post-pay | Work -> invoice -> client pays | Highest | Classic AR risk. Work is done before payment is secured. |
| Secured post-pay | Work -> invoice -> payment secured/committed | Medium | Could be strong client, payment arrangement, card authorization, PO, or reliable payer. |
| Trust-funded earned fees | Client pays trust -> work -> invoice -> transfer from trust | Lower | Better cash security, but trust rules control timing of transfer. |
| Recurring / subscription-like | Client pays before work | Lowest cash risk; professionally risky | If for future legal services or unbilled fees, generally trust money, not operating revenue. |

## Boundary Notes

- Accounting records show what was booked, billed, collected, transferred, or held in trust.
- This taxonomy classifies the revenue model behind those records.
- A payment received before legal work is completed and billed may reduce cash risk, but it does not automatically become operating revenue.
- Trust-funded revenue must be analyzed separately from operating cash because legal/professional rules control when funds may be moved from trust to operating.

## Use

Use this taxonomy when interpreting actuals by matter or client, especially where Q1 2026 actuals show insufficient and uneven revenue conversion.

The target question is not just "how much revenue was booked?" but "what kind of revenue sequence produced it, and how secure was conversion into collected operating cash?"
