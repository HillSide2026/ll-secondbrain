## Top 5 Risks
1. Strategic risk: financial-risk tracking may duplicate or conflict with LLP-001 accounting or LLP-002 budgeting instead of complementing them.
2. Scope risk: the project may drift into autonomous financial decision-making, pricing, or profitability declarations.
3. Schedule risk: risk review cadence may lag behind actual portfolio changes and limit usefulness.
4. Budget risk: the project may demand more monitoring overhead than the firm can sustain.
5. Financial risk: thresholds may create false precision if source data and assumptions are weak.

## Key Assumptions
- Core financial exposures can be described without replacing accounting or budget artifacts.
- ML1 will remain the approval authority for interpretation, prioritization, and response.
- Inputs from LLP-001 and LLP-002 can be assembled consistently enough to support risk review.
- Risk escalation can stay focused on decision value rather than reporting overhead.
- Financial risk visibility will improve portfolio judgment even before full planning artifacts exist.
