# Scope Statement
## MSB Registration Mandate (TM)

## 1. Purpose of Engagement
The MSB Registration Mandate (TM) is a structured regulatory classification and registration support engagement designed for Canadian payment platforms, remittance businesses, and crypto-enabled operators evaluating or undertaking registration as a Money Services Business (MSB).

The objective is to provide:
- Clear activity classification
- Defensible registration analysis
- Structured filing support
- Governance designation alignment

This engagement focuses on regulatory positioning and registration architecture under the framework administered by FINTRAC.

## 2. Complimentary Initial Consultation
Prior to engagement, Levine Law offers a no-obligation consultation call.

Purpose of this call:
- High-level review of the client's business model
- Preliminary discussion of potential MSB triggers
- Clarification of regulatory exposure areas
- Determination of engagement suitability

This call does not constitute formal legal advice and does not create a solicitor-client relationship unless and until a formal engagement agreement is executed.

## 3. Core Scope of Services
The MSB Registration Mandate (TM) includes the following components:

### A. Business Activity Mapping and Classification Analysis
Review of client's service lines and transaction flows.
Identification of activities potentially triggering MSB registration.
Analysis of foreign exchange, fund transfer, and virtual currency exposure.
Assessment of intermediary or PSP role implications.

Deliverable: Written classification summary memorandum outlining whether MSB registration is required and the basis for the determination.

### B. Registration Strategy and Filing Support
If registration is required:
Identification of applicable MSB activity categories.
Guidance on information required for registration submission.
Structured support through the FINTRAC portal filing process.
Review of submitted registration information for completeness and consistency.

### C. Governance and Designation Alignment
Guidance on appointment of representative.
Guidance on appointment of Compliance Officer.
Identification of required ownership and control disclosures.
Overview of governance reporting expectations.

This component ensures registration aligns with internal accountability architecture.

### D. Illustrative Next Steps (Post-Registration Overview)
High-level discussion of:
AML program requirements.
Risk assessment obligations.
Ongoing reporting responsibilities.
Examination preparedness considerations.

This overview is intended to contextualize registration within the broader compliance lifecycle.

## 4. Deliverables
At completion of the engagement, the client receives:
- Written MSB Classification Memorandum
- Registration pathway outline
- Filing support documentation guidance
- Governance designation summary
- Next-step compliance roadmap overview

## 5. Exclusions
Unless otherwise agreed in writing, this engagement does not include:
- Drafting a full AML compliance program
- Acting as Compliance Officer
- Ongoing compliance monitoring
- Representation in enforcement proceedings
- Tax advice
- Banking negotiations
- Cross-border licensing outside Canada

These services may be addressed under separate engagement.

## 6. Client Responsibilities
Client agrees to:
- Provide accurate and complete information regarding business operations
- Disclose all relevant service lines and transaction flows
- Identify all ownership and control stakeholders
- Participate in reasonable follow-up clarification discussions

Classification accuracy depends on operational transparency.

## 7. Fee Structure
Professional fees for the MSB Registration Mandate (TM) are determined based on:
- Number of service lines
- Complexity of transaction flows
- Crypto or cross-border exposure
- Ownership and governance structure
- Urgency of filing

A fixed-fee quote will be provided following the initial consultation call.

## 8. Engagement Outcome
This engagement is designed to deliver:
- Regulatory clarity
- Defensible classification documentation
- Structured registration execution
- Governance alignment

The objective is not merely to complete a filing, but to establish a sound regulatory foundation for operational growth.

## Closing Positioning Line
MSB registration is not a formality.
It is a regulatory classification decision with operational consequences.
The MSB Registration Mandate (TM) provides structured clarity at the outset.
