# The Canadian MSB Registration Primer (TM)
## A Structured Overview for Payment Platforms, Remittance Businesses, and Crypto-Enabled Operators

## Introduction
In Canada, businesses that provide certain payment, foreign exchange, or virtual currency services may be required to register as a Money Services Business (MSB).

Registration is not optional where triggered.

However, classification is not always straightforward.

This Primer provides a structured overview of:
- What constitutes an MSB under Canadian law
- Which activity lines trigger registration
- What the registration lifecycle looks like
- Operational considerations before and after filing

It is intended as a knowledge resource for founders, executives, and compliance leads evaluating their regulatory position.

## 1. MSB and FINTRAC Overview
Canada's anti-money laundering reporting framework is administered by FINTRAC.

Under Canadian law, a business is considered a Money Services Business if it engages in certain prescribed financial service activities as a business.

These activities include:
- Foreign exchange dealing
- Money transferring (domestic or international)
- Issuing or redeeming money orders or similar instruments
- Dealing in virtual currency

Registration is triggered by activity, not company size.

A startup processing limited volume may still meet the definition.

Likewise, a technology platform that intermediates payment flows may fall within scope depending on how funds are handled.

The critical question is not what the company calls itself - but how money moves through its systems.

## 2. Money Services Business Types
This section breaks down the common activity lines that trigger registration.

### A. Foreign Exchange Dealing
Registration may be required where a business:
- Exchanges one currency for another
- Facilitates conversion through its own platform
- Charges spread or commission on exchange

Even where currency exchange is embedded within a broader payment function, registration may still be triggered.

### B. Fund Transfers
Money transfer services are broadly defined and include:
- Domestic fund transmission
- Cross-border remittance
- Facilitating peer-to-peer transfers
- Acting as an intermediary in the movement of funds

The definition may capture:
- Payment facilitators
- PSPs
- Remittance operators
- Platforms holding funds prior to payout

### C. Virtual Currency Dealing
Registration may apply where a business:
- Exchanges virtual currency for fiat
- Transfers virtual currency on behalf of others
- Provides crypto-fiat conversion services
- Operates custodial wallet functions

Crypto activity is assessed based on functional involvement in value transfer, not simply software provision.

## 3. Registration Timeline and Governance Architecture
Registration is not a single filing event. It is a structured lifecycle.

### Phase 1 - Pre-Registration Assessment
- Activity classification
- Service mapping
- Determining MSB status
- Internal readiness review

This is where misclassification risk is highest.

### Phase 2 - Registration Filing
- Submission through FINTRAC portal
- Disclosure of business model
- Identification of services offered
- Ownership and control disclosure

Accuracy at this stage is critical.

### Phase 3 - Appointment of Representative
An MSB must designate:
- A representative authorized to liaise with FINTRAC
- Individuals responsible for compliance communication

Clarity in designation supports regulatory responsiveness.

### Phase 4 - Compliance Officer Appointment
A registered MSB must appoint a Compliance Officer responsible for:
- AML program oversight
- Risk assessment
- Reporting obligations
- Ongoing monitoring

This role must be formally documented.

### Phase 5 - Governance and Board Reporting
Where applicable:
- Board or senior management must approve AML framework
- Periodic reporting must occur
- Program reviews must be documented

Registration triggers ongoing governance obligations.

## 4. Common Misunderstandings
Short clarifying section.

- "We only process payments - we're not an MSB."
- "Our bank handles compliance."
- "We're just a tech platform."
- "We haven't launched yet, so registration doesn't apply."

Registration analysis turns on operational reality.

Misclassification can create:
- Banking friction
- Investor due diligence issues
- Regulatory exposure

## 5. Illustrative Next Steps
Organizations evaluating MSB status may consider:
- Reviewing their flow-of-funds architecture
- Mapping service lines against statutory definitions
- Documenting classification reasoning
- Confirming whether virtual currency triggers apply
- Assessing internal compliance readiness

Where classification is unclear, structured analysis can provide clarity and defensibility.
