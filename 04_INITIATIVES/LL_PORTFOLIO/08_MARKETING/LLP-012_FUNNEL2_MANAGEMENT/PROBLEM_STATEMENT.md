# Problem Statement

Project ID: LLP-26-25
Project Path: 08_MARKETING/LLP-012_FUNNEL2_MANAGEMENT

## Current Problem
- Funnel 02 is defined as planned future state but lacks finalized initiation execution controls.
- The corporate-law growth funnel for businesses with more than `$1M` annual cash flow is not yet operationalized at project level.
- relocation from `levinelegal.ca` to `levine-law.ca` is not yet locked as part of Funnel 02 execution.
- Funnel-specific website implementation for `levine-law.ca` is not yet formalized.
- higher-quality Google Ads traffic strategy is not yet defined.
- authority-content / SEO production is not yet organized as a governed Funnel 02 acquisition lane.
- the overlap between Funnel 2 acquisition and NDA Esq landing-page traffic on `levine-law.ca` is not yet bounded.
- Associate listing requirements for website and landing page are not yet operationalized.
- intake reporting is not yet consistent enough to support reliable decisions.
- intake and conversion metric thresholds are not yet specified.
- setter role design is not yet operationalized.
- senior-lawyer staffing for legal delivery is not yet designed into the funnel model.

## Impact
- Continued dependence on reactive Funnel 01 behavior.
- Delay in transition to maturity-led intake and conversion quality.
- No clearly governed acquisition path for growth-stage corporate-law demand.
- Website and traffic surfaces remain fragmented between domains and offers.
- High-intent growth-stage prospects may be lost because setter and senior-lawyer handoff design is incomplete.
- Unclear launch readiness thresholds.
- Inconsistent public-facing implementation across website and landing assets.
- Inability to evaluate performance against defined intake and conversion targets.

## Why Now
- Funnel 02 is the intended replacement path for Funnel 01 future state.
- Formal initiation is required before planning and controlled launch decisions.
- the domain, traffic, intake, staffing, and reporting model now need to be locked together rather than treated as separate fixes.
