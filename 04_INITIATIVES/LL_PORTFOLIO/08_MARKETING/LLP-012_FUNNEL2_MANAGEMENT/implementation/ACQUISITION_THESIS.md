# Acquisition Thesis — Funnel 02

## What This Document Is

The acquisition thesis explains how and why Funnel 02 targets are acquired — the trigger events, psychographic framing, and conversion logic that turns a qualified prospect into a client. This is distinct from the ICP (`target.yaml`), which defines who the client is.

---

## Maturity Triggers

These are the events that bring a qualified prospect into the funnel:

- Accountant flags governance risk
- Investor discussion introduces scrutiny
- Contractor-to-employee transition increases liability exposure
- Growth stresses the original shareholder structure
- Bank or partner requests updated documentation
- Preparing for expansion or financing

---

## Psychographic Profile

**Thoughts at entry:**
- We have grown beyond what we set up originally.
- I am not sure our agreements would hold up.
- If something goes wrong, we are exposed.
- I do not want to find out the hard way.

**Traits:**
- Commercial
- Time-sensitive
- Risk-aware
- Protective of margin
- Not shopping for the cheapest option

**Values:**
- Clarity
- Structure
- Prioritization
- Calm authority

**Avoids:**
- Litigation theater
- Alarmism
- Legal lectures

---

## Economic Buyer Threshold

**Can:**
- Approve $10K remediation work without installment friction
- Sustain a recurring fractional counsel relationship
- Pay for prevention

**Cannot be:**
- Cash-flow unstable
- Insolvency-adjacent
- Panic buyers seeking quick letters

---

## What They Are Actually Buying

- Structural stability
- Governance confidence
- Risk prioritization
- An ongoing commercial legal partner

---

## Conversion Basis

Maturity-based, not panic-based. The buyer is not in crisis — they have reached a point of operational complexity where doing nothing feels riskier than acting.

---

## Export Sub-Segment

Exporters are not a separate ICP — they are a targetable sub-segment within Funnel 02. Cross-border/multi-jurisdiction exposure is already a mandatory operational characteristic in `target.yaml`. The acquisition question is whether businesses actively preparing to export can be reached before they have sorted their legal exposure.

**Targetability: Yes, with effort.** Acquisition lanes:

- **EDC (Export Development Canada)** — programs, events, partner relationships
- **Ontario trade programs** — Ontario Made, Trade Accelerator Program (TAP), Ontario Chamber of Commerce export networks
- **Accountant referrals** — accountants who serve exporters are a high-fit referral source and reinforce the accountant channel generally
- **Google Ads** — keywords around cross-border contracts, Canada–US agreements, and export compliance are acquirable
- **Trade associations** — sector-specific organizations (food exporters, manufacturers associations) within the three Funnel 02 industries

**Limitation:** There is no list of companies planning to export. They are reached through the channels above or through content that speaks directly to export legal risk.

**Content angle:** Canada–US cross-border agreement risk, export contract structuring, distribution agreement exposure.

---

## Content and Channel Requirements

Entry content must be:
- Plain-English guidance
- Clear framing of what corporate legal hygiene means
- Sequencing of what matters now vs. later
- Calm authority (not alarmist, not academic)
- Visible business literacy (revenue, margin, contracts — not just statutes)
