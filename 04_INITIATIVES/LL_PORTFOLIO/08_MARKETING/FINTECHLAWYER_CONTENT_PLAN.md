# fintechlawyer.ca — Five-Vertical Content Plan

fintechlawyer.ca publishes authority content across five financial services verticals.
Each vertical gets one pillar page and a supporting cluster. Content must map to a
defined vertical before production. No orphan content.

Controlling strategy doc: `FINTECHLAWYER_AUTHORITY_SITE.md`
Domain owner: Levine Professional Corporation

## Vertical 2: Lending

**Target reader:** Private lenders, alternative finance operators, mortgage investment
corporation (MIC) founders, fintech lending platform operators, and family offices
deploying capital through lending vehicles.

**Regulatory hook:** There is no single federal lending license in Canada. Regulation is
provincial and context-dependent. The consumer/commercial distinction, PPSA security
registration, FSRA mortgage oversight, and MIC structure under the Income Tax Act are
the practical regulatory surfaces where operators get caught.

**Pillar:** Private and Alternative Lending in Canada: The Operator's Regulatory Guide

## Vertical 3: Insurance

**Target reader:** InsurTech founders, managing general agents (MGAs), embedded insurance
platform operators, Lloyd's coverholders, and technology companies whose platforms touch
insurance distribution.

**Regulatory hook:** Insurance regulation in Canada is provincial. FSRA governs Ontario.
The InsurTech space is dense with companies that either need a license and do not have
one, or that have structured embedded insurance arrangements without understanding the
distribution licensing implications.

## Vertical 4: Investment

**Target reader:** Investment platform founders, portfolio managers, exempt market dealers,
investment fund managers, wealth tech operators, and family offices with investable capital
above the threshold where informal management raises registration questions.

**Regulatory hook:** Securities regulation in Canada is provincial, governed by NI 31-103.
The "in the business" test is the central tension: platforms that aggregate investor capital,
provide investment advice at scale, or create pooled structures may be caught without
realizing it. Registration category (PM, EMD, IFM) determines what you can do.

**Pillar:** Investment Management and Securities Registration in Canada: The Operator's Guide

## Vertical 5: Crypto

**Target reader:** Crypto trading platform operators, token issuers, DeFi protocol
operators, OTC crypto desk operators, crypto fund managers, and custody providers
operating in or targeting the Canadian market.

**Regulatory hook:** Canada was among the first jurisdictions to require crypto trading
platform registration. The OSC and CIRO have issued guidance, enforcement actions, and
pre-registration undertaking frameworks. The core tension is token classification: is
this a security? That question determines whether OSC or CIRO has jurisdiction and what
registration category applies.

**Scope note:** Crypto+payments overlap (MSB classification for VASPs, stablecoin AML,
on-chain transaction monitoring) is addressed in Vertical 1 (Payments), not here.
This vertical covers exchange regulation, token classification, and capital markets
structure for crypto businesses.

**Pillar:** Canadian Crypto Regulation: Exchange Registration, Token Classification, and Compliance
