# FINTRAC MSB Registration Form: What Information Is Required

The FINTRAC MSB registration form is a structured disclosure of how a business operates.

It is not a simple administrative form. It requires a clear articulation of the business model.

## Core Information Categories

The form requires disclosure across several areas:

- business identity
- ownership and control
- nature of activities
- geographic scope

Each section must align with how the business actually functions.

## Business Activities Description

This is one of the most important sections.

The form requires:

- description of services provided
- explanation of how transactions occur
- identification of MSB activities

Generic descriptions often create issues.

The expectation is functional clarity.

## Ownership and Control Disclosure

FINTRAC requires:

- directors and officers
- beneficial owners
- ownership percentages

This becomes more complex for venture-backed or international structures.

Clarity here is essential.

## Geographic Scope

The business must disclose:

- where it operates
- where clients are located
- jurisdictions involved in transactions

Cross-border activity should be described explicitly.

## Compliance Program Confirmation

The form requires confirmation that:

- a compliance officer is appointed
- an AML program exists

While detailed review happens later, inconsistencies here can create downstream problems.

## The Practical Takeaway

The MSB registration form is not about checking boxes.

It is about presenting a coherent, accurate description of the business.

Where that description is clear, the process is straightforward.

Where it is not, issues surface quickly.
