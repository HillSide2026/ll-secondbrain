# How to Map a Payment Flow for MSB Analysis (Canada)

Most MSB classification errors come from describing products, not analyzing flows.

Regulators do not assess what a business calls itself. They assess how money actually moves.

## Step 1: Identify Where Funds Enter the System

Start with:

- who provides funds
- how funds are received
- which entity receives them

This defines the entry point of regulatory exposure.

## Step 2: Trace Movement of Funds

Map:

- each step in the transaction
- all intermediaries
- how funds are routed

The goal is to understand the full lifecycle, not just endpoints.

## Step 3: Identify Control Points

At each step, determine:

- who decides where funds go
- who controls timing
- who can block or modify transactions

Control is the core regulatory signal.

## Step 4: Assess Custody vs Influence

Determine whether the business:

- holds funds directly
- controls access to funds
- influences movement without custody

All three can be relevant.

## Step 5: Map Third-Party Roles

Identify:

- banks
- processors
- payment partners

Then assess whether they:

- replace the business's role
- or simply execute its instructions

This distinction is critical.

## Step 6: Overlay Regulatory Triggers

Finally, assess whether the flow involves:

- money transmission
- foreign exchange
- virtual currency activity

If yes, MSB analysis applies.

## Where This Process Matters

This exercise is essential where:

- business models are evolving
- multiple parties are involved
- classification is unclear
- external stakeholders require answers

## The Practical Takeaway

MSB classification cannot be determined from product descriptions alone.

It requires mapping how funds actually move and identifying where control sits within that flow.

That analysis produces more reliable answers than any label.
