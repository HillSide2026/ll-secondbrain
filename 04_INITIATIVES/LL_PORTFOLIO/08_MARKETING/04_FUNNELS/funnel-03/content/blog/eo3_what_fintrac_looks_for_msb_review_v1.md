# What Does FINTRAC Look for in an MSB Compliance Review

A FINTRAC compliance review is not an abstract audit of policies. It is an assessment of whether an MSB's AML program works in practice.

The distinction matters. Most deficiencies arise not from missing documents, but from misalignment between documentation and actual operations.

## The Core Question FINTRAC Is Asking

At a high level, the review asks:

Does this business understand its risks, and is it managing them effectively?

Everything else flows from that.

## Policies and Procedures: Do They Reflect Reality?

FINTRAC will review written policies and procedures.

The focus is not on whether they exist, but whether they:

- accurately describe the business model
- reflect actual transaction flows
- are being followed in practice

Generic policies are a common issue. If the documentation could apply to any MSB, it usually does not meet expectations.

## Risk Assessment: Is It Specific and Current?

Risk assessments are examined for:

- relevance to the business model
- identification of real risk factors
- alignment with controls

Outdated or overly high-level assessments are a recurring problem.

If the product has evolved but the risk assessment has not, that gap is visible.

## Transaction Monitoring: Does It Work

Monitoring systems are evaluated based on:

- how rules are designed
- whether alerts are generated appropriately
- how alerts are reviewed and resolved

FINTRAC is not looking for perfection. It is looking for a system that is:

- reasonable
- risk-based
- consistently applied

Where monitoring appears superficial or disconnected from actual activity, it becomes a focus.

## Suspicious Transaction Reporting: Judgment and Consistency

STR processes are examined closely.

Key questions include:

- how suspicion is identified
- how decisions are documented
- whether reporting is timely

A common issue is under-reporting due to overly high internal thresholds.

Another is inconsistent reasoning across similar cases.

## Recordkeeping: Can Decisions Be Reconstructed

Recordkeeping is tested through traceability.

FINTRAC may:

- select transactions
- review how they were handled
- assess whether records support the outcome

If decisions cannot be reconstructed clearly, this is treated as a deficiency.

## Governance and Oversight

The role of the compliance officer is also reviewed.

This includes:

- whether the officer has real oversight
- how issues are escalated
- whether compliance is integrated into operations

A nominal compliance function with limited influence is a recurring weakness.

## Independent Review Quality

FINTRAC will look at prior independent reviews.

The focus is on:

- scope
- depth
- whether findings were addressed

Reviews that are purely formal or checklist-driven tend to carry less weight.

## The Pattern Behind Most Findings

Most deficiencies follow a consistent pattern:

- the business model has evolved
- the compliance program has not kept up
- documentation and operations diverge

This is not usually intentional. It is structural.

## The Practical Takeaway

FINTRAC is not testing whether an MSB understands AML theory.

It is testing whether the business has built a compliance system that reflects how it actually operates.

Where that alignment exists, reviews tend to be manageable.

Where it does not, issues surface quickly.
