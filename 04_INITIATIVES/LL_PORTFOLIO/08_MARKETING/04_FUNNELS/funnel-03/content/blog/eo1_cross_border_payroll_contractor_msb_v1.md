# Cross-Border Payroll and Contractor Payments: Do MSB Rules Apply

Cross-border payroll and contractor payment platforms often do not view themselves as money transmitters.

From a Canadian regulatory perspective, many of them are performing functions that fall within that category.

## Why Payroll Platforms Trigger MSB Analysis

These platforms typically:

- collect funds from an employer
- distribute funds to contractors or employees
- manage currency conversion
- coordinate international payouts

Functionally, this resembles money transmission.

The fact that the use case is payroll does not change the analysis.

## The Key Question: Who Moves the Money

The classification turns on whether the platform:

- receives funds
- controls how those funds are routed
- determines payout timing

If those elements are present, the platform may be performing MSB activity.

## FX as a Built-In Feature

Many payroll platforms include:

- currency conversion
- multi-currency wallets
- localized payouts

This introduces foreign exchange activity, which can independently trigger MSB obligations.

## Role of Banking Partners

These platforms often rely on:

- local payout partners
- banking infrastructure
- global payment networks

This does not automatically remove MSB exposure.

If the platform controls onboarding, payout logic, or transaction structure, it may still be within scope.

## Canadian Nexus

MSB obligations may apply where:

- contractors are located in Canada
- employers are based in Canada
- funds move into or out of Canada

This is frequently identified during expansion or banking onboarding.

## Where Misclassification Happens

The most common issue is treating payroll as an HR function rather than a payment function.

From a regulatory standpoint, the movement of funds is what matters.

## The Practical Takeaway

Cross-border payroll platforms are often engaged in money transmission, even if that is not how they describe themselves.

Where the platform controls the flow of funds, MSB analysis is required.
