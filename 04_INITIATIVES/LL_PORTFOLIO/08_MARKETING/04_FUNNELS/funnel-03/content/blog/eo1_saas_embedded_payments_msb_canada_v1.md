# Do SaaS Platforms with Embedded Payments Need to Register as MSBs in Canada?

SaaS platforms with embedded payments are not automatically required to register as money services businesses (MSBs) in Canada.

Some are. Many are not.

The distinction depends on whether the platform is simply enabling payments through third-party infrastructure or actively participating in the movement of funds.

## Why Embedded Payments Trigger MSB Analysis

Embedded payments typically involve:

- onboarding users or merchants
- enabling payment acceptance
- coordinating payouts or settlements

From a product perspective, this is feature expansion.

From a regulatory perspective, it may introduce money transmission activity.

The embedded nature of payments does not change the analysis. It often obscures it.

## The Core Question: Is the Platform Moving Money?

The classification turns on function.

If the platform:

- receives funds from one party
- controls how funds are routed
- determines how and when funds are paid out

…it may be engaged in MSB activity.

If it only integrates a payment provider and does not control these elements, it may fall outside scope.

## Where Embedded Models Cross the Line

The shift usually happens gradually.

A platform may start with simple payment acceptance via a processor, and then add:

- merchant onboarding
- payout scheduling
- fee splitting
- multi-party payments

Each addition increases control over the flow of funds.

At a certain point, the platform is no longer just enabling payments. It is structuring them.

## Reliance on Payment Processors

Most SaaS platforms rely on processors.

This creates a common assumption: "The processor handles compliance, so we don't need to."

That is only correct if the processor also controls the regulated activity.

If the platform:

- defines transaction logic
- controls onboarding
- determines payout structure

…the processor is executing, not replacing, the platform's role.

## Marketplaces vs SaaS Platforms

Many embedded payment models resemble marketplace flows.

If the platform:

- collects funds from customers
- distributes funds to multiple parties
- manages settlement logic

…the distinction between SaaS and marketplace becomes less relevant.

At that point, the analysis aligns with marketplace MSB considerations.

## Cross-Border and FX Considerations

Embedded payment platforms often expand into:

- international payments
- multi-currency support
- contractor or vendor payouts

These features introduce foreign exchange and cross-border elements, both of which can independently trigger MSB obligations.

## Where Misclassification Happens

The most common issue is treating payments as a feature rather than a function.

Product teams view embedded payments as an extension of software.

Regulators view them as participation in financial activity.

That gap leads to incorrect assumptions about regulatory scope.

## The Practical Takeaway

SaaS platforms with embedded payments are assessed based on how they handle the movement of funds, not how they describe their product.

Where the platform remains a technical layer, MSB registration may not be required.

Where it begins to control onboarding, routing, or settlement, it may fall within Canada's AML framework.

The distinction is structural, and it often becomes visible only when the payment flow is mapped in detail.
