# On-Chain vs Off-Chain Activity: Where AML Visibility Breaks

Crypto payment systems operate across two environments.

On-chain activity is recorded on a public ledger. Off-chain activity occurs within platforms, exchanges, or internal systems.

AML analysis depends on understanding how these environments interact.

## On-Chain Visibility

On-chain transactions provide transparency at the level of movement.

Transfers between addresses can be observed, and patterns can be identified over time.

What is often missing is attribution.

An address does not necessarily reveal the identity behind it. This limits the ability to interpret activity with confidence.

## Off-Chain Context

Off-chain systems provide the opposite.

They often contain user identity, account information, and transactional intent. What they may lack is full visibility into how funds moved before or after entering the system.

This creates a partial view.

## The Break in Continuity

The most significant issue arises at the boundary between these environments.

Funds move from on-chain to off-chain, or vice versa, and the continuity of information is interrupted.

What appears as a complete transaction in one environment may only represent part of a larger sequence.

## Interpreting Incomplete Data

AML decisions must often be made with incomplete information.

On-chain data may suggest patterns without identity. Off-chain data may provide identity without full transaction history.

The analysis requires combining these perspectives, even when they do not align perfectly.

## The Practical Takeaway

AML visibility in crypto systems is not absent. It is fragmented.

Understanding where that fragmentation occurs is critical to forming consistent and defensible decisions.

The challenge is not access to data. It is interpreting what is available within its limitations.
