# Crypto Payment Flows and MSB Classification in Canada

Crypto payment models often blur the boundaries that define regulatory classification.

Businesses may not view themselves as money transmitters, but their role in facilitating movement of value can bring them within scope.

## Function Over Label

Classification is based on what a business does, not how it describes itself.

If a platform:

- receives value from one party
- controls how that value is routed
- facilitates transfer to another party

it may be performing money services business activity.

This analysis applies regardless of whether the value is fiat or crypto.

## Hybrid Models

Many crypto payment systems operate as hybrids.

They combine elements of wallets, exchanges, and payment processors. Value may move between crypto and fiat, across platforms, and through multiple intermediaries.

This complexity can obscure where responsibility sits.

## Points of Control

The key factor is control.

If a business determines how transactions are structured, when they are executed, or how value is transferred, it is participating in the movement of funds.

Reliance on third-party infrastructure does not necessarily remove that role.

## Cross-Border Considerations

Crypto payment flows often involve cross-border activity.

This introduces additional regulatory considerations and may strengthen the connection to Canadian AML obligations, depending on how the system operates.

## The Practical Takeaway

Crypto does not create a separate classification framework.

It introduces new ways of performing existing activities.

Where those activities involve control over the movement of value, MSB analysis applies.

The challenge is identifying that role within complex and evolving payment structures.
