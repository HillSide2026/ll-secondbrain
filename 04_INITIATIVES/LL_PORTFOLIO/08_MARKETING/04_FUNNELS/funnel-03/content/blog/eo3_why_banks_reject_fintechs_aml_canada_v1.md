# Why Banks Reject Fintechs on AML Grounds (Canada)

Banks do not reject fintech companies because of isolated compliance gaps. They reject them when the overall risk profile cannot be clearly understood or managed.

AML is central to that assessment.

## The Bank's Starting Point

When evaluating a fintech, a bank is trying to determine how risk flows through the business.

This begins with understanding:

- how funds move
- who controls the flow
- how activity is monitored
- how decisions are made

If these elements are unclear, the bank cannot assess exposure.

## Lack of Clarity

One of the most common reasons for rejection is lack of clarity.

If a business cannot clearly describe its transaction flows or explain how its compliance system operates, the issue becomes structural.

Ambiguity is treated as risk.

Banks do not need certainty about every detail, but they require a coherent and consistent explanation of how the business functions.

## Misalignment Between Product and Compliance

Banks frequently identify gaps between:

- how the product operates
- how compliance is described
- how controls are applied

If these elements do not align, confidence in the system decreases.

This is particularly common in businesses that have evolved quickly without updating their compliance framework.

## Weak Monitoring and Decision-Making

Banks expect monitoring systems to identify relevant activity and escalate it appropriately.

Where monitoring appears superficial, or where decisions are inconsistent, the issue becomes one of reliability.

If the system cannot produce consistent outcomes, it is difficult to rely on it.

## Documentation and Transparency

Banks assess whether the business can explain its decisions.

This includes:

- how transactions are reviewed
- how conclusions are reached
- how risk is managed

If documentation does not support these explanations, the issue is not just compliance. It is transparency.

## Complexity Without Control

Cross-border activity, multi-party payments, and embedded financial features increase complexity.

Complexity is not inherently a problem. It becomes a problem when it is not matched by clear control and explanation.

If the business cannot demonstrate how it manages that complexity, the risk is difficult to assess.

## The Practical Takeaway

Banks reject fintechs on AML grounds when the system does not provide a clear and defensible picture of how risk is managed.

This is rarely about a single issue. It is about whether the business can explain, consistently and coherently, how it operates and how it controls that operation.

Where that clarity exists, onboarding can proceed.

Where it does not, the relationship becomes difficult to establish.
