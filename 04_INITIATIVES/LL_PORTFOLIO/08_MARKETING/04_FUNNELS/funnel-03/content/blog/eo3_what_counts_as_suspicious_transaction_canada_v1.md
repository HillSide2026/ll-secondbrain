# What Counts as a Suspicious Transaction in Canada (Fintech Context)

A suspicious transaction is not defined by certainty. It is defined by suspicion.

In Canada, the threshold is whether there are reasonable grounds to suspect that a transaction is related to money laundering or terrorist financing. That standard is intentionally lower than proof.

The difficulty lies in applying it in practice.

## Suspicion as a Judgment Standard

Suspicion is not a fixed rule. It is a judgment based on context.

This includes how a transaction compares to expected behavior, whether it aligns with known customer activity, and whether it fits within the normal operation of the system.

What matters is not whether the activity can be explained, but whether the explanation is credible within the context of the business.

## Deviation from Expected Behavior

In fintech environments, suspicion often arises from deviation.

A transaction may be larger than expected, more frequent than usual, or routed in a way that does not align with typical patterns.

These deviations do not automatically indicate wrongdoing, but they create the conditions for suspicion.

## Pattern Over Single Events

Suspicion is often based on patterns rather than isolated transactions.

A series of smaller transactions, when viewed together, may indicate structuring or avoidance of thresholds. Changes in behavior over time may also be relevant.

The analysis is cumulative.

## The Role of Context

Context is critical.

The same transaction may appear normal in one context and unusual in another. Customer profile, transaction history, and product design all influence how activity is interpreted.

Without context, it is difficult to assess whether suspicion is reasonable.

## The Practical Takeaway

A suspicious transaction is one that cannot be reconciled with expected behavior in a way that is credible within the context of the business.

The standard does not require certainty. It requires a reasonable basis for concern.

The challenge is applying that standard consistently.
