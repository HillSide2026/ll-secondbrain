# How Banks Assess AML and MSB Risk in Canada

Banks do not assess AML and MSB risk as a formal classification exercise. They assess it as a question of exposure.

The objective is to determine whether the business presents a risk that can be understood, monitored, and managed within the bank's own framework.

## Starting with Function, Not Labels

Banks begin by looking at what the business actually does.

Descriptions such as "platform," "processor," or "infrastructure provider" are less important than how funds move and who controls that movement.

If the functional activity resembles money transmission, foreign exchange, or multi-party settlement, the analysis follows from that.

## Clarity as a Risk Indicator

Clarity itself is a signal.

A business that can describe its transaction flows, compliance controls, and decision-making processes in a consistent way is easier to assess.

Where explanations are fragmented or overly abstract, uncertainty increases. That uncertainty is often treated as risk in its own right.

## Alignment Between Product and Compliance

Banks look for alignment between how the product operates and how compliance is structured.

If the business describes one model while transactions reflect another, the gap becomes immediately relevant.

This is particularly common in businesses that have scaled quickly or introduced new features without updating their compliance framework.

## Evaluation of Monitoring and Reporting

Monitoring systems and reporting processes are assessed for credibility.

The question is not whether they exist, but whether they produce reliable outcomes.

If monitoring appears superficial, or if escalation decisions are inconsistent, the system becomes difficult to rely on.

## Cross-Border and Structural Complexity

Cross-border activity, multi-party flows, and embedded financial features increase the level of scrutiny.

Complexity is not inherently problematic, but it must be matched by clear structure and explanation.

If the business cannot demonstrate how it manages that complexity, the risk becomes difficult to assess.

## The Practical Takeaway

Banks assess AML and MSB risk by evaluating how clearly and consistently a business can explain its operations and controls.

Where the model is coherent and aligned with its compliance framework, the risk is manageable.

Where it is not, uncertainty becomes the dominant factor.
