# Do You Need a Canadian Entity to Register as an MSB?

A business does not need to be incorporated in Canada to register as a money services business (MSB).

What matters is not where the entity is formed, but whether it is conducting MSB activities with a Canadian nexus.

This is where many international fintechs get caught off guard.

## Canadian Nexus Is the Trigger

MSB registration may be required where a business:

- serves customers located in Canada
- directs services into the Canadian market
- facilitates transactions involving Canada

The analysis is activity-based, not incorporation-based.

A non-Canadian entity can still fall fully within scope.

## What "Directing Services into Canada" Means

This is not defined narrowly.

Indicators include:

- onboarding Canadian users
- supporting Canadian payment methods
- marketing or targeting Canadian customers
- enabling flows involving Canadian accounts or counterparties

The more intentional the connection to Canada, the stronger the regulatory link.

## Why This Becomes a Problem in Practice

This issue typically surfaces late.

Common trigger points include:

- attempting to onboard with a Canadian bank
- expanding into Canadian users
- investor diligence for North American growth

At that point, the business may already be operating in a way that assumes no Canadian obligations exist.

## Structural Options

Businesses facing this issue generally consider:

- registering as a foreign MSB
- establishing a Canadian entity
- restructuring flows to reduce Canadian exposure

Each approach has implications for compliance, banking, and operations.

## The Practical Takeaway

MSB obligations in Canada are based on activity and exposure, not corporate structure.

A business can be fully outside Canada legally and still fully inside the regulatory framework operationally.
