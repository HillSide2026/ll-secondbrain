# How Banks Assess MSB Risk in Canada (From a Fintech Perspective)

Banks do not assess MSB status as a technical classification exercise.

They assess it as a risk question tied to how a business moves money.

For fintechs, this is where MSB issues typically become real.

## The Bank's Core Question

At onboarding or review, banks are trying to answer:

What regulated activity is this business performing, and how well is that risk controlled?

MSB classification is one part of that answer. It is not the entire analysis.

## Why MSB Status Matters to Banks

Banks are exposed to downstream AML risk through their clients.

If a fintech is:

- transmitting funds
- facilitating payments
- operating cross-border

…the bank needs to understand:

- whether the activity is properly classified
- whether registration is required
- whether controls are in place

Uncertainty here is treated as risk.

## What Banks Actually Look At

Banks typically assess:

- how funds move through the system
- who controls onboarding
- who controls transaction logic
- how settlement works

This is function-first analysis.

Labels like "platform" or "processor" carry little weight if the flow suggests otherwise.

## Documentation Expectations

Banks expect clear answers to:

- what the business does
- how transactions are structured
- where regulatory obligations sit

If the explanation is inconsistent or overly high-level, additional diligence follows.

In some cases, onboarding stops entirely.

## The Role of MSB Registration

MSB registration is often treated as a baseline requirement.

However, registration alone is not sufficient.

Banks also assess:

- whether the business should be registered
- whether its operations align with that classification
- whether its AML program is credible

Registration without alignment can still create issues.

## Where Fintechs Run Into Problems

Common issues include:

- unclear classification
- reliance on partners without understanding responsibility
- mismatch between product description and actual flow

These problems usually surface when the bank asks for a detailed explanation of the model.

## Cross-Border Risk Sensitivity

Cross-border activity increases scrutiny.

Banks focus on:

- jurisdictional exposure
- transaction routing
- foreign exchange activity

Fintechs expanding into or out of Canada often encounter this layer of review for the first time.

## Why This Drives Real Outcomes

Bank assessment is not theoretical.

It affects:

- account approval
- payment capabilities
- scaling ability

A business that cannot clearly explain its regulatory position will face delays or restrictions.

## The Practical Takeaway

Banks assess MSB risk based on how a fintech operates, not how it is described.

Clear classification, aligned documentation, and a coherent explanation of transaction flows are essential.

Where those elements are in place, onboarding tends to proceed.

Where they are not, progress slows quickly.
