# When to Seek STR Triage Support

STR decisions are often made internally. In some situations, that approach becomes difficult to sustain.

The issue is not lack of effort. It is the point at which internal processes no longer produce clear or consistent outcomes.

## The Signal of Uncertainty

The need for triage support typically emerges when uncertainty persists.

Transactions are reviewed multiple times without a clear conclusion. Different reviewers reach different interpretations. Escalation does not resolve the issue.

At that point, the question is no longer about a specific transaction. It is about whether the decision framework is sufficient.

## Repeated Ambiguity

Occasional uncertainty is expected.

Repeated ambiguity is not.

If similar types of activity consistently lead to unclear outcomes, the issue is structural. The system does not provide enough guidance to support decision-making.

## Pressure from External Parties

Triage support is also triggered by external pressure.

A bank may question whether certain activity should have been reported. A review may require explanations that are not readily available. Internal teams may identify gaps that cannot be resolved through existing processes.

These situations require more than incremental adjustment.

## The Role of Independent Assessment

Triage introduces an external perspective.

It allows decisions to be assessed against a consistent standard, independent of internal variation. This can clarify whether the issue lies in the transaction itself or in how it is being evaluated.

## The Practical Takeaway

STR triage support becomes relevant when internal processes no longer produce clear, consistent, and defensible outcomes.

It is not a substitute for internal decision-making. It is a way to stabilize it when uncertainty becomes persistent.
