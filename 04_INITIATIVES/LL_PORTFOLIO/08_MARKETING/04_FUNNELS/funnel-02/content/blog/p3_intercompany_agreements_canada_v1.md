# Intercompany Agreements Between a Foreign Parent and Canadian Subsidiary

A subsidiary does not operate independently of its parent.

Funds flow between entities. Services are provided. Intellectual property may be shared.

Without formal intercompany agreements, these relationships lack structure.

This creates tax exposure, particularly in transfer pricing.

It also creates uncertainty about who owns what and who is responsible for which obligations.

The agreements do not create the relationship. They define it.
