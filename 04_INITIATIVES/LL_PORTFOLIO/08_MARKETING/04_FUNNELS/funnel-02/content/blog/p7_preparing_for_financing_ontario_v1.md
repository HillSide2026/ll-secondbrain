# Preparing for Financing: Why Legal Structure Gets Scrutinized Late

Financing introduces a different perspective.

Internal assumptions are replaced by external review.

## What Gets Tested

Investors and lenders do not rely on summaries.

They examine structure.

Ownership must be clear, rights must be defined, contracts must be enforceable, and employment relationships must be properly classified.

Each element is tested against documentation.

## Where Issues Surface

Issues that were manageable internally become material externally.

Ambiguity in ownership becomes a negotiation point. Contractual gaps affect valuation. Unclear obligations introduce risk.

The process does not create these issues.

It reveals them.

## Timing Matters

By the time financing is underway, leverage is reduced.

Issues must be addressed within the timeline of the transaction.

Options are limited by urgency.

## The Practical Takeaway

Preparation is not about anticipating every question.

It is about ensuring that structure aligns with reality before external review begins.

A health check moves that process earlier.
