# What Good Actually Looks Like: When Structure Is Aligned

Alignment is not the absence of issues.

It is coherence.

## What Alignment Means

Ownership reflects actual expectations. Control matches how decisions are made. Contracts allocate risk in a way the business can absorb. Employment relationships match legal classification. Corporate records reflect real decisions.

## Not Complexity

Aligned structure is not more complex.

It is more accurate.

Documents reflect reality. Relationships are defined clearly. Obligations are understood.

## Stability Under Pressure

Alignment becomes visible when the business is tested.

Transactions proceed without structural friction.

Disputes are resolved within defined frameworks.

External review confirms, rather than questions, the structure.

## The Practical Takeaway

Good is not theoretical.

It is observable when the business is required to rely on its structure.

A health check defines what alignment looks like in the context of the business as it operates today.
