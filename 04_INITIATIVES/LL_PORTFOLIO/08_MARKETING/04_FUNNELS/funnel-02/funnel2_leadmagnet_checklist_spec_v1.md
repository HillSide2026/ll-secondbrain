# Lead Magnet Spec — Corporate Health Check Checklist

## Strategic Purpose

Pre-sell the Corporate Health Check by making the operator confront their
own structural gaps line by line. The list does not teach — it reveals.

The lead magnet's job is not to explain what structural drift is. It is to
produce a felt gap between where the operator is and where they need to be,
specifically enough that they want the paid diagnosis.

This asset replaces "Growth Without Structural Drift" as the primary Funnel 02
lead magnet. That asset tried to sell the concept before the operator had
confronted their own situation. This asset skips the concept and goes straight
to the confrontation.

## Format

Checklist / scorecard. Each item is a three-option response:

- **Yes** — in place and current
- **No** — not in place
- **Not sure** — exists but unreviewed, or unclear

"Not sure" is as important as "No." It surfaces the liability the operator
knows they have but has not addressed. By the end of the checklist the operator
has self-diagnosed. Three or more "No" or "Not sure" answers across the five
pillars is the natural bridge to the health check offer.

## Framing

Working title: **"What Growing Ontario Companies Almost Always Get Wrong"**

Subframe: "A 25-point structural checklist for operating businesses between
$5M and $30M in annual revenue."

The framing is observational, not alarmist. "Almost always" signals pattern
recognition from practice — not a scare tactic. It tells the operator that
their gaps are predictable and fixable, which is more motivating than implying
negligence.

## Structure — Five Pillars

Organized to match the Corporate Health Check's five-pillar review structure,
so the checklist maps directly onto the paid product.

---

### Pillar 1 — Shareholder Agreements

- Shareholder agreement exists and has been reviewed in the last 3 years
- Agreement reflects current equity ownership accurately
- Shotgun, right of first refusal, or drag-along provisions are present and understood by all shareholders
- Vesting schedule (if applicable) is documented and enforceable
- Dividend and distribution policy is defined in writing

---

### Pillar 2 — Corporate Records

- Minute book is maintained and current
- Director and officer registers reflect the current composition
- Annual resolutions have been passed for each year of operation
- Share register is accurate and matches the shareholder agreement
- Corporate organization chart exists and is current

---

### Pillar 3 — Employment Structure

- All employees have signed written employment agreements
- Employment agreements contain enforceable termination clauses
- Independent contractor relationships are documented and classified correctly
- Non-solicitation and confidentiality provisions are in place for key roles
- Equity grants or option agreements (if applicable) are documented

---

### Pillar 4 — Commercial Contracts

- Key customer contracts are in writing and have been reviewed
- Material supplier or vendor agreements are documented
- Indemnity and liability exposure in commercial contracts has been assessed
- Intellectual property assignment clauses are present in relevant agreements
- Contract renewal and expiry dates are tracked

---

### Pillar 5 — Transaction Readiness

- The company could produce a complete document package for a buyer or investor within 48 hours
- No undisclosed encumbrances, liens, or security interests exist on company assets
- Outstanding shareholder loans or related-party transactions are documented
- Key-person dependencies are identified and disclosed
- Regulatory licences and compliance obligations are current

---

## Gate Mechanics

Two options — ML1 to decide:

**Option A — Gate before delivery:** Operator submits email to receive the
checklist. Lead captured before they see content. Risk: lower completion rate,
less felt gap before email capture.

**Option B — Deliver immediately, gate results:** Checklist is accessible
without email. Operator completes it. To receive a score summary and "what to
do next" guidance, they submit email. Captures lead at peak motivation —
after they've confronted the gaps. Recommended.

## Bridge to Health Check

After the checklist, the natural next step is:

- 3+ items answered "No" or "Not sure": direct CTA to book or purchase the
  Corporate Health Check
- 0-2 items: softer CTA with framing that the health check confirms what's
  already in place and identifies what's not visible from the outside

## Corporation Type Considerations

Levine Law serves five corporation types within this funnel (see FUNNEL_SPEC.md
for full taxonomy): start-up (digital, includes VC-backed), family-owned,
private non-digital non-family (includes PE-backed), foreign subsidiary in
Canada, and professional corporation.

The five pillars apply across all four types. Individual checklist items may
weight or present differently by type — for example, IP assignment is more
prominent for digital start-ups; succession and family governance items are
more prominent for family-owned companies; intercompany agreement items are
more prominent for foreign subsidiaries. Whether the checklist requires
type-specific variants or a single universal list is an open production
decision pending ML1 direction.
