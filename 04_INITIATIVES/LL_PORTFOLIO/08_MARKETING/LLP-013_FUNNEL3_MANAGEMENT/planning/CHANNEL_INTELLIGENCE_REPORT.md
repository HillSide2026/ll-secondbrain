# F3 Channel Intelligence Report

## Research Method

Live web research conducted 2026-04-14 via WebSearch and WebFetch across 10 candidate
channels. Findings labelled per evidence standard: Confirmed / Probable / Hypothesis.

ICP anchor: payments operators, fintech founders, heads of compliance, product leads at
PSPs, MSBs, stablecoin companies, embedded-finance platforms, and cross-border payment
companies — building or running payment infrastructure.

---

## Channel Findings

---

### 1. LinkedIn

**ICP Presence: Confirmed**

The professional payments and fintech community is unambiguously on LinkedIn. Direct
evidence: competitors (SBSB Fintech Lawyers, Renno & Co.) are producing MSB/RPAA/FINTRAC
content on LinkedIn and ranking for the same terms. Cross-border payments, RPAA, stablecoin
settlement, and operator-level compliance content is active and circulating on the platform.
Decision-makers — founders, compliance heads, product leads — are present and engaging.

**Content Format That Works**

Short posts (3–6 paragraphs), opinion-driven, in the operator's register. Not articles.
Not legal analysis. Observations about payments operations, market dynamics, and structural
questions. Posts that start with an operational problem ("Why your payout fails in country
X") outperform posts that start with a legal framing.

**Contribution Pathway**

Organic. No pitch required. Publish directly.

**Audience Overlap**

Primary distribution channel. All other channels are additive to a LinkedIn base.

**Notes**

Competitors are already here with MSB/regulatory content — which confirms demand but also
means the ICP has existing voices to compare against. The TOF category 1–6 content (pure
payments operations) is differentiated from what competitors post (which is mid-funnel
regulatory). This is the key advantage.

---

### 2. Stablecoin Insider

**ICP Presence: Confirmed (secondary ICP); Probable (primary ICP with stablecoin exposure)**

Stablecoin Insider explicitly targets: compliance officers, fintech analysts, CTOs and
product leads at payment firms, blockchain developers integrating stablecoin APIs. This
is a direct ICP match for the secondary profile (international tokenized-payments
operators). Covers USDC, USDT, fiat on-ramps/off-ramps, regulatory developments,
stablecoins as payment infrastructure. Deliberately avoids hype/meme coin content.

**Content Format That Works**

Articles and deep dives on stablecoin infrastructure, regulatory positioning, and operator
use cases. Research reports and sponsored deep dives are also offered. Runs on Beehiiv
(newsletter + web).

**Contribution Pathway**

Active contributor program. Contact: partnerships@stablecoininsider.com
Accepts: guest contributions, sponsored stories/deep dives, founder interviews, research
collaborations.

**Audience Overlap**

Additive to LinkedIn. This audience is more international and stablecoin-focused than the
LinkedIn payments professional community.

**Notes**

Best channel for category 6 (stablecoins and new rails) content. Also the right destination
for EO4 (Tokenized Payment Systems Regulatory Structuring) when that offer is activated.
Pitch pathway is clear and welcoming.

---

### 3. BetaKit

**ICP Presence: Probable**

BetaKit is the "publication of record for Canadian tech and startup news since 2012."
Directly covers RPAA, Interac access for fintechs, RTR, Bank of Canada PSP registrations,
and Neo Financial's rail access. The Canadian payments operator ICP reads BetaKit — it
is the primary news source for Canada-connected fintech operators. 2026 direction is
expanding regional Canadian coverage ("report locally, connect nationally").

**Content Format That Works**

Feature-length editorial journalism. Not short takes. A BetaKit piece is a 600–1,200 word
article with a Canadian angle — not a thought leadership post. The value is editorial
credibility and Canadian audience reach, not frequency.

**Contribution Pathway**

No formal open contributor program identified. Evidence of some industry contributors
(e.g., Kurtis Broda of Wyvern). Pathway: pitch directly to editorial. Contact:
editorial@betakit.com or directly to fintech/payments reporters. Story tips welcomed.
Formal contributor policy unclear — direct outreach required to confirm.

**Audience Overlap**

Additive for the Canada-specific lane. Reaches Canadian operators who may not follow
payments-specific international media.

**Notes**

Best channel for category 7–8 (Canada bridge content): "Why Canada is a deceptively
difficult payments market," "What makes Canada slower than expected." These are natural
BetaKit angles. The Canadian regulatory specificity (RPAA, FINTRAC, OSFI) plays well
here. Editorial pitch required — not self-serve.

### 4. PYMNTS.com

**ICP Presence: Probable**

PYMNTS is the #1 payments industry site by traffic. C-suite and VP-level executives in
payments are the stated audience. Confirmed large payments professional readership. US-heavy
but global coverage. Canadian-specific content appears but Canada is not a primary focus.
ICP match is probable for operators watching the global payments space; less confirmed for
Canada-specific regulatory concerns.

**Content Format That Works**

700–1,500 word articles. "Informative without being academic." Practical, operator-relevant
analysis. Accepts regulatory updates, trend pieces, practical advice.

**Contribution Pathway**

Clear editorial submission process. Contact: editorial@pymnts.com
Also accepts press releases via Newswire (published within 2 hours during operating hours).
Prohibits: AI-generated content that fails detection, previously published material,
blatant sales pitches.

**Audience Overlap**

Overlaps with Fintech Blueprint and Payments NEXT for the global payments professional
audience. Additive reach vs. LinkedIn and BetaKit.

**Notes**

Good channel for category 4 (infrastructure and rails) and category 3 (cost and margin)
content — topics PYMNTS covers regularly. Less suited to Canada-specific bridge content.
High-volume, high-reach channel but not the most targeted for the F3 ICP specifically.

### 5. Payments NEXT

**ICP Presence: Probable**

Explicitly targets "global financial payments industry leaders." Covers payments trends,
fintech, regulation, practical advice, and case studies. Audience profile fits payments
operators and executives. Not Canada-specific.

**Content Format That Works**

700–1,200 word articles. Accessible and practical tone. Data-backed pieces and regulatory
updates accepted.

**Contribution Pathway**

Open contributor program with clear guidelines.
Contact: jeff@paymentsnext.com, subject line "Guest Post Pitch"
Prohibits: AI-generated content, previously published material, excessive external links.

**Audience Overlap**

Overlaps with PYMNTS for the global payments professional audience. Lower traffic/reach
than PYMNTS but more accessible for contributors and more targeted.

**Notes**

Good starting point for category 1–3 content (growth, cost, speed) that has broad operator
relevance. Accessible pitch pathway makes this a practical early channel while building
LinkedIn following.

### 6. Fintech Blueprint (Lex Sokolin)

**ICP Presence: Probable**

200,000+ subscribers. Audience: fintech founders, operators, investors, financial services
professionals. Covers cross-border payments, stablecoins, embedded finance, money movement,
payment infrastructure. Strong overlap with categories 4 and 6 (infrastructure/rails,
stablecoins). US/global orientation — not Canada-specific.

**Content Format That Works**

Newsletter analysis (written by Sokolin directly — not an open publication model) and
podcast conversations with industry executives. Guest contributions are via podcast, not
written articles.

**Contribution Pathway**

No open article submission model identified. Guest appearances on the associated podcast
are the viable pathway. Pitch: direct outreach to Lex Sokolin via LinkedIn or contact
page. Subject matter would need to fit his fintech/payments/stablecoin focus.

**Audience Overlap**

Partially overlaps with Stablecoin Insider for the stablecoin/payments infrastructure
audience. Reaches a broader fintech investor/founder audience.

**Notes**

Podcast guest appearance is the right play here, not article submission. A 30-minute
conversation on "how stablecoin settlement intersects with Canadian regulatory structure"
or "what cross-border payments operators miss about Canada" would fit his editorial angle.
Medium-effort, medium-reach. Not a priority until LinkedIn presence is established.

### 7. Payments on Fire (Glenbrook Partners)

**ICP Presence: Probable**

Glenbrook Partners is a highly respected payments consulting firm. "Payments on Fire" is
their podcast, described as "the industry's leading resource center for payments
professionals." Guest conversations with industry executives. Covers stablecoins, payment
infrastructure, settlement, and treasury operations. Audience is confirmed payments
professionals — not general fintech or retail.

**Content Format That Works**

Podcast interviews. No written contribution model.

**Contribution Pathway**

No formal guest pitch process identified on their site. Contact via Glenbrook's general
contact page. Glenbrook's own partners and invited experts appear to be the primary guests.
Cold pitch pathway is uncertain.

**Audience Overlap**

Payments-professional audience, likely overlaps with PYMNTS and Payments NEXT readership.
Distinct from LinkedIn (more specialist, consultancy-adjacent audience).

**Notes**

Worth a pitch attempt once Levine Law has established content presence — a guest
appearance would be more credible with a body of published work to reference. Medium-term
channel, not immediate.

### 8. Future Nexus (formerly Fintech Nexus)

**ICP Presence: Hypothesis**

Fintech Nexus was a well-known fintech media brand. It relaunched in February 2025 under
new ownership as "Future Nexus" with a broadened scope covering AI and general tech beyond
fintech. The rebrand diluted the payments-specific audience focus. Guest content is
accepted, but the ICP match is now less targeted than it was under the original brand.

**Content Format That Works**

Guest articles, newsletters, webinars.

**Contribution Pathway**

Accepts contributions. Contact via their site.

**Audience Overlap**

Partially overlaps with PYMNTS and Payments NEXT. Post-rebrand audience composition is
unclear.

**Notes**

Deprioritize. The rebrand away from fintech-specific focus weakens ICP targeting. Revisit
if the publication re-establishes a payments-operator audience.

### 9. Mondato

**ICP Presence: Hypothesis (primary); Probable (secondary ICP — emerging markets)**

Mondato is a consulting firm (not a media outlet) publishing a weekly research blog
(Mondato Insight) on global fintech and digital finance with an emerging markets emphasis.
Covers mobile money, digital payments, e-commerce, and cross-border infrastructure.
Audience appears to be consultants, academics, and policy researchers rather than
operators building payment products.

**Content Format That Works**

Research-style long-form analysis.

**Contribution Pathway**

Not a media outlet with open submissions. Primarily Mondato's own consultants publish.
External contribution pathway unclear.

**Notes**

Deprioritize as a content channel. The emerging markets angle is relevant to the secondary
ICP, but Mondato does not appear to be where that ICP goes for news — it's more of an
analyst publication. Watch for whether Mondato Insight develops an operator readership.

---

### 10. Reddit (r/fintech, r/payments)

**ICP Presence: Hypothesis**

No confirmed evidence of the payments operator ICP (founders, compliance leads, product
leads at PSPs/MSBs) being present in meaningful numbers on Reddit. Cross-border payments
and compliance topics appear in these subreddits but the discussions skew toward retail
users, consumers, and early-career professionals rather than the operators building
payment infrastructure.

**Notes**

Deprioritize. The F3 ICP is on LinkedIn and specialist publications, not Reddit.

## Recommended Starting Point

Given a limited time budget, begin with three channels in parallel:

**1. LinkedIn (immediate, continuous)**
The only channel where the ICP is confirmed, no pitch is required, and volume can be
built quickly. Start publishing category 1–6 content now. 2–3 posts per week. This is
the primary audience-building channel for the full 6–8 week TOF runway.

**2. Stablecoin Insider (pitch this week)**
Active contributor program, confirmed secondary ICP, clear contact (partnerships@stablecoininsider.com).
Editorial lead time will consume some of the LinkedIn runway. Category 6 content (stablecoins
and new rails) is the natural fit. A published piece here before the LinkedIn bridge phase
strengthens credibility when category 7–8 content begins.

**3. Payments NEXT (pitch this week)**
Open contributor program, payments-operator audience, accessible process
(jeff@paymentsnext.com). Lower barrier than PYMNTS. Category 1–3 content (growth,
cost, speed) fits their format. Published pieces here create reference material to cite
on LinkedIn.

**BetaKit: pitch at week 4–6.** The Canadian bridge content (categories 7–8) is the
natural BetaKit angle. Pitch when the LinkedIn voice is established enough to serve as
a credibility signal in the pitch.
