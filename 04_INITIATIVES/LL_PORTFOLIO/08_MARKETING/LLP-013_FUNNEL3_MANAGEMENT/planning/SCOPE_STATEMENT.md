# Scope Statement

Project ID: LLP-013
Project Path: 08_MARKETING/LLP-013_FUNNEL3_MANAGEMENT
Stage: Planning

---

## Execution Stage Model

- `pre-launch`: first execution stage after planning approval. Includes
  controlled setup, asset finalization, platform configuration, QA, and
  readiness work.
- `launch`: second execution stage. Includes public publishing, paid channel
  activation, external promotion, and live conversion activity.

## In Scope

### 1. Positioning and ICP Formalization

- Define and lock the Funnel 3 ICP as ICP-03: the F03 payments / regulatory audience.
- Define the primary ICP-03 profile: Canada-connected payments operators or managers with live regulatory, compliance, reporting, or payments-structure needs.
- Define the secondary ICP-03 profile: international (emerging markets) stablecoin or tokenized-payments operators whose legal/commercial risk is driven by strategic-partnership architecture, even before a Canada nexus exists.
- Confirm positioning claim: Levine Law as GTA's specialist payments regulatory counsel for operator-side MSB, RPAA, AML, and adjacent payment-structuring work — not a fractional GC, not a full-service firm. Tokenized-payment matters are adjacent and bounded, not a standalone securities lane.
- Confirm value proposition claim: Bay Street-caliber strategic and regulatory judgment at an acceptable price point for serious operators who cannot support major-firm pricing.
- Define the competitive white space explicitly: no GTA firm currently holds the "specialist payments regulatory + fractional GC" position. Funnel 3 exists to claim it.
- Record the internal strategic upside of the secondary profile explicitly: access to market information and practice-skill development in tokenized-payments and strategic-partnership architecture matters.

### 2. Channel Architecture

Primary paid channel:
- LinkedIn Ads targeting job titles at payments/fintech companies (Head of Compliance, CFO, CEO, General Counsel, Co-Founder) in Ontario and Canada.

Primary organic/authority channels:
- BetaKit newsletter sponsorship/editorial presence.
- Payments/fintech podcasts (sponsorship or guest appearances).
- LinkedIn organic content tied to regulatory deadlines (RPAA, CARF, open banking, OSFI).

Secondary channel (network-driven):
- Structured referral relationships with accountants, US fintech lawyers, and SaaS platform advisors who encounter Canadian regulatory questions.

Out of scope: Google Ads (F01 channel), health check diagnostics (F02 channel), direct cold outreach at volume.

### 3. Entry Offer Definition

Primary F03 entry offer:
- Regulatory triage consultation: a defined-scope, fixed-fee consultation focused on a specific regulatory question (MSB registration analysis, RPAA applicability, AML program gap review).

Secondary F03 entry offer:
- AML Health Check (where applicable and not duplicated with F02): operational AML compliance review for MSBs and regulated fintechs.

Entry offer pricing: to be defined in WORKPLAN. Must be set before LinkedIn Ads activation.

Out of scope: Health Check corporate diagnostic (F02 offer). Funnel 3 entry offers are regulatory-specialist in nature, not corporate health generalist.

### 4. Core Offer Map

Retainer and recurring work served by Funnel 3:
- MSB registration and ongoing compliance
- RPAA registration and post-registration reporting
- AML program design, review, and remediation
- Payments program agreements (sponsor bank, processor, BaaS, PayFac)
- Embedded finance regulatory structuring
- International fintech entry into Canada (MSB/RPAA triage)
- Ongoing compliance counsel retainers for regulated operators

Core offer map must be documented in a canonical artifact before channel activation.

### 5. Asset Production

Assets required before `launch`:
- Website page: Payments/MSB/Fintech regulatory counsel page on levine-law.ca. Must be live before LinkedIn Ads activation.
- LinkedIn profile optimization: Levine Law positioning language updated on ML1's LinkedIn profile.
- Content calendar: minimum 8 LinkedIn posts addressing regulatory deadlines before paid campaign launch.

Asset production is in scope for Planning workplan definition. Controlled asset
execution may occur in `pre-launch`. Public rollout belongs to `launch`.

### 6. Distribution and Signal Tracking

- Define conversion attribution rules: how does a Funnel 3 contact become a qualified consult? What triggers the matter opening?
- Define signal tracking: LinkedIn Ads conversion events, BetaKit click-through metrics, consultation booking source tracking.
- Define baseline: current number of F03-attributed consultations per month (zero if not currently tracked).

### 7. Regulatory Deadline Targeting

Buying trigger calendar to be built:
- RPAA registration deadline: 2026-03-31 (passed; retain as a historical demand trigger and reframe future content around post-deadline applicability, remediation, reporting, and ongoing operating discipline).
- CARF (Crypto Asset Reporting Framework): 2027 filing obligations, operator prep needed in 2026.
- Open banking framework: timeline uncertain, creates ongoing advisory demand.
- OSFI operational risk guideline: September 2026 deadline for regulated firms.

## Out of Scope

- F01 funnel (Google Ads, GHL reactive intake) — governed under LLP-011.
- F02 funnel (Corporate Health Check) — governed under LLP-012.
- Matter delivery and execution of client work.
- Any content publishing, ad activation, or channel launch — these are
  execution-stage actions and remain outside Planning.
- Hiring or staffing decisions.
- CA-01 Conversion Architect activation (blocked until F02 traffic baseline established).
- SPE-01 Selective Provocation Engine activation (blocked until ≥3 accountant referral relationships active).

---

## Completion Condition

Planning stage completion authorizes entry into `pre-launch`, not automatic
entry into `launch`.

Funnel 3 may enter `pre-launch` when:
1. ICP-03 and positioning are formally locked.
2. Entry offer pricing and scope are ML1-approved.
3. Core offer map is documented.
4. Website page implementation path is approved and publication-ready.
5. LinkedIn Ads campaign is configured and ready for controlled activation.
6. Signal tracking baseline is captured.
7. RPAA deadline content calendar (minimum 4 posts) is drafted and SE-01 reviewed.

`Launch` requires a later ML1 decision confirming that public publishing, paid
channel activation, and external promotion should actually go live.
