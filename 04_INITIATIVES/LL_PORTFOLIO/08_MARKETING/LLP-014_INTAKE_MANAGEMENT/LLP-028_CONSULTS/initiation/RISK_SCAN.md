# Risk Scan

Project ID: LLP-028
Project Path: 04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/08_MARKETING/LLP-014_INTAKE_MANAGEMENT/LLP-028_CONSULTS

## Top Risks

1. ML1 capacity: consultations require ML1 time directly; as funnel volume
   grows, unmanaged consultation demand creates a hard capacity ceiling that
   no process improvement can resolve without a staffing decision.
2. Undefined conversion criteria: without agreed criteria for what constitutes
   a retained client, conversion tracking is subjective and the pipeline
   metric is meaningless.
3. Handoff gap upstream: if LLP-027 does not deliver a complete handoff
   package, LLP-028 receives incomplete information and must re-triage the
   inquiry, degrading the prospect experience.
4. Handoff gap downstream: if LLP-028 does not deliver a complete handoff
   to LLP-029, onboarding starts with missing context, degrading the
   new-client experience.
5. Bottleneck accumulation: qualified inquiries from LLP-027 accumulate if
   consultation scheduling capacity does not scale with inquiry volume;
   no governed queue management means this becomes invisible until it is
   already a problem.

## Key Assumptions

- ML1 is the primary consulting practitioner for all consultations in scope;
  no delegation to other practitioners is assumed at this stage
- LLP-027 will define its handoff output format before LLP-028 Planning
  finalizes its intake requirements
- LLP-029 will be initiated before LLP-028 Planning reaches the handoff
  design milestone

## Go / No-Go Judgment

Go — advance to Planning. Sequence: initiate first, then Planning can begin
only after LLP-027 Planning has defined the handoff protocol LLP-028 depends
on (per SEQUENCING_RECOMMENDATIONS.md).
