# Risk Scan

Project ID: LLP-014
Project Path: 04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/08_MARKETING/LLP-014_INTAKE_MANAGEMENT

## Top Risks

1. Coordination failure: subprojects advance independently without a
   functioning parent governance layer, creating handoff gaps between stages
   that no subproject owns.
2. Metric fragmentation: each subproject tracks different data points, making
   cross-stage conversion invisible at the pipeline level even when individual
   stages are performing correctly.
3. Scope bleed: LLP-014 attempts to execute what belongs to its subprojects,
   causing duplication, confusion, and accountability gaps.
4. Dependency timing: LLP-027, LLP-028, and LLP-029 cannot finalize their
   handoff logic until LLP-014 defines the stage protocols they rely on —
   Planning must sequence these correctly.
5. LLP-026 gap: if lead capture is not governed, inquiry volume entering the
   pipeline is unpredictable and unattributed, making pipeline planning and
   conversion reporting unreliable from the start.

## Key Assumptions

- LLP-027, LLP-028, and LLP-029 will each complete initiation before LLP-014
  Planning artifacts lock in the pipeline handoff protocols
- LLP-026 (Lead Capture) will be planned in parallel so that lead routing
  into the pipeline is defined before inquiry handling is built
- The boundary between LLP-029 (marketing-side onboarding) and LLP-004
  (operational onboarding) will be defined in Planning before either project
  configures its onboarding steps
