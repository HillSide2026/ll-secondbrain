# Problem Statement

Project ID: LLP-029
Project Path: 04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/08_MARKETING/LLP-014_INTAKE_MANAGEMENT/LLP-029_ONBOARDING

When a prospect decides to retain Levine Law, there is no governed
client-facing onboarding experience. What happens between the decision to
retain and the first substantive matter meeting is unstructured: the client
may receive an engagement letter, but they do not receive a defined welcome,
a clear set of expectations, or a structured sequence of communications that
frames what the relationship will look like.

The quality of the first few days as a client shapes the client's confidence
in the relationship and their likelihood of referring others. It also directly
affects the operational side — a client who arrives at LLP-004 (matter setup)
with clear expectations and properly set context creates less friction than
one who does not.

The second problem is that the boundary between marketing-side onboarding
(this project) and operational-side onboarding (LLP-004) is not defined.
Both projects need to know what they own before either can design their
respective workflows.

This project initiates the work to govern the marketing side of client
onboarding and define the boundary with LLP-004.
