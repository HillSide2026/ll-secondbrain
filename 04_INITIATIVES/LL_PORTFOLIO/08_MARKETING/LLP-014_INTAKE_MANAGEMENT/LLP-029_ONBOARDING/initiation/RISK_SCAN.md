# Risk Scan

Project ID: LLP-029
Project Path: 04_INITIATIVES/LL_PORTFOLIO/08_MARKETING/LLP-014_INTAKE_MANAGEMENT/LLP-029_ONBOARDING

## Top Risks

1. LLP-004 scope boundary undefined: if LLP-029 and LLP-004 plan their
   onboarding scopes independently, they will either duplicate touchpoints
   (creating a disjointed client experience) or leave gaps between them
   (creating dropped handoffs). Scope boundary must be defined before either
   project finalizes its Planning scope.
2. Incomplete handoff from LLP-028: if the Consultation → Onboarding handoff
   does not include the context and client information LLP-029 needs, the
   welcome experience starts with a gap that the client will notice.
3. Over-engineering the experience: the first version of onboarding should
   be simple, reliable, and repeatable — not elaborate. The risk is designing
   a high-touch experience that is not sustainable at volume.
4. Template staleness: welcome communications and expectation-setting
   materials go stale if the firm's matter process or communication protocol
   changes without a trigger to update onboarding materials.
5. LLP-005 boundary gap: if the transition from onboarding (LLP-029) to
   matter activation (LLP-005) is not defined, a retained client may
   experience a dead zone between the end of onboarding and the start of
   active matter work.

## Key Assumptions

- LLP-004 is available to coordinate on the scope boundary during LLP-029
  Planning; this coordination is a hard dependency
- LLP-028 will be planned sufficiently to define its handoff output format
  before LLP-029 Planning reaches the intake design milestone
- The first version of onboarding is designed for current volume levels;
  it can be scaled in a later iteration

## Go / No-Go Judgment

Go — advance to Planning. Sequence: initiate now; Planning begins after
LLP-028 defines the Consultation → Onboarding handoff protocol and LLP-004
coordination is engaged (per SEQUENCING_RECOMMENDATIONS.md).
