# Strategy Brief — Level 2 Industries Pillar Page

## 1. Pillar Page Purpose

The Industries / Industries Served page is a Level 2 pillar page within the canonical Levine Law site hierarchy defined in POL-051.

Its job is to:
- Explain that Levine Law works with businesses in selected industries, not all-comers.
- Show that the firm's advice is shaped by the industry context of the client's business.
- Organize the firm's industry-facing content and route visitors to the correct Level 3 pages.
- Signal to qualified visitors (ICP-01 and ICP-02) that the firm understands the specific commercial and regulatory environment they operate in.

It does not:
- Replace the Services page (which covers how the firm helps, not who it helps).
- Replace the Inquiries page (which is the general contact path).
- Serve as a primary opt-in or lead capture surface.
- Provide legal advice.

---

## 2. Site-Structure Fit

The Industries page occupies a specific structural position:

```
Home
  -> Industries / Industries Served  [THIS PAGE]
      -> Level 3: industry-specific soft landing pages
      -> Level 3: client-profile-specific soft landing pages
  -> Services / How We Help  [SEPARATE]
      -> Level 3: service-specific soft landing pages
  -> Inquiries  [SEPARATE]
```

The boundary between Industries and Services is critical:

- Industries page answers: "What kinds of businesses does Levine Law work with?"
- Services page answers: "What does Levine Law actually do / how does it help?"

These are distinct questions that attract visitors at different points in their navigation. A visitor browsing by industry is often earlier in the orientation phase than one browsing by service. The Industries page should deepen their context and route them toward the right Level 3 page.

## 3. ICP Alignment

The Industries page must represent both approved ICPs:

**ICP-01: Ontario Operating Company**
- Revenue range: $1M to $8M
- Employees: 5 to 30
- 2+ years operating
- Accountant-involved, document-ready, not in crisis
- Industries from the current site (Agriculture, Health, Financial Services, Managed Services, Energy and Logistics, Construction) map primarily to this ICP
- The page must speak to these operators in commercial, structural terms — not in crisis-response language

**ICP-02: Fintech / Payments / MSB / PSP Operator**
- Canada exposure
- Commercially minded, regulatory-aware
- Structural clarity preference
- Decision-maker access
- Currently absent from the /industries page entirely — this is a gap that must be closed
- F03 funnel feeds from this ICP; the Industries page is one entry point into F03 routing

## 4. Positioning Constraints

From MARKET_POSITIONING.md and MARKETING_STRATEGY.md:

- The firm positions as preventative counsel, not reactive or crisis-driven.
- Entry points are productized and maturity-gated: Corporate Health Check (F02), MSB Registration / AML Health Check (F03).
- Tone: calm authority. Operator-aligned. Structural and preventative. Not alarmist.
- "What LL is NOT": pre-revenue startup counsel, crisis-only, very small businesses, pure litigation.

The Industries page must reflect this:
- Industry descriptions must frame the legal and structural challenges as systems problems, not crises.
- The firm's value in each sector is characterized as structural stability, regulatory competence, governance clarity — not firefighting.
- Language must be direct and specific, not generic ("we serve many clients in many industries").

## 7. Content Strategy Principles

**Section 1 — Intro / Why Industry Context Matters:**
Establish that the firm does not apply the same advice across all businesses. Industry context shapes structure, governance, regulatory exposure, and contract requirements. This section is the philosophical grounding for the page.

**Section 2 — Industries We Serve:**
A clean, specific list. Each industry entry should be business-facing and grounded in actual operational characteristics, not generic descriptors. Both ICP-01 and ICP-02 industries must appear.

**Section 3 — How Our Advice Changes By Industry:**
Demonstrate sector-specific awareness without tipping into legal advice. Show that a construction company's legal needs are structurally different from a payments operator's needs. This is the differentiation proof point.

**Section 4 — Common Industry-Specific Business and Legal Issues:**
Concrete, recognizable issues by industry. These should resonate with a business owner who reads them and says "yes, that is a real problem we have." Not alarmist — structural.

**Section 5 — Related Resources / Where To Go Next:**
Internal links to Level 3 pages. This section performs the routing function. It tells the visitor: here is the more specific page for your situation.

**Section 6 — Contact / CTA:**
Restrained. Routes to Level 3 or Inquiries. Does not attempt hard conversion. Does not position a free consultation as the primary entry point. Consistent with the firm's maturity-gated, paid-diagnostic model.

---

## 8. What This Page Is Not

This page is not:
- A homepage: no broad firm introduction, no testimonials, no process schematic.
- A Services page: no service-by-service breakdown, no practice area descriptions.
- A hard-conversion landing page: no lead capture form, no urgency language, no panic-based messaging.
- A blog or resource library: no long-form articles, no listicles.

---

## 9. Success Criteria

The Industries page succeeds when:
- A visitor arriving from the homepage understands which industries the firm serves and why industry context matters.
- A visitor in a named industry can identify their sector and click through to a relevant Level 3 page.
- A payments or fintech operator can see ICP-02 coverage and route to the relevant Level 3 page.
- The page does not create confusion about whether they are on a Services page, a contact page, or a homepage.
- The tone is consistent with calm authority and operator alignment throughout.
