## The Core Diagnosis

The marketing architecture is structurally sound but operationally stalled. You have a correct thesis — reactive Google Ads is the wrong long-term channel for ICP-01, F02 is the right structural replacement, paid diagnostics filter for fit — and you have none of it built yet. F01 is underperforming on both volume and quality. F02 has no landing page, no entry offer, and no distribution channel selected. F03 is active but ungoverned.

The three open decisions in the workplan are not administrative. They are the entire reason nothing is moving. The workplan correctly sequences them — channel first, then offer framing — but the decisions are all sitting with ML1 and have been since at least late February.

The biggest risk is not that you pick the wrong answer on any of these three questions. The biggest risk is that you keep leaving them open while F01 continues to consume $1,500/month acquiring low-value matters that do not match ICP-01.

## Decision 1 — ICP-01 Channel Hypothesis

**Recommendation: Accountant referral network, with LinkedIn as a secondary channel. Do not lead with SEO for ICP-01.**

The ICP-01 definition is specific in a way that is incompatible with search-intent acquisition. A mature Ontario operator running a $3M business with 15 employees and an accountant already involved is not Googling "corporate lawyer Toronto" when they have a structural problem. They are asking their accountant. The accountant is the gatekeeper — the workplan acknowledges this, but does not follow it to its logical conclusion, which is that you should be building a referral relationship with 10 to 15 Ontario accountants who serve this segment before you do anything else.

This is not a complex channel to activate. It requires you to identify the accounting firms that serve $1M–$8M Ontario operators (mid-market CA firms, not Big Four), have direct conversations with two or three partners at each, and create a referral mechanic that makes it easy for them to send you clients. The referral mechanic should be tied to F02 — the Corporate Health Check becomes the thing you offer their clients, not a vague "call my lawyer friend." A productized $1,500 diagnostic is far easier for an accountant to recommend than an open-ended retainer.

LinkedIn is the correct secondary channel because this is a relationship and authority channel, not a volume channel. It is appropriate for warm ICP-01 engagement once the referral network has a foundation. Do not open a LinkedIn content program until you have the referral architecture in place, because LinkedIn without warm social proof is noise for a solo firm.

Do not lead with SEO for ICP-01. SEO takes 12–18 months minimum, it will not acquire mature operators who are not searching, and you cannot afford to wait for it as the primary ICP-01 channel. SEO belongs in the channel mix but as a supporting channel that converts people who are already warm, not as the acquisition driver.

## Decision 2 — SEO Direction: Exits vs Structuring

**Recommendation: Structuring cluster as primary. Exits cluster as secondary for F03 cross-pollination only.**

Structuring keywords ("executive compensation," "corporate governance") signal exactly the kind of operator who is building systems, not solving a crisis. These are ICP-01 signals. Exits keywords ("M&A lawyer Toronto," "business succession planning") attract episodic, high-intent traffic that is more likely to be one-engagement than retainer-track.

There is a secondary consideration the documents do not address: Exits keywords have significantly higher competition. "M&A lawyer Toronto" puts you against Bay Street firm websites with full SEO teams. You will not win on that keyword without sustained authority-building investment you cannot provide as a solo lawyer doing your own marketing.

Structuring keywords are lower competition, lower search volume, and higher ICP specificity. That is the correct tradeoff for a firm your size. Rank for three or four Structuring keywords that bring you 40 qualified visitors a month over ranking page 3 for Exits keywords that bring you 500 unqualified visitors.

One decision not addressed in the documents: which Structuring keywords specifically to target first. "Corporate governance" is too broad. "Shareholder agreement review Ontario" or "executive compensation plan review Ontario" are more specific and actionable. This is a research task appropriate to delegate to an SEO agent — but ML1 must approve the final list before any content is produced.

## Decision 3 — F02 Entry Offer Framing

**Recommendation: Corporate Health Check. Lock it now. Do not continue deliberating.**

"Premium Consultation" is not a product. It is a free consult with a price tag. Anyone reading it will wonder why they should pay for a consultation when other lawyers offer free ones. You will lose the positioning battle before the conversation starts.

"Strategic Consultation" is positioned for Exits — it sounds like pre-transaction advisory. If you are targeting ICP-01 operators who need governance and structural work, "strategic" implies they are preparing to do something (sell, raise, merge) rather than building better systems. It is misaligned with the preventative thesis.

"Corporate Health Check" is the right framing because it is a diagnostic product with a defined scope, a defined output, and an implied recurring value. It signals structure, systems, and prevention — exactly the positioning thesis. The concern that it "may require buyer education" is real but it is the correct problem to have. You want clients who understand what a governance review is.

One thing the documents do not define that is required before the landing page can be built: the scope and price. What does ML1 actually do in a Corporate Health Check? What does the client receive — a written memo, a verbal debrief, a scored framework? What is the price? Suggested range: $1,500–$2,500, which filters for fit without being a barrier to a $3M business owner. ML1 must set this. The offer cannot be built without it.

## F01 Performance Problem: Volume AND Quality

F01 is underperforming on both dimensions and they are related problems, not separate ones.

On volume: the conversion model (25–30% lead → consult, 40–50% consult → project, 8–10% blended) is structurally sound but the campaign may have execution problems — quality score, campaign structure, landing page conversion — that are addressable without changing the $1,500 budget. Neither can be diagnosed without the actual campaign data.

On quality: a subset of retained matters being very low value is a filtering problem, not a marketing problem. The intake gate is not working. Either consult qualification is too loose (booking consults with non-ICP-01) or the post-consult retention decision is too loose (retaining matters that should be declined). The solution is not to change the ad messaging — it is to tighten the intake filter. Three pre-booking questions will eliminate 60% of non-ICP-01 leads before you spend a minute on the phone: (1) business revenue, (2) number of employees, (3) whether they have an accountant.

The strategic implication: do not reduce F01 spend until F02 is live. F01 is delivering some qualified matters even if noisily. Fix the filter instead of cutting spend.

## F02 Build Priority

F02 is the most important project in the marketing portfolio and it is entirely unbuilt.

The minimum viable F02 build is four items:

1. Entry offer: Corporate Health Check at a defined price with a defined scope and deliverable
2. Landing page: Single page on levine-law.ca with a paid booking form (GHL already in place)
3. Distribution: Three to five accountant relationships that can refer initial clients
4. Intake gate: Pre-booking questions that enforce ICP-01 filter (revenue, employees, accountant)

None of this is technically complex. The blocker is ML1 decisions: price, scope, and which accountants to approach. Once those inputs are defined, F02 can be live in weeks, not months.

Do not activate the Blog/SEO agent or the Conversion Architect until the offer exists. Producing content for a funnel with no landing page or offer is wasted effort.

## Capacity Risk

You are the sole lawyer. Every matter you accept comes out of the same hour pool. F01 is already consuming capacity with low-value matters. F02, if it works, will add more matters — and the Corporate Health Check itself is a billable engagement that requires ML1 time to deliver. F03 is active. And you are being asked to make all planning decisions, approve all agent outputs, and execute the referral network development.

The capacity math does not work if F02 succeeds at scale without deliberate constraints. Three capacity decisions that need to be made before F02 launches:

**First**: What is the maximum number of active matters you can carry without compromising quality? Start with a real number — suggested ceiling is 15 active matters.

**Second**: What is the minimum matter value that justifies accepting new work? A $1,500 Health Check that converts to a $6,000 remediation project is fine. A $1,500 Health Check that converts to a $1,500 NDA review is not. Suggested floor: $5,000+ projected matter value for new corporate work.

**Third**: Are there practice area categories within ICP-01 scope that you want to stop accepting to protect capacity for higher-value work? If incorporations or low-complexity NDA reviews are filling your calendar, that is a capacity problem masquerading as a marketing problem.

If you do not make these decisions before F02 launches, a successful F02 will create a capacity crisis, not revenue growth.
