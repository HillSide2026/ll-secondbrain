# LLP-025 — Problem Statement

## Current State

Levine Law currently operates with two Ontario business audiences and one separate vertical audience:

- **ICP-01**: the reactive Funnel 01 Ontario entrepreneur audience
- **ICP-02**: the intended Funnel 02 Ontario operating company audience
- **F03 audience**: payments / fintech / MSB / PSP operators with Canadian exposure

The current channel portfolio is as follows:

- **Funnel 01 (Google Ads)**: Active. Generates reactive, search-intent-driven inquiries. This is the live ICP-01 bridge audience: Ontario entrepreneurs and small business owners with reactive legal triggers. It also skews toward crisis-driven, transactional, and low-fit matters.
- **Funnel 02 (Paid Corporate Health Check)**: Not launched. This is the intended primary acquisition path for ICP-02, but the entry offer has not been built or deployed.
- **Funnel 03 (Payments/MSB)**: Active. Serves the F03 payments audience. Separate vertical; does not address the ICP-02 gap.

Baseline KPI data has been collected (30+ days). Targets have not been formalized.

## Core Problem

**The firm's intended preventative growth audience (ICP-02: Ontario operating companies with more than $1M revenue) is not reachable through any current acquisition channel.**

ICP-02 clients are not search-intent-driven prospects. A $5M Ontario operating company with two shareholders and a governance gap is not searching "Toronto corporate lawyer." They surface through:
- Accountant or advisor referral networks
- Recognition of a specific operational problem (shareholder dispute exposure, corporate structure review, AML compliance gap)
- Authority content that names their situation precisely and credibly
- Paid entry offers distributed through channels they already trust

F01 (Google Ads) cannot reach this population structurally. F02, which could reach them through content and paid diagnostics, is unbuilt. The result: LL's positioning is defined but stranded — there is no channel delivering the clients the firm is designed to serve.

## Secondary Problems

1. **F02 is unbuilt**: The intended primary corporate acquisition path (paid Corporate Health Check diagnostic) has no entry offer, no landing page, and no distribution. The F01 → F02 transition thesis cannot be executed until F02 has a launchable product.

2. **ICP-02 channel strategy undefined**: The firm has not defined how it intends to reach mature Ontario operating companies. Candidate channels (accountant referral networks, LinkedIn, targeted SEO for operational problems, paid diagnostics) have not been evaluated or prioritized.

3. **Payments vertical underdeveloped**: Funnel 03 is active but lacks a managed authority-building program. The vertical has strong differentiation potential that is not yet being systematically captured.

4. **KPI targets not formalized**: 30-day baseline data exists. Phase 2 (provisional targets) and Phase 3 (KPI lock) have not been completed.

5. **Agent architecture ungoverned**: Six marketing agents are deployed or specified but operate without a consolidated strategy project anchoring their scope, priorities, and governance rules.

## Impact of Inaction

- F01 continues to consume intake capacity with low-fit matters
- ICP-02 is never systematically acquired — the firm's differentiated positioning generates no matching clients
- F02 remains a thesis with no deployment path
- The payments vertical builds slowly without a governed content and conversion program
- KPI drift: agent outputs and funnel decisions made without formalized targets

## Desired State

A governed marketing strategy project that:
- Defines the ICP-02 channel strategy — how the firm actually reaches mature Ontario operating companies
- Owns the F02 build and launch as a project milestone, not just a thesis
- Sets KPI targets (Phase 2 and Phase 3) now that baseline data exists
- Governs the payments vertical authority-building program
- Anchors the agent architecture to approved doctrine
- Maintains positioning, objectives, and goals as a managed, reviewable artifact set
