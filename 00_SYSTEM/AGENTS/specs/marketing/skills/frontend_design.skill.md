# Skill: Frontend Design

## Purpose
Establish a distinctive, production-grade frontend design direction before
design or build work begins, then translate that direction into detailed,
implementation-ready guidance for landing pages, page sections, ad creatives,
design QA, and brand reviews.

## Inputs
- Approved objective, offer, and funnel context
- Approved copy or approved content blocks
- Target asset or surface: landing page, page section, ad creative, brand
  review, design QA packet, or implementation handoff
- Audience / ICP and conversion goal
- Applicable brand doctrine and page constraints
- Platform constraint set: Canva, Thrive Architect, WordPress, HTML/CSS/JS,
  React, or other approved surface

## Process
1. Clarify purpose, audience, conversion target, and platform constraints.
2. Choose a deliberate aesthetic direction with explicit tone and
   differentiation. Do not proceed with a vague "clean" or "modern" direction.
3. Define the memorable design anchor: the one visual or structural choice that
   makes the artifact feel intentional rather than generic.
4. Specify the design system for the artifact:
   - typography pairings and hierarchy
   - dominant colors, accents, and background treatment
   - motion strategy
   - spatial composition, density, and section rhythm
   - component styling and asset treatment
5. Match implementation complexity to the chosen direction:
   - restrained and precise for minimalist work
   - elaborate and layered for maximalist work
6. Produce detailed execution guidance:
   - layout precision
   - visual hierarchy
   - CTA placement and conversion copy fit
   - asset specs and dimensions
   - interaction notes
   - handoff notes and QA checks
7. Validate the direction against brand rules and platform feasibility before
   output is finalized.

## Outputs
- Distinctive Design Brief
- Page / Creative Direction Packet
- Detailed Asset Spec Sheet
- Design QA Checklist
- Brand Review Notes
- Handoff Notes for downstream implementation

## Constraints
- Never default to generic AI aesthetics.
- Avoid overused default font choices such as Arial, Inter, Roboto, or system
  stacks unless the existing surface already requires them.
- Avoid cliched palette choices, especially generic purple-on-white treatments.
- Avoid cookie-cutter layout patterns that do not reflect the asset's purpose.
- Respect approved brand rules, legal constraints, and platform limits.
- Where Canva or Thrive limits the ideal direction, preserve the design intent
  as closely as possible and document the compromise explicitly.

## Invocation
Use when the quality of frontend design, page aesthetics, creative direction,
visual detail, or implementation fidelity materially affects marketing
performance or brand memory.
