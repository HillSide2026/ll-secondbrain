# Solution Scope: Business Acquisition

---

## Primary Classification Variable: Deal Size

Deal size is the first intake variable. It determines the transaction pathway and the lawyer's role.

| Level | Deal Size | Transaction Pathway |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| Level 1 | < $250,000 | APA prepared by real estate agent; lawyer in review and advisory role |
| Level 2 | $250,000 – $1,000,000 | APA prepared by lawyer; no LOI |

> Deal size means total purchase price including any vendor take-back, earnout, or deferred consideration.

> Transactions exceeding $1,000,000 where an LOI is required are governed by the **BUSINESS_ACQUISITION Strategy** — see `../../STRATEGIES/BUSINESS_ACQUISITION/`.

---

## Transaction Structure

| Structure | Description | Typical Use |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Asset Purchase | Buyer acquires specified assets and assumed liabilities of the business; seller retains the corporate entity | Default for small business acquisitions; cleaner liability profile for buyer |
| Share Purchase | Buyer acquires shares of the operating corporation; all assets and liabilities transfer | Less common at this deal size range; used where contracts, licences, or permits are non-assignable |

---

## Included

- Business acquisitions in Ontario at Level 1 and Level 2
- Asset purchase transactions (both levels)
- Share purchase transactions (both levels, with appropriate escalation flags)
- Lawyer-prepared APAs (Level 2)
- Review of agent-prepared APAs (Level 1)
- Diligence scoped proportionately to deal size
- Closing and post-closing checklist

---

## Explicitly Excluded

- Real property acquisitions (land or buildings as the primary asset)
- Financing and securities advice
- Tax structuring advice (refer to tax counsel)
- Amalgamations (separate solution)
- Cross-border transactions
- Public company transactions
- Franchise acquisitions involving franchisor consent requirements beyond standard landlord/licensor consent
- Transactions where purchase price is not ascertainable at intake

---

## Mandatory Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately when:

- Share purchase is proposed — assess whether non-assignable contracts or licences drive the choice
- Purchase price is payable by earnout and earnout mechanics are disputed or complex
- Vendor take-back (VTB) financing is part of the deal
- Buyer is acquiring employees and employment succession issues are material
- Environmental concerns exist in relation to the business premises or operations
- A regulatory licence or permit is the primary asset being acquired (e.g., cannabis, pharmacy, AGCO licence)
- Significant CRA liability risk — consider whether a tax clearance certificate is required
- Counterparty is unrepresented and APA is being prepared by the other side
- Any fact pattern indicating the seller is in financial distress
- Deal size is at or near a level threshold (assess classification carefully)

---

## Boundary Conditions

- If the acquisition requires a new corporate entity for the buyer, the INCORPORATION Tier 1 Solution applies independently
- If the acquisition involves a shareholder agreement among co-buyers, the SHAREHOLDER_AGREEMENT Tier 1 Solution applies independently
- PPSA searches and discharges are within scope; PPSA security registrations by the buyer are out of scope unless specifically instructed
- Post-closing regulatory registrations (business licence, sector licensing) are within scope as a post-closing checklist item, not a transaction deliverable
