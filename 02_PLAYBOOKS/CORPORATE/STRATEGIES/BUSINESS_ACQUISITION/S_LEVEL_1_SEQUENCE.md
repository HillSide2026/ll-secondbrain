# S-Level 1 Engagement Sequence
## Business Acquisition — $1M–$2.5M — Brokered Deal

---

## Overview

At S-Level 1, the deal originates with a seller-side broker. Phase A is client-led — it is the client's preliminary filtering and bank liaison work done before LL is engaged substantively. LL's role in Phase A is limited: review the broker's NDA if asked, and be available for advisory questions. LL's active work begins at Phase B (LOI).

Phase A has five sub-phases. The client must complete all five before proceeding to LOI. Each sub-phase has a filter function — the deal should be killed at the earliest point a disqualifying issue appears rather than carried forward.

This sequence applies to brokered deals. Non-brokered deals follow a modified sequence.

---

## Phase A — Pre-LOI (Client-Led Filtering and Underwriting)

### A-1 —Initial Broker Package Review

**Goal:** Decide whether to engage.

**Typical documents received:**
- CIM or teaser
- SDE calculation
- Basic business summary
- Asking price

**Client must check:**

*Financial sanity*
- Revenue trend (flat / declining / growing)
- SDE vs. revenue ratio
- SDE adjustments — are the add-backs realistic or inflated?
- Margin vs. industry norms

*Deal structure sanity*
- Price / SDE multiple
- Asset-heavy vs. goodwill-heavy
- Inventory inclusion
- Working capital expectations

*Business quality*
- Customer concentration
- Owner involvement
- Recurring vs. project revenue
- Employee count

**LL's role:** None unless client requests NDA review. Broker's NDA is typically their standard form — if asked, review for onerous provisions (non-circumvent clauses, exclusivity-like restrictions, broad confidentiality definitions).

---

### A-2 —Initial Broker Questions

**Goal:** Filter the deal before investing significant time.

This stage is still screening, not diligence. The client is testing: broker responsiveness, whether the business narrative matches the numbers, and broker competence.

**Typical questions to put to the broker:**

*Financial*
- Monthly revenue for last 24 months
- Revenue by service line
- Customer concentration
- Top 10 customers by revenue

*Operational*
- Owner's weekly hours
- Employee breakdown (FT/PT, roles)
- Sales process
- Key contracts

*Transaction*
- Reason for sale
- Seller's expected transition period
- Landlord / lease details
- Working capital expectations

**LL's role:** None at this stage.

---

### A-3 —Buyer Qualification Call

**Goal:** Mutual assessment — broker qualifies the buyer; client qualifies the deal.

**Broker's goals:**
- Confirm client can close
- Confirm client is serious
- Confirm client understands the industry

**Client's goals:**
- Assess seller dependency — does the business run without the owner?
- Test earnings credibility
- Test risk concentration

**Typical call topics:**
- Owner's daily activities
- Customer acquisition process
- Churn and retention
- Vendor dependency
- Seasonality
- Employee stability

Duration: 30–60 minutes.

**LL's role:** None. Client attends without counsel.

---

### A-4 —Round 2 Information Request

**Goal:** Obtain slightly deeper information. Still not full diligence.

**Typical requests:**

*Financial*
- Last 3 years' tax returns
- Detailed P&L by month
- Add-back / SDE adjustment breakdown
- AR / AP snapshot

*Operational*
- Employee roles and pay ranges
- Customer concentration report
- Equipment list
- Lease summary

*Sales*
- Pipeline or backlog
- Contract terms

**LL's role:** None. Client reviews materials independently and with their accountant.

---

### A-5 —Back-of-Envelope Underwrite

**Goal:** Before writing an LOI, the client must run a rough underwriting to confirm the deal is financeable and the numbers work.

**Step 1 — Normalize SDE**

The client estimates adjusted / normalized SDE by working back from the broker's stated SDE figure:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Reported SDE | $420,000 |
| Owner salary normalization | – $50,000 |
| Questionable add-backs removed | – $30,000 |
| **Adjusted SDE** | **≈ $340,000** |

The client should apply their own judgment to every add-back. Broker-presented SDE figures are promotional.

**Step 2 — Debt Capacity Test**

The client models a typical Canadian financing stack against the asking price:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Purchase price | $1,100,000 |
| Bank + BDC financing | $750,000 |
| Seller note (VTB) | $200,000 |
| Buyer equity | $150,000 |

Debt service coverage test:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Adjusted SDE | $340,000 |
| Annual debt service (estimated) | $180,000 |
| **DSCR** | **≈ 1.9x** |

A DSCR above 1.25x is generally acceptable to lenders. The above example passes.

**This tells the client:**
- Is the deal financeable at the asking price? (Probably yes in the example above)
- What equity is required?
- Is there room for a VTB, and on what terms?
- Is the asking price negotiable — and if so, how much headroom exists?

**If the underwrite does not work at the asking price, the client must either negotiate the price down before LOI or walk away. Do not proceed to LOI on a deal the underwrite does not support.**

**LL's role:** Advise on LOI structure and VTB terms if client reaches out. The underwrite itself is the client's work. LL does not validate or comment on financial projections.

---

### A → B Transition: LOI Decision

Proceed to Phase B (LOI) only when:

1. A1–A4 complete — no disqualifying issues identified
2. A5 underwrite passes — deal is financeable at or near the asking price
3. Client confirms genuine intent to proceed
4. No escalation conditions identified that change the mandate scope

**The LOI is the instrument that moves the deal from the broker's domain to the lawyer's domain. LL takes the lead from Phase B forward.**

---

## Phase B — LOI

### B-1 —Draft LOI

**Mandatory terms (all S-Level 1 deals):**

| Term | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Non-binding statement | Explicitly state which provisions are non-binding (all commercial terms) |
| Purchase price | Total, including any VTB component |
| Deal structure | Asset purchase (or share purchase if confirmed) |
| VTB terms (if applicable) | Principal, interest rate, term, security — headline only in LOI; security instrument in APA |
| Conditions to closing (headline) | Financing, diligence satisfactory, landlord consent, etc. |
| Diligence period | 30–45 days from LOI execution is standard at S-Level 1 |
| Exclusivity period | Match to diligence period; seller cannot negotiate with other parties during exclusivity |
| Binding provisions | Explicitly identify: confidentiality, exclusivity, governing law, costs (each party bears own) |
| Target closing date | Indicative; typically 2–4 weeks after diligence period ends |

**Optional at S-Level 1 (include if fact-pattern warrants):**
- Earnout framework (headline only)
- Management transition / consulting period expectations
- Deposit provisions

### B-2 —Negotiate and Execute LOI

- LOI is typically negotiated through the broker
- Common seller pushback: shorter exclusivity, narrower conditions, higher deposit
- Confirm broker understands lawyer's role — APA will be lawyer-prepared, not broker-prepared
- **Exclusivity commences on execution**

## Phase C — Post-LOI Diligence (Lawyer-Led)

Exclusivity is running. Diligence must be completed within the agreed period. The diligence process shifts from broker-controlled information flow to a structured lawyer-issued request process.

### C-1 —Issue Diligence Request List

Issue within 2–3 business days of LOI execution. Do not wait.

Standard diligence request list at S-Level 1 covers:

**Corporate / Legal**
- Corporate search of seller entity (Ontario/federal)
- All material contracts (in full, executed copies — not broker summaries)
- Lease and any amendments
- All licences and permits
- Any outstanding or threatened litigation
- Any environmental assessments (if flagged)

**Financial**
- Financial statements — 3 years (full, not broker-normalized versions)
- Most recent year-end + interim statements if available
- Tax returns — 3 years (T2 and HST returns)
- Accounts receivable aging
- Accounts payable aging
- Any outstanding CRA correspondence or assessments
- Equipment list with book values and any financing

**Employees**
- Employee list with: name, role, start date, compensation, benefits, FT/PT status
- Any employment agreements (especially non-competes or termination provisions)
- Any pending grievances or claims

**Operations**
- Supplier and key vendor list
- Customer concentration (top 5–10 customers by revenue)
- Any material pending changes (customer departures, supplier issues)

---

### C-2 —Concurrent Searches (Order Immediately)

These run in parallel with the document review and do not need seller cooperation:

| Search | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| PPSA search (all provinces where business operates) | Identify security interests registered against seller's assets |
| Corporate search (seller entity) | Confirm good standing, registered address, officer/director |
| Execution search (seller individuals, if sole proprietor or guarantor) | Identify any judgments against the seller personally |
| Municipal business licence search | Confirm current licence status |

---

### C-3 —Financial Review (Client-Led)

At S-Level 1, financial review is the client's responsibility. The client may engage their own accountant independently; LL does not coordinate or retain the accountant.

**LL's role is limited to:**
- Advising the client that independent financial review of the full, unredacted statements (not the broker-normalized version) is strongly recommended before the client confirms instruction to proceed to APA
- Flagging any financial matters surfaced in the diligence request list response that are legally material (e.g., CRA assessments, outstanding HST, undisclosed financing)
- Incorporating any price or condition implications raised by the client's financial review into the APA

**Key questions LL advises the client to put to their accountant:**
- Does the normalized EBITDA in the CIM hold up against the full financials and tax returns?
- Are there unexplained revenue or expense items?
- What is the working capital position at the reference date?
- Any deferred tax liabilities, undisclosed contingencies, or unusual accounting treatments?

> LL will not express a view on the financial merits of the deal. That is the client's judgment, informed by their accountant.

---

### C-4 —Document Review Sequence

As seller delivers materials, review in this priority order:

**Priority 1 — Lease**
- Remaining term and renewal rights
- Assignment clause — does assignment require landlord consent?
- If yes: consent is a condition to closing (must be in APA and LOI if not already)
- Personal guarantees of the seller — will they be released on closing?
- Any outstanding defaults or disputes with landlord

**Priority 2 — Material Contracts**
- Identify all contracts material to the business
- For each: check assignment clause, change of control clause, expiry date, auto-renewal
- Flag any contracts that cannot be assigned without consent — these become closing conditions
- Note any contracts that will terminate on change of ownership

**Priority 3 — Licences and Permits**
- Inventory all licences: municipal, provincial, sector-specific
- Confirm assignability of each
- Non-assignable licences must be reapplied for by buyer before or at closing — confirm timeline with relevant authority
- Flag any licences with conditions, pending renewals, or recent compliance issues

**Priority 4 — Employee Review**
- Confirm number of employees and their status
- Identify any key persons whose departure would materially affect the business
- Assess ESA obligations on succession:
  - Asset purchase: buyer is not obligated to assume employees, but if employees continue working, ESA deems continuity of service
  - Any employees with contractual termination entitlements above ESA minimums — these survive the acquisition
- Any employment agreements with non-competes binding on employees post-closing?

**Priority 5 — PPSA Results**
- Review PPSA search results as they come in
- Identify all registrations against seller's assets
- Each registration must be discharged on or before closing — confirm with seller counsel
- Blanket charges (e.g., bank debenture) require discharge and PPSA confirmation letter at closing

**Priority 6 — Tax and CRA**
- Review tax returns — any discrepancies with financial statements?
- Any outstanding CRA correspondence, notices of assessment, or audit activity?
- HST: confirm seller is a registrant; confirm s. 167 election applicability
- Payroll: confirm employer health tax compliance, WSIB status

---

### C-5 —Diligence Issues and Client Reporting

As issues are identified, report to client in real time — do not accumulate and report at the end.

**Categorize each issue:**

| Category | Meaning | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Deal-breaker | Issue that, if unresolved, prevents closing | Report immediately; pause further diligence until resolved |
| Price adjustment | Issue that warrants a reduction in purchase price | Note for APA conditions or purchase price adjustment |
| Closing condition | Item that must be resolved before closing but does not affect price | Incorporate as condition in APA |
| Disclosure-only | Disclosed fact with no actionable impact | Record in diligence memo |

---

### C-6 —Diligence Complete Gate

Before moving to APA drafting, confirm:

- [ ] All requested documents received or gaps documented
- [ ] PPSA and corporate searches received and reviewed
- [ ] Client's financial review complete (client-led; LL confirms client has obtained independent review)
- [ ] Lease — assignability confirmed and landlord consent strategy determined
- [ ] All licences inventoried and assignability confirmed
- [ ] Employee obligations quantified
- [ ] All deal-breaker issues resolved or escalated
- [ ] Client has confirmed instruction to proceed to APA on identified terms

---

---

## Phase D — APA Drafting

APA drafts only after the Diligence Complete Gate is passed.

Diligence findings directly inform:
- **Representations and warranties** — carve-outs based on disclosed issues
- **Conditions to closing** — landlord consent, licence transfers, PPSA discharges, financing
- **Indemnities** — survival period (12–18 months standard at S-Level 1), indemnity for undisclosed pre-closing liabilities
- **Purchase price adjustments** — if any price adjustment was warranted by diligence findings
- **VTB instrument** — if VTB is part of the deal, promissory note and security instrument are closing deliverables

APA is negotiated and executed before the closing agenda is issued. All conditions to closing are identified in the APA and tracked against the closing checklist.

---

## Phase E — Closing

### E-1 —Conditions Satisfaction

Track each closing condition from the APA against its current status. No closing proceeds with unsatisfied conditions unless the client has expressly waived a waivable condition in writing.

| Condition (Standard) | Responsible Party | Notes |
|---------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Financing confirmed | Buyer | Bank commitment letter or confirmation |
| Landlord consent to lease assignment | Buyer to obtain | Must be in writing; confirm form acceptable |
| Licence transfers confirmed or reapplications submitted | Buyer | Non-assignable licences — confirm new licence in place or timeline |
| PPSA discharges confirmed | Seller to deliver | All encumbrances on purchased assets discharged |
| Key employee retention (if condition) | Seller | If any key employee is a condition |
| No material adverse change | Seller to certify | Covered by closing certificate |

---

### E-2 —Closing Agenda

Issue closing agenda at least 5 business days before the closing date. The agenda identifies every document to be delivered at or before closing, the party responsible, and the delivery sequence.

**Standard closing deliverables at S-Level 1:**

| Document | Delivered by |
|----------|-------------|
| Executed APA (already signed) | Both parties |
| Bill of sale | Seller |
| Assignment of lease (executed by seller and consented to by landlord) | Seller + Landlord |
| Assignment of material contracts (where required) | Seller |
| PPSA discharge confirmation(s) | Seller counsel / lender |
| Seller's closing certificate (reps true as of closing; conditions satisfied) | Seller |
| Buyer's closing certificate (reps true as of closing) | Buyer |
| Corporate authorizing resolutions — buyer | Buyer |
| Corporate authorizing resolutions — seller (if corporate seller) | Seller |
| HST s. 167 joint election form (signed by both parties) | Both parties |
| VTB promissory note (if applicable) | Buyer |
| VTB security instrument / PPSA registration (if applicable) | Buyer counsel |
| Non-competition / non-solicitation agreement | Seller (and key principals) |
| Transition services agreement (if applicable) | Both parties |
| Funds flow / closing statement | LL |
| Keys, access codes, passwords — physical handover | Seller |

---

### E-3 —Funds Flow

Prepare closing statement confirming:
- Total purchase price
- Adjustments (if any — prorated rent, deposits, inventory)
- VTB holdback (if applicable)
- Net amount payable to seller on closing
- Confirmation of wire or trust account disbursement

At S-Level 1, funds typically flow through LL's trust account. Confirm trust accounting requirements are met before closing.

---

### E-4 —Closing

Closing at S-Level 1 is typically conducted by document exchange (electronic or in person) rather than a formal closing meeting, unless the parties prefer otherwise.

Confirm on closing day:
- [ ] All documents signed and delivered in correct order per closing agenda
- [ ] All conditions confirmed satisfied or waived in writing
- [ ] Funds released from trust only after all conditions confirmed and documents exchanged
- [ ] HST election signed and dated as of the closing date
- [ ] Physical handover of business confirmed (keys, access, inventory)

---

## Phase F — Post-Closing

### F-1 —Post-Closing Checklist

| Item | Responsible | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| PPSA discharges — confirm registrations discharged | LL verifies | Within 5 business days of closing |
| HST s. 167 election — file with CRA | Client / accountant | As soon as practicable after closing |
| Municipal business licence — transfer or new application | Client | Per municipal timeline |
| Sector licences — transfer or reapplication | Client (LL advises on process) | Per regulatory timeline |
| CRA — update business number registrations (payroll, HST) | Client / accountant | Within 30 days of closing |
| WSIB — update account | Client | Within 10 days of closing |
| Lease assignment — confirm landlord has countersigned and copy filed | LL confirms | At or immediately after closing |
| Utilities, insurance — transfer to buyer | Client | On or before closing date |
| Supplier and customer notifications | Client | Per contract requirements or business judgment |

---

### F-2 —VTB Monitoring (If Applicable)

If a VTB is part of the deal:
- Confirm security instrument is registered (PPSA or otherwise) — LL confirms
- Set up payment schedule reminder for client
- Advise client on default provisions and notice requirements under the VTB instrument
- Note in matter file that VTB obligation is outstanding until repaid

---

### F-3 —Closing Binder

Assemble and deliver closing binder to client containing:
- Executed APA and all schedules
- All closing deliverables
- Search results (PPSA, corporate)
- Post-closing checklist (marked complete)
- PPSA discharge confirmations
- HST election copy
- Any licence transfer confirmations

Deliver to client (electronic preferred) within 30 days of closing. File copy retained in SharePoint folder 6 (Post-Closing).

---

### F-4 —Matter Status

On completion of post-closing checklist:
- Update matter to reflect Deal 2 (Additional Service) as delivered
- Confirm billing and collection steps with ML1
- Note any ongoing obligations (VTB monitoring, earnout if applicable)
