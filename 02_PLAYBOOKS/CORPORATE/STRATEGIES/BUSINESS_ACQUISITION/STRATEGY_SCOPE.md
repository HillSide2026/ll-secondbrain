# Strategy Scope: Business Acquisition

## Purpose

This Strategy governs business acquisitions where the total purchase price exceeds $1,000,000. At this threshold, a non-binding LOI is required before proceeding to an APA. The complexity, advisory requirements, and risk profile of these transactions are materially different from sub-$1M acquisitions and warrant a distinct engagement architecture.

Sub-$1M transactions are governed by the BUSINESS_ACQUISITION Tier 2 Solution (Levels 1 and 2).

---

## Engagement Trigger

Both conditions must be present to engage this Strategy:

1. Total purchase price exceeds $1,000,000 (including VTB, earnout, and any deferred consideration)
2. A non-binding LOI is required or appropriate before proceeding to APA

If only one condition is present, assess whether the BUSINESS_ACQUISITION Tier 2 Solution is the correct frame.

---

## Deal Size Levels

| Level | Deal Size | Profile |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| S-Level 1 | $1M – $2.5M | Owner-operator seller; both parties represented; moderate diligence; LOI + lawyer APA |
| S-Level 2 | $2.5M – $5M | Broker-assisted; structured diligence (legal + accountant); working capital adjustment standard |
| S-Level 3 | $5M – $7.5M | Advisory-supported; full diligence team; tax counsel as standard; R&W insurance consideration |
| S-Level 4 | $7.5M – $10M | Full advisory deal; formal data room; comprehensive APA and supplemental agreements; R&W insurance standard |

> S-Level prefix distinguishes Strategy levels from Solution levels (Level 1/2/3).

> Upper bound assumed at $10M. Transactions at or approaching $10M should trigger an assessment of whether specialist M&A co-counsel is appropriate.

---

## In Scope

- Business acquisitions in Ontario with total purchase price $1M–$10M
- Non-binding LOI drafting and negotiation (all S-Levels)
- Lawyer-prepared APA — asset purchase or share purchase
- Legal diligence (all S-Levels, scaled to S-Level)
- Accountant-assisted financial diligence (S-Levels 2–4; S-Level 1 as instructed)
- VTB security instruments (where applicable)
- Earnout agreements or schedules (where applicable)
- Closing and post-closing coordination

---

## Out of Scope

- Real property as primary asset
- Tax structuring advice (refer to tax counsel; mandatory at S-Levels 3–4)
- Financing and securities advice
- Cross-border transactions
- Public company transactions
- Transactions exceeding $10M without reassessment

---

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate immediately when:

- Share purchase structure — assess non-assignable contracts and CRA liability exposure
- Earnout mechanics are disputed or require post-closing conduct covenants
- VTB is present — separate security instrument required; priority and default provisions material
- Seller is in financial distress
- Environmental exposure in the business or premises
- Regulatory licence is the primary asset being acquired
- CRA clearance certificate is required (share purchase)
- Deal at or above S-Level 3 — tax counsel engagement is standard, not optional
- Transaction at or near $10M — assess co-counsel requirement
