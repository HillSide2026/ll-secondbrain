# Risk Surface: Business Acquisition

---

## Default Risk Posture

Elevated relative to the BUSINESS_ACQUISITION Tier 2 Solution. At $1M+, deal failures, undisclosed liabilities, and documentation gaps carry material financial consequences. Diligence cannot be compressed, and escalation discipline is critical.

---

## Strategy-Level Risk Flags

| Risk | S-Level | Why It Matters |
|------|---------|----------------|
| LOI binding provisions misidentified | All | Exclusivity and confidentiality are binding — failure to identify creates enforcement exposure |
| LOI terms reopened in APA without documentation | All | Creates ambiguity about what was agreed; undermines negotiating position |
| Diligence commenced before LOI executed | All | Buyer exposes negotiating position and incurs cost without committed deal framework |
| APA drafted before diligence complete | S-Level 3–4 | Reps and warranties and disclosure schedules drafted on incomplete information |
| Tax counsel not engaged at S-Level 3–4 | S-Level 3–4 | Tax-driven structuring issues missed; deal structure may be suboptimal or defective |
| Share purchase — CRA liability not surfaced | All | CRA assessments follow the shares; buyer assumes undisclosed tax exposure |
| Working capital adjustment — reference balance sheet disputed | S-Level 2–4 | Post-closing disputes if methodology and reference date not precisely defined |
| Earnout — post-closing conduct covenants insufficient | S-Level 2–4 | Buyer can manipulate EBITDA/revenue to reduce earnout; must have covenants limiting interference |
| VTB — no security instrument or defective registration | All | Seller has no enforceable remedy on default |
| Rep & warranty insurance not assessed at S-Level 3–4 | S-Level 3–4 | May affect indemnity negotiation; buyer may demand lower cap if R&W insurance obtained |
| Mandate accepted at S-Level 4 without co-counsel assessment | S-Level 4 | Capacity and complexity risk; assess before accepting |

---

## Failure Surface: LOI Phase

- Non-binding language absent or ambiguous — LOI may be binding in whole
- Exclusivity period not defined — seller free to continue shopping
- Diligence access provisions absent — seller can obstruct
- Break fee absent at S-Level 3–4 — no remedy for aborted deal after full diligence

---

## Failure Surface: Diligence Phase

- PPSA encumbrances on key assets not identified or discharged
- Material contracts not reviewed — undisclosed non-assignment clauses
- Lease not assignable or assignment conditional — key asset at risk
- Employee liabilities not quantified — ESA obligations, vacation pay, potential constructive dismissal
- Tax liabilities (especially share purchase) not surfaced

---

## Failure Surface: APA Phase

- Disclosure schedules incomplete — qualifies reps and warranties, limits indemnity
- Survival period too short — buyer has no remedy after expiry
- Indemnity basket and cap not proportionate to deal size
- No tax and accounting covenants — post-closing filing and cooperation unclear
- Working capital adjustment mechanics ambiguous — post-closing disputes

---

## Failure Surface: Post-Closing

- Business licence not transferred or obtained before operations commence
- CRA registrations not updated — HST, payroll, new account numbers
- PPSA discharges not confirmed — encumbrances persist against assets
- Earnout monitoring not established — disputes arise without tracking mechanism

---

## Escalation Principle

At Strategy level, escalation failures are more consequential than at Solution level. Transaction size means errors are harder to unwind and client exposure is greater.

Escalate early. Document every escalation trigger identified and its disposition.
