Hi Chris,

Good question. You're right to flag it. Corporate registration and business licensing are separate tracks.

Here's what you're looking at:

- **LCBO/AGCO**: Selling lottery products in a retail context requires an AGCO Retail Store Authorization (not a direct LCBO licence — LCBO is the Crown corporation, AGCO is the regulator). This is your primary gaming/lottery retail licence.
- **OLG**: OLG has its own retailer registration process, separate from AGCO. You'll need to register directly with OLG to sell lottery products.
- **Master Business Licence**: Ontario wound down the MBL program — you likely don't need this and it's no longer issued in the traditional form.
- **City of Toronto municipal licence**: Depending on your business type and location, you may need a municipal business licence from the City of Toronto. This is separate from provincial licensing.

So in short: your corporation is the legal vehicle, but you'll need the AGCO, OLG, and municipal licences on top of it to operate fully.
