## Current posture

Laura has produced a useful first draft package, but the founders agreement is not yet ready to send as final client work. It is still dependent on factual confirmations from Nilabh and should be reviewed for transaction architecture before circulation to the founders.

## Draft review issues

1. The document is styled as a "Founders Agreement Term Sheet Summary Table" but reads as a binding shareholders/founders agreement. Decide whether this is intended to be a non-binding term sheet or a binding founders/shareholders agreement.
2. The agreement says it is not a unanimous shareholder agreement. That is likely correct for now, but the next work product should decide whether a separate NUSA follows, or whether this document should be merged into a more complete shareholder agreement.
3. The corporation is described as CBCA, but Laura's email says she will proceed with NUANS and registration of the corporation in Ontario. Confirm whether MRKT Technologies Inc. is already federally incorporated and whether Ontario extra-provincial registration is the intended step.
4. Shareholdings are inserted as Nilabh 44.5%, Justin 39.5%, Gurjevan 16%, with Laura's comment beside Justin's shares to update per MRKT. Treat capitalization as unconfirmed until Nilabh/accountant confirms total issued shares, certificate numbers, and issue dates.
5. The draft includes a special dilution rule where the first 10% post-issuance dilution is borne solely by Nilabh. This is economically material and should be expressly confirmed with Nilabh.
6. Mandatory transfer is triggered by incapacity or competition and uses book value/no goodwill. This is severe; confirm whether the founders actually intend a non-FMV exit.
7. Governance has three directors and majority approval, but no meaningful deadlock mechanism. With three founders this may be acceptable initially, but it should be confirmed.
8. IP assignment is included but should be checked against the factual goal: independent contractor status, no partnership, and assignment of all pre-incorporation and founder-created IP to the corporation.
9. The draft contains some cleanup issues: "Nilabh Anad" typo in Schedule A, "license public accountant", possible duplicated inter-class dilution language, and "relocated" instead of "reallocated" in pre-emptive rights.

## Proposed next action

Do not send the founders agreement out as final. Ask Nilabh for the outstanding factual confirmations, then revise the draft into either:

- a clean non-binding founders term sheet for client/founder review; or
- a binding founders/shareholders agreement if that is the intended deliverable.

The first concrete deliverable should be the founders term sheet / founders agreement architecture review, not the NUSA.
