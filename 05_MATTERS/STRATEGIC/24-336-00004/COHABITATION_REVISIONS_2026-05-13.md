# Cohabitation Revision Notes -- 2026-05-13

## Source materials reviewed

- Alessandra Goulet email dated 2026-05-08: marked-up cohabitation agreement link.
- Alessandra Goulet email dated 2026-05-12: summary of comments on comments.

## Current posture

The agreement is not ready to circulate. Alessandra's review raises substantive fairness, enforceability, tax, disclosure, and mechanism issues. The next work is to revise the agreement into a clearer structure before returning it to Alessandra or sending it for ILA.

## Revision buckets

### 1. Financial disclosure

Add a disclosure schedule and acknowledgement confirming what financial disclosure each party has received and whether either party is waiving further disclosure.

The agreement should not depend on a formula tied to Max's company without giving Amber enough information to assess the economic effect.

### 2. Relationship Support

Separate the current "Relationship Support" concept into clearer sub-parts:

- support or allowance during the relationship;
- post-separation spousal support;
- review/recalculation mechanics;
- dispute process if the parties disagree on the annual amount.

The current hybrid structure creates confusion because it uses support-like language for payments made during the relationship, then converts into post-breakdown support.

### 3. Review and dispute mechanism

Add an annual review process:

- Max provides the relevant company/payment information by a fixed date;
- Amber may obtain accounting advice at her own cost or at shared cost, depending on policy choice;
- disagreement first goes to discussion/mediation;
- unresolved issues go to arbitration or court, depending on chosen dispute path.

### 4. Tax treatment

Revise the tax language so the agreement does not assume CRA will treat during-relationship payments like deductible/taxable spousal support.

Add that the parties should obtain tax advice and that if payments are not deductible to Max or taxable to Amber, the parties should decide whether the amount is grossed up, reduced, or left unchanged.

### 5. Household expenses

Clarify:

- what counts as household expenses;
- what proportion each party pays;
- whether proportions are fixed, income-based, or reviewed annually;
- how Amber's income is treated.

### 6. Termination of support

Rework the termination clause. Alessandra flagged that if the children are grown and the relationship breaks down later, the draft may leave Amber with no support even if she has stronger entitlement at that time.

The revised clause should preserve a support review on relationship breakdown rather than assuming no support once the child-linked period has expired.

### 7. Property regime

The draft excludes equalization and trust claims and divides property by title. That may be intended, but it is a major policy choice given Amber's role as mother of three children and possible long-term marriage/common-law relationship.

Add plain acknowledgement language that each party understands the property consequences and has had independent advice.

### 8. Enhanced settlement / family residence

The enhanced settlement clause needs simplification. If Max transfers his interest in the family residence, the draft should clearly state:

- what exactly is transferred;
- whether the property is mortgage-free;
- what happens if Amber cannot assume or refinance the mortgage;
- whether any valuation credit is actually needed.

## Proposed next action

Prepare a revised clean draft addressing the eight buckets above, then return it to Alessandra for a focused second-pass family-law review before circulation for ILA.
