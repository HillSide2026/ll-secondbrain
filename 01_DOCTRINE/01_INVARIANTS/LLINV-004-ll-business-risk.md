# LLINV-004 — LL Business Risk

**Invariant ID:** LLINV-004
**Status:** APPROVED
**Authority:** ML1

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the canonical categories of business risk that apply to Levine Law as a firm. This invariant defines the risk structure. Thresholds, scoring, and lifecycle governance are defined at the policy layer.

This is distinct from matter-level risk (LLINV-005) and from the firm's project risk model (INV-0013). LL business risk concerns the firm's commercial viability and operating stability.

---

## 2. LL Business Risk Categories

LL business risk is decomposed into five categories. Each is structurally distinct.

### A. Revenue Risk

The risk that firm revenue is insufficient, unstable, or poorly structured to sustain operations and disciplined practice.

Components:
- Revenue volatility and concentration (over-reliance on a small number of clients or matters)
- Absence of recurring or semi-recurring revenue to anchor firm cash flow
- Dependence on single-matter or single-engagement revenue spikes

### B. Realization Risk

The risk that billed work does not convert to collected revenue at acceptable margins.

Components:
- Realization rate deterioration (billed vs collected)
- Receivables aging and collections velocity
- Write-offs and discount frequency

### C. Capacity Risk

The risk that the firm's human capacity (ML1 time and attention) is misallocated, overextended, or not generating economic return.

Components:
- Utilization on non-economic work (uncollectable, pro bono, relationship maintenance with no return)
- Inability to take on high-value work due to capacity occupied by low-value work
- Single-lawyer dependency (no leverage or delegation path)

### D. Scope Risk

The risk that undefined or expanding scope degrades realization, margin, and capacity without commensurate compensation.

Components:
- Scope creep on active matters
- Ambiguous mandates that expand after the retainer is set
- Client expectations that exceed the engagement terms

### E. Professional and Regulatory Risk

The risk that the firm's conduct, advice, or operational practices create professional liability, regulatory exposure, or reputational damage.

Components:
- Professional liability from advice errors or omissions
- Regulatory non-compliance (LSOO obligations, trust accounting, file retention)
- Reputational exposure from engagement conduct or public association

---

## 3. Invariants

1. Every LL business decision with material economic consequence must be assessable against these five categories. No category may be ignored.
2. Revenue Risk and Realization Risk are assessed at the firm level (portfolio ratios). They are not matter-level assessments.
3. Capacity Risk is assessed at the matter and portfolio level. Every engagement accepted has a capacity cost.
4. Scope Risk is assessed at the matter level at intake and monitored during delivery.
5. Professional and Regulatory Risk is assessed at the matter and firm level. It is never optional.
6. Risk assessment does not require a formal scoring exercise. It requires that ML1 has considered the applicable categories before making the decision.
