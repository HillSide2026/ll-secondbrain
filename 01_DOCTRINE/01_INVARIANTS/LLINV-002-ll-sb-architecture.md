# LLINV-002 — LL-SB Architecture: LL Layer

**Invariant ID:** LLINV-002
**Status:** APPROVED
**Authority:** ML1

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the invariant structural rules governing how LL relates to the Second Brain system. This is the LL-scoped view of INV-0009. It defines what LL receives, what it does not receive, and how information flows between LL and the system.

---

## 2. What LL Receives

LL receives:
- ML1-approved artifacts derived from ML2 (templates, playbooks, checklists, matter records)
- Versioned outputs from System orchestration
- Controlled operational inputs through gated workflows

LL does not receive:
- Agent-generated policy
- Unapproved interpretations of doctrine
- Draft or unversioned system artifacts as operative guidance

---

## 3. What LL Does Not Do

LL does not:
- Treat external platforms (Office 365, SharePoint, Clio, Gmail) as canonical repositories
- Treat published copies of artifacts as authoritative over ML2-canonical versions
- Modify canonical artifacts held in ML2
- Operate outside gated workflows without ML1 direction

---

## 4. Information Flow

LL is the origin of operational signals that flow back into the system:

LL delivery + market interaction → System diagnostics → ML1 review → ML2 update (if approved)

LL signals inform but do not determine doctrine updates. The path from signal to doctrine requires ML1 approval.

---

## 5. Canonical vs Published

**Canonical** artifacts are stored in ML2.
**Published** artifacts are distributed copies used by LL in operations.

Published copies are operational; they do not govern. When a published artifact conflicts with the ML2-canonical version, the ML2 version governs.

---

## 6. Invariants

1. LL operates from approved, versioned artifacts. It does not operate from agent-generated guidance.
2. External platforms used by LL are execution surfaces, not canonical repositories.
3. Operational signals generated by LL flow upward for review. They do not automatically update ML2.
4. A published artifact never supersedes its canonical counterpart in ML2.
5. LL does not extend or modify the architecture. It operates within it.
