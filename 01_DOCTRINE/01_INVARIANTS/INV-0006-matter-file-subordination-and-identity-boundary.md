# Matter File Subordination and Identity Boundary Invariant (Binding Invariant)

## Invariant
Matter identity precedes Matter File composition. The Matter File is a governed,
federated envelope subordinate to Matter identity, not a peer or replacement
for it.

## Structural Rules
1. A governed Matter may have one Matter File envelope spanning approved external systems.
2. Matter identity is established by the approved identity authority for the Matter domain, not by external artifacts alone.
3. SharePoint folders, Gmail threads, Clio records, and other approved external artifacts may belong to a Matter File, but none of them by itself may create, redefine, or supersede Matter identity.
4. Matter File composition is subordinate to canonical Matter identity and canonical matter fields.
5. Conflicts, gaps, or ambiguity in external Matter File surfaces do not license identity drift; they require mapping, verification, or escalation.

## Boundary
This invariant defines the constitutional relationship between Matter and Matter
File. Policies and protocols define system-specific mapping, authoritative
source verification, and operational handling.
