# PRN-041 — Transform Uncertainty Into Risk

## Statement

Uncertainty must be converted into named, categorized risk. Vague unknowns
left unaddressed are not managed — they are ignored. The discipline of risk
management begins with the discipline of naming.

## Canonical Distinction

This principle operates alongside `PRN-012` (Risk vs Uncertainty), which
establishes that risk and uncertainty are epistemically distinct:

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Risk | Probabilities are known or estimable; can be modeled |
| Uncertainty | Probabilities cannot be known; requires judgment |

PRN-041 governs what to do with uncertainty once it is identified: **convert
it into a risk entry with a category, a named consequence, and a management
approach** — even where likelihood cannot be precisely quantified.

## Operational Rule

When preparing or reviewing a risk register, risk scan, or business plan:

1. Identify what is currently unknown or unresolved about the business
2. For each uncertainty, ask: *what is the worst plausible consequence if this
   resolves badly?*
3. Name that consequence as a risk entry using the taxonomy in `POL-074`
4. Assign a management approach, even if likelihood is estimated rather than
   calculated
5. Do not leave uncertainties in prose commentary — convert them or flag them
   explicitly as unconverted uncertainties requiring a future risk entry

## Implication

A plan or business model that does not name its uncertainties as risks is not
well-governed — it is optimistic. The purpose of this principle is to force
the act of naming, which is the prerequisite for managing.
