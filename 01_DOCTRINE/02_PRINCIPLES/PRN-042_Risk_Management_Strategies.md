# PRN-042 — Risk Management Strategies

## Statement

Every named risk must be assigned one of five management strategies. Naming a
risk without assigning a strategy is incomplete governance.

## The Five Strategies

| Strategy | Definition | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Avoidance** | Completely eliminate the risk by not engaging in the activity that creates it | When the risk is unacceptable and the activity is not essential to the business |
| **Mitigation** | Implement controls and procedures to decrease the probability or severity of an adverse event | When the activity is essential and the risk can be reduced to an acceptable level |
| **Transference** | Shift the financial burden or responsibility to another entity | When the cost of bearing the risk is better absorbed by a third party (e.g. insurance, indemnification clauses, contractual allocation) |
| **Acceptance** | Acknowledge the risk and knowingly retain it | When the cost of mitigation outweighs the expected loss, or when the risk is inherent and unavoidable |
| **Diversification** | Spread exposure so that no single failure is catastrophic | When concentration in one area amplifies the impact of any single adverse event |

## Operational Rule

When completing a risk register entry under `POL-074`:

1. Name the risk and assign a category (Existential / Strategic / Operational)
2. Assess likelihood and impact
3. Assign one primary management strategy from the five above
4. Define the specific action that implements the strategy

A mitigation is not a strategy on its own — it is the implementation of the
Mitigation strategy. An entry that says "monitor" without assigning a strategy
is not governed.

## Notes on Application

- **Transference** in a professional services context most commonly takes the
  form of insurance (LawPRO), contractual indemnification, or scope-of-work
  letters that limit liability
- **Acceptance** is not negligence — it is a deliberate, documented decision
  that the risk is tolerable; it must be named and recorded, not silently assumed
- **Diversification** applies at the channel level (F01→F02→F03), client level
  (concentration limits), and practice area level (corporate + regulatory)
- A single risk may use more than one strategy (e.g. Mitigation + Transference
  for professional liability)
