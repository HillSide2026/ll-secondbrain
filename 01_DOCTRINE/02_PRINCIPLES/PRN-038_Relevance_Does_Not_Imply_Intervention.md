# PRN-038 — Relevance Does Not Imply Intervention

Title: Relevance Does Not Imply Intervention

Statement:
A matter, signal, or issue may be relevant enough to remain visible to ML1 without requiring ML1 intervention. Relevance governs visibility; intervention requires a separate threshold such as judgment need, approval need, or escalation.

Rationale:
Without this distinction, important but properly delegated work turns into command-layer noise. The system should preserve ML1 awareness where needed while reserving ML1 action for decisions, approvals, and escalations that genuinely require it.
