# LLPRN-021 - Portfolio Selectivity Over Client Count

Title: Portfolio Selectivity Over Client Count

Statement:
Optimize the client portfolio for predictable realization, predictable gross margin, predictable utilization, and predictable collections velocity. Do not optimize for client count, matter count, legal complexity, or prestige.

Rationale:
Most small firms incorrectly treat more clients, more complex work, or more prestigious mandates as signs of progress. Those signals can hide poor economics. A scalable firm behaves like a selective portfolio manager: it chooses work that fits the operating model, prices consistently, collects predictably, and can be delivered without bespoke founder-dependent effort.

The firm should move from episodic, bespoke, emergency-driven work toward standardized service scope, recurring contracts, process-enabled delivery, qualified pipeline management, policy-based pricing, and forecastable collections. The first lawyer should operate like leveraged infrastructure, not like an hourly freelancer.

Application Notes:
1. Rejection right: fixed revenue coverage and pipeline discipline should be used to reject low-fit matters, not to subsidize them.
2. Concentration limit: no single client should become so important that the firm abandons pricing, scope, or collection discipline to preserve the relationship.
3. Prestige warning: legal complexity is valuable only when it supports margin, leverage, positioning, or recurring advisory dependence.
