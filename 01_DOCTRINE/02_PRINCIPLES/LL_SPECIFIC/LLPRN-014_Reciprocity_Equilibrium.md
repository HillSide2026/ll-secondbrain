# LLPRN-014 — Reciprocity Equilibrium

Title: Reciprocity Equilibrium

Statement:
In repeated professional relationships, the long-term value of sustained reciprocity exceeds the short-term gain of defection. Two important risks: (1) last-period problem: when a counterparty knows the relationship is ending, defection becomes rational for them; recognize these situations and adjust expectations; (2) new counterparties: the first interactions are signal-gathering, not full reciprocity.

Rationale:
In game theory, cooperation in a Prisoner's Dilemma is unstable in a one-shot game but sustainable in a repeated (infinite-horizon) game, because both players know that defection today triggers retaliation tomorrow, eliminating all future reciprocity gains. In legal practice this applies to ongoing relationships with opposing counsel, referral sources, and professional counterparties. Short-term extraction (overreaching in negotiation, burning a colleague, taking advantage of an information asymmetry) may produce a one-time gain but destroys the repeated-game surplus. The correct posture in a long-horizon relationship is to treat the reciprocity itself as the asset worth protecting.
