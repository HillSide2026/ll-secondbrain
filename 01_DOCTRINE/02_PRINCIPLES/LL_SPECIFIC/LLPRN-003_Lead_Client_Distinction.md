# LLPRN-003 — Lead-Client Distinction

Title: Lead-Client Distinction

Statement:
A lead is not a client. Pre-conversion contacts — prospects, inquiries, and intake calls — are not clients and must never be treated as such. The pre-conversion interaction is a diagnosis, not a consult.

Rationale:
No retainer, no engagement, no legal authority. A lead may be tracked, contacted, and nurtured through the intake pipeline (LLP-014). The appropriate pre-conversion format is diagnostic: identify the problem type, assess fit, and demonstrate domain command. It is not a legal consultation, abbreviated or otherwise. Emotional analysis, financial analysis, and preliminary non-legal business analysis are appropriate and expected during this stage. Legal analysis, regulatory assessment, and structuring advice are not — not because the conversation is brief, but because the format is diagnostic by design. Permitting substantive legal work to bleed into pre-conversion interactions gives away legal work, creates false obligation, and trains leads to expect free analysis. The diagnosis model prevents this by defining what the pre-conversion interaction is, not just what it must exclude.

Application Notes:
1. The marketing lifecycle ends at conversion (POL-032). Prior to conversion, contacts are in a marketing stage — not a legal engagement.
2. Permitted pre-conversion analysis: emotional (reading the lead, fit assessment), financial (ability to pay, retainer sizing), and non-legal business analysis (understanding their operations, model, or problem space).
3. Not permitted pre-conversion: legal analysis of the lead's situation, characterization of their regulatory exposure, or structuring recommendations for their matter.
4. Lead tracking belongs in LLP-027 (Inquiries) and LLP-028 (Intake Calls). Matter tracking begins only after conversion and matter opening in Clio. "Intake call" describes a pipeline stage, not a legal consultation.
5. A diagnosis identifies the problem type and establishes fit. It does not resolve the problem. The lead should leave the interaction understanding that they have a problem and that this firm can solve it — not with a free analysis of how.
