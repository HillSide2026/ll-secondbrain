# LLPRN-002 - Client Quality Precedes Revenue

Title: Client Quality Precedes Revenue

Statement:
When client quality and short-term revenue conflict, client quality wins. Low-fit, high-friction, or below-ICP work is excluded regardless of short-term revenue need.

Rationale:
Revenue pressure is the primary mechanism through which a firm erodes its client quality standards. A matter that does not fit ICP-01 or ICP-02 consumes capacity that would otherwise be available for matters that do. Over time, accumulating below-ICP work degrades realization, increases friction, and occupies the time the firm needs to build the specialist book.

The ICP filter is the primary lever for improving per-hour economics. It is not a marketing preference — it is a capacity allocation decision. Accepting below-ICP work because of short-term revenue pressure is the same as trading long-term economics for short-term cash. The trade is almost always wrong.

Application Notes:
1. The conflict test: when revenue pressure creates temptation to accept a below-ICP matter, the answer is no unless ML1 makes an explicit exception with documented rationale.
2. ICP definitions: ICP-01 (Ontario operators, CAD 5M+ cash flow, accountant involved, not in acute crisis) and ICP-02 (payments/MSB/fintech operators with Canadian regulatory exposure) are the canonical filters.
3. Distinction from LLPRN-021: LLPRN-021 governs how the portfolio is optimized across dimensions; this principle governs the specific conflict resolution rule when revenue pressure challenges ICP discipline.
