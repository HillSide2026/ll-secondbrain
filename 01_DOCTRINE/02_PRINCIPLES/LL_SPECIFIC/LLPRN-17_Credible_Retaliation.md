# LLPRN-17 - Credible Retaliation

Title: Credible Retaliation

Statement:
To sustain cooperation, have the capacity and stated willingness to punish defectors — and ensure counterparties know it. The deterrence value of retaliation depends entirely on its credibility. A lawyer who retaliates reliably, swiftly, and proportionately trains counterparties not to defect. A lawyer who does not retaliate trains counterparties to exploit.

Rationale:
Trigger strategies (Grim Trigger, Tit-for-Tat) in repeated game theory demonstrate that credible retaliation is the mechanism that sustains cooperation. If the opponent knows that defection triggers a permanent or costly punishment, the expected value of defection becomes negative and cooperation becomes the dominant strategy. In practice this means: follow through on stated consequences, respond to overreaching with proportionate pushback, and never signal that bad behavior will go unremarked. The goal is not punishment for its own sake but calibrated retaliation that makes defection irrational.

Note: Retaliation must be calibrated to remain credible without becoming disproportionate. Disproportionate retaliation destroys the cooperative surplus and escalates unnecessarily. The Tit-for-Tat model (match the defection, then return to cooperation if the opponent cooperates) is generally superior to Grim Trigger (permanent defection after any single defection) because it leaves room for relationship repair.

Counterpart: See LLPRN-14 (Credible Commitment) for how pre-commitment amplifies the deterrence of retaliation.

Application Notes:
1. Proportionality: calibrate retaliation to the defection. Disproportionate retaliation signals irrationality rather than strength and invites escalation rather than deterrence. The goal is restoration of equilibrium, not punishment. Once the defector returns to cooperation, so do you.
2. Audience: retaliation visible only to the defector has limited deterrent value for the broader network. Professionally visible responses — how you handle a matter, what you say when asked, how you respond to a reference check — generate deterrent value beyond the immediate counterparty. The most efficient retaliation teaches the lesson to third-party observers as well.
