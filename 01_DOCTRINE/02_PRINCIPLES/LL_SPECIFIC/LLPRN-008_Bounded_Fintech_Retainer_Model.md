# LLPRN-008 - Bounded Fintech Retainer Model

Title: Bounded Fintech Retainer Model

Statement:
The fintech retainer mandate model is viable only as a bounded strategic legal advisory model. It must not become an uncapped fractional legal department, embedded compliance function, or crisis-response subscription.

Rationale:
The current model has three mandate tiers: CAD 8,000/month, CAD 12,000/month, and CAD 20,000/month. The CAD 8,000 tier is not ready for sale until scope and weekly capacity are defined. The CAD 12,000 tier is the core bounded advisory tier with a 6-hour/week cap. The CAD 20,000 tier uses the same substantive scope envelope as the CAD 12,000 tier but carries a 10-hour/week cap.

The model works because it converts scarce owner capacity into predictable, collectible, recurring revenue. It becomes economically dangerous if the client treats the arrangement as unlimited access, embedded compliance support, regulatory crisis coverage, launch counsel, or ongoing operational management. The current solo-owner model requires at least two active fintech retainers to reduce existential business risk, but cannot safely support more than three active fintech retainers without additional support, deferral, replacement, offboarding, or revised capacity approval.

Application Notes:
1. Economic unit: each mandate must be evaluated by tier, weekly capacity cap, actual usage, mandate mix, and client fit, not price alone.
2. Boundary protection: work exceeding capacity, requiring accelerated timelines, or involving expanded business, regulatory, incident, launch, examination, enforcement, jurisdictional, tax, or securities scope must be separately scoped.
3. Relationship posture: the firm remains external legal counsel. It does not become management, compliance staff, product owner, or a fractional general counsel infinity pool.
4. Concentration management: because each retainer may exceed ordinary client-concentration targets during the early model, renewal cadence, pipeline coverage, cash reserve, and offboarding readiness must be actively managed.
5. 2026 scope lock: until December 31, 2026, the model should remain basic bounded advisory only unless ML1 expressly approves an exception.
