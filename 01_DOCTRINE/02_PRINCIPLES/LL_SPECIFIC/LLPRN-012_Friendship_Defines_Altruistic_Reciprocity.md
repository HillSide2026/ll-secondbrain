# LLPRN-012 - Friendship Defines Altruistic Reciprocity

Title: Friendship Defines Altruistic Reciprocity

Statement:
Only friends get altruistic reciprocity: giving and supporting motivated by genuine care, not by expectation of proportional return and not by strategic calculation. This is distinct from both unconditional giving (no relational basis) and from quid pro quo (transactional). Extending altruistic reciprocity to colleagues or clients does not make those relationships warmer; it corrupts the reciprocity norms that make them function. A warm, professionally reliable good colleague is not thereby a friend. Friendship requires mutual off-the-clock investment, genuine personal disclosure, and behavior in adversity, not just professional warmth.

Rationale:
Altruistic reciprocity is distinct from both unconditional giving and from quid pro quo. Unconditional giving has no relational basis — you give to anyone regardless of relationship. Quid pro quo is transactional — you give because you expect proportional return. Altruistic reciprocity is neither: it is relational and genuine but not calculated. It exists only within a real friendship, where both people care about each other, and that is precisely what makes it appropriate in that category and inappropriate in the colleague or client categories.
