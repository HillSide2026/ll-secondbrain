# LLPRN-023 - Collections Are Economic Discipline

Title: Collections Are Economic Discipline

Statement:
Treat collections policy as a core operating discipline, not as back-office administration. Work that is billed but not collected quickly enough is not economically equivalent to realized revenue.

Rationale:
Weak receivables management converts legal work into working-capital stress. As receivables age, the lawyer becomes exposed to client leverage, delayed cash conversion, and relationship-preserving write-downs. Volatility worsens the problem: when the firm needs revenue, it becomes more likely to tolerate slow payment, continue work without renewed financial commitment, or accept relationship pricing that hides real margin loss.

Healthy collections protect independence. A firm that collects promptly can enforce engagement boundaries, price by policy, stop subsidizing clients, and avoid making desperate intake decisions to fill cash gaps.

Application Notes:
1. Retainer link: this principle extends LLPRN-006. Retainer discipline must continue through scope expansion, replenishment, and renewal, not only at matter opening.
2. Collection velocity: A/R days, collection rate, replenishment compliance, and write-offs are strategic indicators, not accounting trivia.
3. Boundary trigger: slow payment should trigger scope control, work pause, or retainer replenishment review before the firm extends more value.
