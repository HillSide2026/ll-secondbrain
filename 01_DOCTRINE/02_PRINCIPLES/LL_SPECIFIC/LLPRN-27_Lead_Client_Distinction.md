# LLPRN-27 — Lead-Client Distinction

Title: Lead-Client Distinction

Statement:
A lead is not a client. Pre-conversion contacts — prospects, inquiries, and consults — are not clients and must never be treated as such.

Rationale:
No retainer, no engagement, no legal authority. A lead may be tracked, contacted, and nurtured through the intake pipeline (LLP-014). Emotional analysis, financial analysis, and preliminary non-legal business analysis are appropriate and expected during the pre-conversion stage. What does not apply until conversion is legal analysis, regulatory assessment, or legal professional work. Treating a lead as a legal client creates false obligations, distorts matter tracking, and misrepresents the nature of the relationship.

Application Notes:
1. The marketing lifecycle ends at conversion (POL-032). Prior to conversion, contacts are in a marketing stage — not a legal engagement.
2. Permitted pre-conversion analysis: emotional (reading the lead, fit assessment), financial (ability to pay, retainer sizing), and non-legal business analysis (understanding their operations, model, or problem space).
3. Not permitted pre-conversion: legal analysis of the lead's situation, characterization of their regulatory exposure, or structuring recommendations for their matter.
4. Lead tracking belongs in LLP-027 (Inquiries) and LLP-028 (Consults). Matter tracking begins only after conversion and matter opening in Clio.
5. This distinction applies regardless of how substantive the consult conversation was or how well the call went.
