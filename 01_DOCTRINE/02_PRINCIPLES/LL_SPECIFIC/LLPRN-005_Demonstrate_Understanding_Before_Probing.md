# LLPRN-005 - Demonstrate Understanding Before Probing

Title: Demonstrate Understanding Before Probing

Statement:
Do not ask a potential client to articulate their pain before listening to and demonstrating that you already understand the domain, the typical problem patterns, and the stakes involved. Establish command of the space first; invite the client to share specifics second.

Rationale:
Prospects open up most readily when they believe the lawyer already understands the landscape they operate in. Premature probing before demonstrated competence positions the lawyer as a generic questioner, not a domain expert. The client should be confirming specifics, not doing the diagnostic work for the lawyer. Asking a prospect to describe their pain before showing you understand what that pain typically looks like signals that you need them to explain the obvious.

Application Notes:
1. Referral threshold: if you cannot demonstrate understanding of the client's domain, refer rather than probe indefinitely. Probing without demonstrated competence is worse than acknowledging you do not know the space. Competence is a prerequisite for the engagement, not just a sales tool.
2. Intake funnel: demonstrate command of the domain → invite the client to share specifics → funnel to the particular matter. Each stage earns the right to the next. Do not advance to specifics before the first stage is established.
