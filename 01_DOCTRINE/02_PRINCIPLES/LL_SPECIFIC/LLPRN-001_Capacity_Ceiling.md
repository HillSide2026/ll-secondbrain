# LLPRN-001 - Capacity Ceiling

Title: Capacity Ceiling

Statement:
ML1 capacity is the primary constraint governing all firm decisions. Growth that requires exceeding the ML1 capacity ceiling is not authorized until staffing is in place to absorb the load. The ceiling governs; revenue does not.

Rationale:
A single-lawyer firm has a hard throughput limit. When that limit is breached, quality degrades, not volume. Work gets compressed, matters get under-served, and the firm's economic model deteriorates even as revenue appears to grow. The correct response to capacity pressure is to reduce intake, raise the matter quality filter, or add staffing — not to absorb the overload.

The capacity ceiling is the highest-priority constraint in the firm's optimization stack. It precedes client quality, per-client economics, and revenue growth in priority order. A growth decision that violates capacity discipline fails the strategy even if it improves short-term collections.

Application Notes:
1. Staffing precondition: adding a setter, junior associate, or intermediate lawyer is a precondition for volume growth, not a response to it. The decision to staff precedes the decision to scale.
2. Intake discipline: when capacity is constrained, the correct response is higher selectivity on intake, not longer hours.
3. Quality indicator: sustained capacity breach is a signal that the matter mix or intake filter is wrong, not that the ceiling should be raised by effort alone.
