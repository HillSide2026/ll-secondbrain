# LLPRN-022 - Standardized Scope Enables Leverage

Title: Standardized Scope Enables Leverage

Statement:
Prefer practice models with repeatable workflows, ongoing advisory dependence, moderate legal complexity, low litigation volatility, and embedded client relationships. Standardized scope is the foundation for delegation, automation, and higher realization.

Rationale:
Small-firm economics improve when work can be repeated, templated, delegated, and priced with confidence. Excessive customization converts the first lawyer into the bottleneck, weakens realization, and makes delivery dependent on bespoke judgment. By contrast, standardized scope allows repeat process execution, junior-staff leverage, templating, automation, and tighter margin control.

Practice areas with highly episodic, emotionally driven, crisis-driven, contingency-heavy, litigation-only, or structurally bespoke work tend to create lower forecasting accuracy, inconsistent utilization, uneven collections, partner dependency, and realization leakage through over-servicing.

Application Notes:
1. Good recurring work: recurring work is strongest where client dependence is ongoing but the delivery pathway is sufficiently standardized to protect margin.
2. Bad recurring wrapper: do not mistake repeated chaos for recurring revenue. A client who repeatedly generates emergencies may create recurrence without stability.
3. Delivery design: every recurring or repeatable service should be reduced into scope boundaries, standard artifacts, intake gates, delegation points, and write-down controls.
