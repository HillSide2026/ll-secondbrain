# LLPRN-019 - Client-Level Earnings Variability

Title: Client-Level Earnings Variability

Statement:
Analyze clients by contribution quality, not only by revenue potential. The first lawyer's effective realization rate is impaired when client-level earnings vary because of poor qualification, inconsistent intake, excessive customization, or weak receivables management.

Rationale:
The practical unit of small-firm economics is the client, not the abstract matter. Some clients generate healthy revenue because they are qualified, scoped, priced, standardized, and collectible. Others consume attention, create ambiguity, resist boundaries, delay payment, and convert billings into weak realized earnings. If the firm does not distinguish these client types before engagement, it imports variability directly into realization, margin, and workload.

Client-level earnings variability explains why nominal revenue can look adequate while the firm still feels economically fragile. A client who pays late, requires bespoke handling, resists scope boundaries, or triggers write-downs may create less real firm value than a smaller client on a clearer recurring arrangement.

Application Notes:
1. Qualification before conversion: do not treat a signed client as a good client until fit, scope, pricing, and payment behavior are qualified.
2. Intake consistency: inconsistent intake converts unknown client risk into delivery risk. Intake must identify complexity, urgency, scope ambiguity, payment risk, and fit before the firm commits.
3. Earnings lens: evaluate client quality by realized contribution after discounts, write-offs, unpaid time, receivable aging, and management burden.
