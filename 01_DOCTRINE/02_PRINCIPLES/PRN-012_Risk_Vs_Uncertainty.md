# PRN-012 - Risk vs Uncertainty

Title: Risk vs Uncertainty

Statement:
Decision quality requires explicit separation between risk and uncertainty.

Canonical distinction:

| Concept | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Risk | Probabilities are known or can be calculated | Insurance, actuarial tables |
| Uncertainty | Probabilities cannot be known or calculated | Starting a new business |

Implications:
- Use probability-based controls only where probability can be estimated.
- Treat uncertainty domains as judgment-heavy and scenario-based.
