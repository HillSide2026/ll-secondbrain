# POL-074 — Risk Taxonomy

## Purpose

This policy defines the universal risk taxonomy applicable to all business projects
within the LL Portfolio and HillSide Portfolio. It governs the categories and
sub-categories used in all risk registers and risk scans.

**The taxonomy is universal. Specific risk assessment — likelihood, impact, and
mitigation — is conducted on a per-business basis.**

---

## Category Structure

Risk is classified into three top-level categories:

| Category | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Existential** | Risks that could end the business entirely or cause unrecoverable harm |
| **Strategic** | Risks that materially damage the business model, revenue trajectory, or competitive position — survivable but consequential |
| **Operational** | Risks that affect execution quality, process reliability, or day-to-day operations |

---

## Existential

| Sub-category | Sub-sub-categories |
|---|---|
| Financial | Insolvency; inability to meet fixed obligations; forced wind-down |

---

## Strategic

| Sub-category | Sub-sub-categories |
|---|---|
| Founder / Key Person | Permanent incapacity; death; voluntary exit without succession |
| License & Regulatory | Loss of professional license (e.g. LSO disbarment); regulatory shutdown order |
| Liability | Catastrophic claim exceeding insurance coverage; any claim |
| Reputational | Criminal investigation or allegation; public scandal; fraud allegation |
| Revenue & Financial | Revenue concentration (client); channel dependency; revenue shortfall vs. plan; margin compression; pricing power erosion; cash flow gap |
| Market | Competitive encroachment; market contraction or demand shift; regulatory environment change (demand-affecting); positioning failure; market saturation; oversupply of lawyers; no enforcement of rules leading to reduced demand for legal compliance |
| Client & Acquisition | Client acquisition cost; client acquisition underperformance; conversion rate failure; client quality drift; client concentration |
| People & Capacity | Key person dependency (non-existential); staffing model failure; capacity ceiling without leverage path; associate underperformance |

---

## Operational

Operational risks are organized under three sub-categories: **Scope**, **Schedule**,
and **Budget**.

| Sub-category | Sub-sub-category | Sub-sub-sub-categories |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Delivery | Quality control failure; scope creep; system or template failure; supervision failure; matter scoping errors |
| Scope | Financial Operations | Collections and AR aging; payment terms enforcement failure; disbursement management; revenue shortfall vis-à-vis budget |
| Scope | Compliance | Professional liability / malpractice; regulatory compliance (professional obligations); conflict of interest; out-of-scope delivery |
| Schedule | | Timeline slippage; milestone failure; channel build delay; dependency failure |
| Budget | | Out-of-budget expense; cost overrun |

---

## Usage

When preparing a risk register or risk scan for any business project:

1. Classify each risk using the category and sub-category from this taxonomy
2. Use the format: `Category — Sub-category` (e.g. `Strategic — Market`, `Operational — Scope / Delivery`)
3. Assess likelihood, impact, and mitigation specific to the business
4. Do not modify this taxonomy at the project level — propose amendments to POL-074 directly

**Cross-reference:** All risk registers and risk scans under `04_INITIATIVES/` should
reference this policy.
