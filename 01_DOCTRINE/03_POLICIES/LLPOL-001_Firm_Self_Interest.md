# LLPOL-001: Firm Self-Interest

Policy Statement: Levine Law makes business decisions based on the firm's economic interests. Client goodwill, relationship preservation, and professional courtesy do not override firm economics without ML1-approved justification.

Authority (Principles referenced): LLPRN-018 (Revenue Volatility), LLPRN-021 (Portfolio Selectivity), LLPRN-024 (KPI-Governed Scaling)

Related policies: LLPOL-002 (Purse First), LLPOL-003 (New Client Retainer Floor)

---

## 1. Policy Purpose

Levine Law is a commercial law firm, not a charitable or reputational enterprise. The firm's sustainability depends on economic discipline. This policy establishes self-interest as the default operating orientation.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All client intake and engagement decisions
- All pricing, retainer, and fee-setting decisions
- All scope, capacity, and referral decisions
- Any decision where firm economics and client preferences conflict

---

## 3. Rule

The firm acts in its own economic interest. This means:

| Decision type | Default |
|---|---|
| Engagement acceptance | Accept when economics are sound; decline or refer when they are not |
| Pricing | Price to market value and firm capacity; do not discount to preserve goodwill |
| Scope creep | Enforce scope; do not absorb overruns to avoid a difficult conversation |
| Collections | Collect; do not write off to preserve relationships |
| Referrals | Refer work that does not meet economics; do not carry it |

---

## 4. Limits

Self-interest does not mean:
- Conduct that violates professional obligations (LSOO rules of professional conduct apply regardless)
- Conduct that is commercially shortsighted (reputation, referral networks, and long-term relationships have economic value)
- Acting against ML1's considered judgment in a specific case

Below-economics exceptions are allowed only with ML1-approved justification documented at the matter level.
