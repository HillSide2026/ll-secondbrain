# LLPOL-010: Client-Friend Exclusion Policy

Policy Statement: A client cannot be a friend. A friend cannot be a client. No legal engagement may be entered with a friend. No client relationship may be permitted to become a friendship during the engagement.

Authority (Principles referenced): LLPRN-004 (Client-Friend Exclusion)

Related policies: LLPOL-001 (Firm Self-Interest), LLPOL-002 (Purse First)

---

## 1. Policy Purpose

Converts the structural principle into an enforceable operational rule covering intake, active engagement, the friend-referral scenario, and the temporal boundary after matter close.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies at intake, throughout active engagements, and for a reasonable period after matter close.

---

## 3. Rule

| Scenario | Rule |
|---|---|
| Friend requests legal engagement | Decline. Refer to suitable counsel. Do not engage. |
| Active client relationship develops into friendship | Maintain the professional boundary. If genuine friendship is confirmed, the commercial relationship must close before the personal relationship can begin. |
| Friend refers a third party as a client | The referred party is a client. The friend's relationship does not transfer. Apply standard intake: LLPRO-001, LLPOL-002, LLPOL-003. |
| Former client (matter closed) | The exclusion applies during the engagement and for a reasonable period after. Test: the professional relationship has fully ended and both parties have recognized the shift. ML1 judgment call. |

---

## 4. Limits

The exclusion is bidirectional and does not require the other party to be aware of it. It is ML1's obligation to maintain, not a mutual agreement.

This policy does not apply permanently to former clients. The temporal boundary is ML1's judgment call based on whether the professional relationship has genuinely closed.
