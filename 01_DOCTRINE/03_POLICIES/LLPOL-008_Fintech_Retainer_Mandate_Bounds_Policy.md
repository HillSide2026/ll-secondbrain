# LLPOL-008: Fintech Retainer Mandate Bounds Policy

Policy Statement: Fintech retainer mandates operate under defined tier structures, weekly capacity caps, and a firm-wide mandate count ceiling. The model must not become uncapped access, embedded compliance, or crisis coverage.

Authority (Principles referenced): LLPRN-008 (Bounded Fintech Retainer Model)

Related policies: LLPOL-001 (Firm Self-Interest), LLPOL-002 (Purse First), LLPRN-001 (Capacity Ceiling)

---

## 1. Policy Purpose

Converts the bounded fintech retainer principle into operational limits enforceable at intake and renewal. Protects ML1 capacity and firm economics from mandate creep.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all fintech retainer mandates: new sales, renewals, and scope discussions with existing retainer clients.

---

## 3. Tier Structure

| Tier | Monthly fee | Weekly capacity cap | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | CAD $8,000 | To be defined | Not available for sale until scope and weekly capacity are defined |
| Tier 2 | CAD $12,000 | 6 hours/week | Core bounded advisory tier |
| Tier 3 | CAD $20,000 | 10 hours/week | Same substantive scope as Tier 2; higher capacity |

---

## 4. Mandate Count

| Active retainer count | Status |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 2 | Below minimum; existential concentration risk — pipeline required |
| 2–3 | Operating range |
| More than 3 | Not permitted without additional support, deferral, replacement, offboarding, or ML1-approved capacity revision |

---

## 5. Out-of-Scope Work

Work that exceeds the weekly capacity cap, requires accelerated timelines, or falls outside bounded advisory scope must be separately scoped and separately retainered. The retainer does not cover:

- Embedded compliance function
- Regulatory crisis response
- Launch counsel
- Ongoing operational management
- Fractional general counsel arrangements

---

## 6. 2026 Scope Lock

Until December 31, 2026, all active retainers are limited to basic bounded advisory scope. Expanded scope requires explicit ML1 approval with documented rationale.

---

## 7. Concentration Management

Because each retainer may exceed standard client-concentration targets during the early model phase, renewal cadence, pipeline coverage, cash reserve, and offboarding readiness must be actively managed. No single retainer client may cause the firm to relax pricing, scope, or collections discipline to preserve the relationship.
