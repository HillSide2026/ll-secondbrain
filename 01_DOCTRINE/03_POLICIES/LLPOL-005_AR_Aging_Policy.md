# LLPOL-005: A/R Aging Policy

Policy Statement: Receivables are governed by defined aging thresholds. Each threshold triggers a mandatory response. Write-offs require ML1 approval.

Authority (Principles referenced): LLPRN-023 (Collections Are Economic Discipline), LLPRN-024 (KPI-Governed Scaling)

Related policies: LLPOL-001 (Firm Self-Interest), LLPRO-003 (A/R Review Protocol)

---

## 1. Policy Purpose

Converts the collections principle into a rule with specific triggers. Prevents receivables from aging by default and ensures ML1 visibility before write-offs occur.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all open receivables across active and closed matters.

---

## 3. Rule

| Age past invoice date | Required action |
|---|---|
| 30+ days | Payment reminder issued to client |
| 45–60 days | ML1 direct client contact; collectability assessed |
| 60+ days | ML1 decision required: demand letter, payment arrangement, or write-off |
| Write-off (any age) | ML1 approval required; rationale documented at the matter level |

---

## 4. Write-Off Requirements

Write-offs may not be applied without:
1. ML1 approval
2. Rationale documented at the matter level
3. Prior escalation attempt (except where collectability is confirmed as zero)

---

## 5. Payment Arrangements

Payment arrangements require ML1 approval and documented terms at the matter level. An arrangement does not suspend the aging clock; it replaces it with the arrangement schedule.

---

## 6. Targets

From LLPRN-024: A/R days under 45 firm-wide average; write-offs under 5% of billings. Compliance with this policy is the mechanism for achieving those targets.
