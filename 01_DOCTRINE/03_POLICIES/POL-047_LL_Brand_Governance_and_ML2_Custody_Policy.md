# POL-047 — LL Brand Governance and ML2 Custody Policy

Policy Statement: LL brand governance is ML2 doctrine. Brand artifacts and usage controls must be stored in ML2, and LL-distributed outputs must be template-derived from approved bases or explicitly ML1-approved.

Authority (Principles referenced): PRN-030, PRN-031, PRN-032, PRN-033, PRN-034  
Enforcement expectation: Any distributed output without approved-template derivation or explicit ML1 approval is non-compliant.  
Supersedes: None

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines ML2 governance responsibility for LL brand doctrine and establishes required custody and authorization boundaries for branded output.

---

## 2. ML2 Brand Doctrine Responsibility

Brand policy is system doctrine and must be governed inside ML2.

ML2 is responsible for storing and maintaining governed brand artifacts, including:
- logo files
- color codes
- font specifications
- Canva templates
- example documents
- template usage rules

---

## 3. Output Authorization Rule

Any output distributed by LL must satisfy one of the following:
- derived from an approved template
- explicitly approved by ML1

If neither condition is met, the output remains non-authorized and must not be distributed.

---

## 4. Relationship to Existing Doctrine

This policy operates with:
- `POL-014` (Doctrine Residency Requirement)
- `POL-015` (Artifacts Residency Requirement)
- `POL-046` (Canva Template Enforcement Policy)
- `POL-004` (External Output Approval Requirement)

---

## 5. Summary

ML2 is the custody and governance layer for LL brand doctrine and brand artifacts.  
Distribution is authority-gated: approved-template derivation or explicit ML1 approval is mandatory.
